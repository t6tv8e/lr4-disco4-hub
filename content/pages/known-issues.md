## Suspension

### Air Suspension Pump Failure

- Symptoms from source: vehicle would not raise because the pump was not generating enough pressure
- Diagnosis note: confirmed with a GAP2 diagnostic tool
- Fix performed: pump replacement
- Repair difficulty: straightforward on a hoist, with one awkward mounting bolt
- Preventive note: not stated in the source

### Front Air Shock Leak

- Symptoms from source: leak was only visible when the suspension was at full height
- Fix performed: both front air shocks were replaced
- Repair difficulty: reconnecting the air line was awkward because of limited spare hose length
- Inspection note: if you are hunting an air leak, full-height inspection may reveal leaks that are hidden at normal ride height

## Engine, Intake, And Lubrication

### Cracked Throttle Body

- Symptoms from source: crack was hidden underneath the unit
- Diagnosis note: smoke testing revealed the failure
- Fix performed: replacement throttle body
- Cost from source: AUD $1,200 for a genuine Land Rover part, about AUD $300 for an aftermarket alternative
- Buying note: the owner believes the aftermarket part would probably have been adequate

### Oil Cooler Failure

- Symptoms from source: not described
- Fix performed: oil cooler replacement
- Repair difficulty: access is poor because the cooler sits in the V of the engine
- Practical note: the owner could not follow the factory removal method because a bracket blocked removal, so the bracket was cut to get the cooler out
- Planning note: this job overlaps with inlet manifold access, so related intake work may be worth evaluating at the same time

### Cracked Left Inlet Manifold

- Symptoms from source: not described
- Timing note: found shortly after the oil cooler repair
- Fix performed: manifold replacement
- Repair difficulty: hidden bolt behind the timing cover on the left side
- Time from source: about 10 hours
- Planning note: this would have been easier to combine with the oil cooler job

## Electrical

### Alternator Failure

- Symptoms from source: charge error remained even after replacing the battery
- Fix performed: alternator replacement
- Repair difficulty: alternator is mounted low in the engine bay and the power cable is too short to make the job comfortable
- Preventive note: not stated in the source

## HVAC

### Intermittent A/C

- Symptoms from source: intermittent air conditioning
- Suspected cause: faulty solenoid valve
- Repair status: replacement part already sourced
- Planned approach: drain and refill with HyChill 30 after repair
- Confidence note: the source frames this as a suspected diagnosis, not a confirmed one

## Body, Water Ingress, And Exterior

### Cracked Rear High-Mount Stop Light

- Symptoms from source: heavy rain caused interior flooding
- Suspected fault path: cracked high-mount stop light
- Repair status: replacement already sourced
- Repair note: the unit is glued in and the owner plans to reseal it with Sikaflex 258 primeless windscreen adhesive

### Clear Coat Failure

- Areas affected: bonnet, roof, rear door handle
- Repair expectation from source: sanding back and repainting will likely be required

## Interior, Trim, And Access

### Sagging Headlining

- Current condition: headlining is sagging
- Temporary measure: fabric pigtail pins
- Permanent repair: not decided yet

### Rear Door Locks Intermittent

- Scope: both rear door locks
- Repair status: parts already sourced
- Additional note: owner intended to film this repair, which suggests it may be a repeatable DIY task worth documenting later

### Front Seat Stitching Failure

- Scope: both front seats need stitching repair
- Practical note: removal should be simpler on non-electric seats

## Chassis And Ride

### Clunky Suspension

- Symptoms from source: suspension feels clunky
- Suspected causes: worn bushes and sway bar components
- Status: inspection pending
- Confidence note: this is still an unconfirmed diagnosis

## Preventive Maintenance Takeaways

- The owner changes oil every 5,000 km and never stretches beyond 7,000 km
- Filters and fluids are handled as DIY work to control quality and cost
- Frequent oil changes are used as a mitigation strategy against crankshaft failure concerns on the V6 diesel
- This source does not prove that shorter oil intervals prevent crank failure, but it does show how at least one owner manages that risk

## Ownership Cost Context

- Purchase price in the case study: about AUD $35,000
- Estimated current value in the case study: about AUD $17,000
- Depreciation over 4.5 years: about AUD $18,000
- The source argues that, at this value level, dealer labour makes many major repairs economically hard to justify
