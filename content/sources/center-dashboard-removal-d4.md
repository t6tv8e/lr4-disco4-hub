## Summary

This video shows how to remove the centre dashboard side panels and trim on a Discovery 4, following on from a lower console removal video. The process covers removing the trim panel below the screen, the silver and grey fascia panels, the side cheek panels, and also demonstrates how to remove the start/stop switch. The presenters note that all screws in this job are the same size (T20 Torx), making it straightforward.

The video is relatively short and serves as a middle step in a series -- the lower console has already been removed, and a follow-up video covers removing the screen and head unit. A useful bonus tip is included: the start/stop switch can be replaced by just levering it out with a trim tool, without needing to remove any other dashboard components.

## Key Points

- Start by removing the small trim panel beneath the screen -- held by two fierce clips, just pull it straight out
- All screws in this job are T20 Torx and are all the same size (approximately 10 screws total)
- Remove two T20 screws to release the fascia panels
- The silver trim and grey trim may come off together or separately -- try the silver first
- The start/stop switch can be popped out independently with a trim tool (bonus tip for replacing a worn switch without full disassembly)
- Raise the steering wheel to its highest position before removing the side cheek panel for clearance
- The side cheek panel has 4 spring clips around its edges
- Remove the start/stop button before pulling the side cheek panel to avoid snagging wires
- The opposite side cheek is simpler -- twist the bottom out; this is where the air vent cushion pieces go
- Manual steering column adjustment makes this easier; electric adjust owners may need to look up the procedure

## Parts Mentioned

- T20 Torx screws (approximately 10, all the same size)
- Start/stop switch (can be replaced independently)
- Trim panel clips x2 (below screen)
- Side cheek panel spring clips x4
- Trim removal tool
