## Summary

This video walks through a complete engine oil and filter change on the Land Rover Discovery 4 with the 3.0 TDV6/SDV6 engine. The presenter demonstrates removing the sump plug (which is a plastic hose-type fitting rather than a traditional sump plug), draining the oil, and then locating and replacing the oil filter canister buried in the V of the engine.

The oil filter housing requires a 32mm socket to remove, and the video covers removing the old filter element, cleaning the housing, fitting the new o-ring and filter, and refilling with oil. The presenter notes that the D4 TDV6 does not have a traditional dipstick, so oil level must be checked via the onboard computer system. Approximate oil capacity is cited as 5.9 litres, though they advise checking the specific capacity for your vehicle.

## Key Points

- Sump plug is a plastic hose fitting, not a traditional metal sump plug -- uses a 13mm socket
- Oil comes out fast and messy -- use a large washing-up bowl to catch it
- Oil filter is located in the middle of the V of the engine, inside a plastic canister
- The filter canister cap requires a 32mm socket to remove
- Pay attention to the o-ring location -- it sits in a groove at the bottom of the canister
- Replace the o-ring with the new one supplied; apply a bit of oil around it before refitting
- The filter element fits snugly either way up inside the housing cage
- There is no dipstick on the 3.0 TDV6 -- oil level is checked electronically via the dashboard
- Oil capacity is approximately 5.9 litres -- verify for your specific vehicle
- Do not refit the rocker cover until you have started the engine and confirmed no oil leaks around the filter canister
- A new sump plug with o-ring is recommended each time

## Parts Mentioned

- Brit Part oil filter (compatible with TDV6/SDV6)
- New sump plug with o-ring
- New canister o-ring (included with filter kit)
- Approximately 5.9 litres engine oil (check spec for correct grade)
