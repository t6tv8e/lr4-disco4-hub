## Summary

This video covers the complete removal and rebuild of a seized tow bar hitch assembly on a Range Rover Sport (same procedure for Discovery 3/4). The presenters use a specialist "Land Raider" extraction tool to remove a seized hitch from the rear chassis receiver. The video then moves into a full strip-down on the bench, cleaning, painting, greasing, and step-by-step reassembly of the entire tow bar mechanism including the lock pin, ball bearing, spring, rack-and-pinion worm drive, and green locking cap.

This is a detailed, hands-on rebuild video that shows the internal workings of the removable tow hitch mechanism. The Land Raider tool is essential for extracting seized hitches where the standard green handle release no longer works.

## Key Points

- The green cap on the tow hitch has a pull-out and rotate-down release, but it seizes with age and corrosion
- To remove the green cap: drift out the roll pin from underneath using a punch and hammer
- Underneath the green cap are two 3mm Allen bolts holding the red disc/cog
- Behind the red disc is a spring; be careful when removing as the ball bearing can ping out
- The Land Raider extraction tool is 22mm and made from hardened steel; it engages in the hitch socket and uses a socket wrench to push the seized pin out
- You may need to hammer the hitch back and forth to free it after the tool has extracted the main pin
- The shaft/pin that seizes is the main cause of failure -- it needs thorough cleaning, painting, and greasing
- During reassembly, the cog segment must be aligned with a triangular segment stop inside the housing to ensure correct rotation range
- Use a hose clip trick to hold the ball bearing and spring compressed during reassembly
- The red cog's bolt holes are not at 180 degrees -- they sit slightly below the centre line
- The green cap has a flat that must align with the flat on the shaft -- it only goes on one way
- The roll pin goes in from the top during reassembly
