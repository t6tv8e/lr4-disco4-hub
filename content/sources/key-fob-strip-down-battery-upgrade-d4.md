## Summary

This video demonstrates a full strip-down of a Discovery 4 key fob and shows how to transplant the circuit board from the original early-style key case into a newer, sleeker Evoque-style key case. The presenters were responding to a viewer (Victoria and Adam) who had been quoted around 770 GBP by a dealer for a replacement key fob. The video covers battery replacement, hidden key blade removal, complete disassembly of the key fob case, and reassembly into the new case.

The circuit board from the early D4 key does fit the later Evoque-style case, but one corner of the board needs to be filed down slightly to clear a peripheral channel inside the new case. The presenters used a needle file for about 5 minutes to remove the excess material. After reassembly with a new CR2032 battery, the transplanted key worked for both remote locking and keyless start, though the button feel was slightly shallower in the new case.

## Key Points

- The early D4 key fob circuit board is compatible with the later Evoque-style key case
- One corner of the circuit board must be filed down to fit the new case's peripheral channel -- check for circuitry before filing (the corner area is clear)
- The new case has foam pads that press on the circuit board; use the half with the foam pads to test-fit before final assembly
- Battery is CR2032; positive side goes up (towards the spring marked with positive)
- The hidden mechanical key blade is released by pressing a small button on the side of the fob near the textured area
- Getting the key blade back in can be tricky -- press down on the small ramp to get it to click over
- The battery tray has a groove that only goes in one way, aligned with an upstand in the housing
- There is a risk of static damage to electronics (ESD) when handling the exposed circuit board -- wrist straps are recommended but weren't used in the video
- The two halves of the key case should snap together without needing super glue
- Early and late lock barrels are physically the same part but priced differently; changing to the later barrel is cheaper and matches the later key style
- Buttons may feel slightly shallower in the new case as the circuit board sits fractionally lower
