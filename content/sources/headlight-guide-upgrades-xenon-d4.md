## Summary

A comprehensive "mythbusters"-style exploration of Discovery 4 headlights covering the differences between halogen and xenon units, how to identify which type you have, upgrading from halogen to xenon, bulb replacement, the beam pattern deflection switch for continental driving, and the LED side light elements. The presenters also attempt to open a headlight by oven-heating it (as they previously did with a Discovery 3) but find the D4 units use a silicone-sealed U-channel design that makes them very difficult to separate.

The key finding is that while xenon headlights physically plug straight into a halogen-equipped car (same 16-pin connector), the dipped beam will cut out because the CCF (Car Configuration File) detects the higher current draw. The xenons initially pull 9 amps versus 3.8 amps for halogens, and the car's electronics shut down the circuit. Main beam and side lights work fine. The presenters note that a dealer or specialist tool is normally needed to reprogram the CCF, but they plan to find a DIY workaround.

## Key Points

- Halogen dipped beam bulbs are H7; the main beam bulbs are also H7 — both clip in the same way
- Do not touch the glass of halogen bulbs with bare fingers (oils cause premature failure)
- To identify xenon vs halogen without removing: xenon units have a ballast/driver pack on the back; halogen units have an empty space where the driver would be
- The LED elements in the headlight are wired to the side light circuit, not as daytime running lights
- Xenon headlights have visible yellow LED elements inside; halogen units look clear inside
- The 16-pin connector is identical between halogen and xenon headlights — they are a direct physical swap
- Xenon dipped beam draws ~9 amps initially vs ~3.8 amps for halogen; the CCF detects this and cuts power
- Main beam, indicators, side lights, and LEDs all work without CCF changes when swapping to xenon units
- To fully enable xenon dipped beam, the CCF must be reprogrammed (requires dealer or diagnostic tool)
- The left-hand/right-hand drive beam deflection switch inside the headlight exists but has minimal effect on the D4 — it slightly modifies the beam but does not flatten the kick-up sufficiently for continental use
- Discovery 4 headlights are silicone-sealed and extremely difficult to open (unlike Discovery 3 units), making internal modification impractical for DIY

## Parts Mentioned
