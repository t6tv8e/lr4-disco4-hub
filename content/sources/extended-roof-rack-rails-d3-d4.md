## Summary

This video provides a detailed step-by-step guide to fitting the extended (full-length) roof rack rails on a Discovery 3 or 4. The Discovery 3 and 4 share the same roof gutter system, so the rails are interchangeable. There are two types available: a short rack that clamps into the rear section only, and the extended rack that runs all the way to the front. This video covers the extended type.

The process involves removing the existing roof channel trim (held by fir-tree fasteners), cutting it to length at a specific measurement (1520mm from a reference line), drilling a 19mm clearance hole in the front section for the rail foot, and then bolting the rail down using four threaded inserts already present in the roof. The rear three bolts require spacers with keep-on washers; the front bolt does not. The whole job takes about 20 minutes per side.

## Key Points

- Discovery 3 and Discovery 4 have the same roof gutter system -- the extended rails fit both models
- Two types of roof rack rails exist: short (rear section only) and extended (full length to front)
- Remove the existing roof channel trim by extracting four fir-tree fasteners using a snake-tongue trim removal tool
- Spread the load with a second tool when pushing out the centre of the fir-tree fasteners
- The channel must be cut to length: measure 1520mm from the reference line on the channel (not the very end -- there is a 10mm offset at the rubber end)
- Cut with a hacksaw; the cut section is covered by the rail foot so it does not need to be perfectly neat
- Drill a 19mm hole in the front section of the cut channel using a 19mm blade drill on a piece of wood with cutting oil
- Clean up the drilled hole with a round rat-tail file, mainly removing rubber coating remnants
- Four threaded inserts are pre-installed in the roof (revealed by removing the filler strip)
- Rear three bolts require spacers with keep-on washers; the front bolt has no spacer
- The rail foot has a raised plastic section on the bottom that must sit down through the channel cover into the mount
- Drop bolts through from the top, engage a few threads on each before tightening -- don't overtighten early or the last bolt won't align
- Push the rubber channel all the way back before final tightening to prevent overlap at the front join
- The cosmetic filler plate at the front goes in from the back with a twist-and-slide motion
- Check all internal bolts are tight before fitting the blanking caps
- Approximately 20 minutes per side

## Parts Mentioned

- Extended roof rack rail kit (includes bolts, spacers, keep-on washers, blanking plates, cosmetic filler plate)
- 19mm blade drill (included or recommended with kit)
- Fir-tree fasteners (existing, may need replacement)
- Snake-tongue trim removal tool
- Hacksaw (for cutting channel)
- Rat-tail file (for cleaning drilled hole)
- Allen key (for rail bolts)
- Cutting oil

## Notes

- A separate video covers fitting the cross bars to the installed rails
- The short rack type is simpler to fit as it does not require channel cutting or drilling
- The presenters note these are sometimes marketed as "Discovery 4 racks" but they fit Discovery 3 equally well
