## Summary

This video demonstrates how to install the removable tow hitch (tow ball) on a Land Rover Discovery 3/4 or Range Rover Sport. These vehicles come with a tow bar already built into the rear chassis -- you only need to purchase the removable tow hitch assembly itself. The video covers the locking mechanism, insertion procedure, and common difficulties encountered when fitting the hitch into the chassis receiver.

The presenters also discuss an important safety recall related to tow bar vibration causing chassis wear, and show the difference between the standard removable hitch and a heavy-duty version designed to be left installed permanently.

## Key Points

- Discovery 3/4 and Range Rover Sport share the same tow bar setup built into the rear chassis -- no separate tow bar purchase/installation needed
- UK/European spec uses a standard ball hitch; US spec uses a square receiver for American-style hitches
- The removable bumper panel uses quarter-turn fasteners -- when aligned with the direction of travel, they pop out (can be turned with a coin)
- A plastic dust insert sits in the chassis receiver to prevent dirt build-up; remove it before inserting the hitch
- Clean the receiver cavity before insertion -- compacted dirt prevents the lock pin from engaging properly
- The lock pin mechanism: inserting the hitch activates a pin that fires out to lock it in place; pull the key out and twist to engage/disengage
- When fully inserted, the red band on the hitch should be barely visible / fully disappeared
- Do NOT leave the removable hitch installed when not towing -- vibration in the chassis caused a Land Rover recall for chassis wear
- Contact your local Land Rover dealer to check if your vehicle has had the recall; they will replace the rear chassis section for free if worn
- Do not lubricate the receiver cavity with oil-based products (per the instructions)

## Parts Mentioned

- Removable tow hitch / tow ball assembly (UK/European spec shown)
- US-spec square receiver hitch (mentioned as alternative)
- Heavy-duty permanent tow hitch with extra bracing -- designed to be left installed full-time, eliminates rattling
- Dust cover / plastic insert for chassis receiver
- Quarter-turn bumper panel fasteners
- Replacement lock and keys (available; covered in a separate rebuild video)
