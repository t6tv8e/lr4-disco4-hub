This note captures a single owner's repair history and pending jobs on a 2010 Discovery 4 / LR4 3.0L V6 diesel over roughly four and a half years. It is useful as field evidence for failure modes, repair difficulty, and ownership economics, but it should not be treated as population-level reliability data.

## Vehicle Snapshot

- Spec: 2010 Discovery 4 / LR4, 3.0L V6 diesel, SE trim, Australia
- Configuration notes: 7 seats, rear A/C, cloth interior, no electric seats
- Aftermarket change: Carpuride W901 Android Auto / Apple CarPlay unit on a custom flip-up bracket
- Purchase price: about AUD $35,000
- Estimated current value: about AUD $17,000
- Depreciation over 4.5 years: about AUD $18,000

## Confirmed Repairs In This Case Study

### Suspension

- Air suspension pump failure
  - Symptom: pump could not generate enough pressure to raise the vehicle
  - Diagnosis note: confirmed with a GAP2 diagnostic tool
  - Repair note: straightforward on a hoist apart from one awkward bolt
  - Cost: not stated

- Front air shock leak
  - Symptom: left front airbag leak only visible at full suspension height
  - Repair note: both front units were replaced
  - Difficulty note: reconnecting the spare hose length was awkward
  - Cost: not stated

### Electrical

- Alternator failure
  - Symptom: charge error remained even after fitting a new battery
  - Repair note: alternator sits low in the engine bay and the power cable is short and awkward to work with
  - Cost: not stated

### Engine, Intake, and Lubrication

- Cracked throttle body
  - Symptom: crack was hidden underneath the unit
  - Diagnosis note: found during a smoke test
  - Cost note: genuine replacement quoted at AUD $1,200; aftermarket equivalent around AUD $300
  - Owner view: aftermarket would probably have been sufficient

- Oil cooler failure
  - Location note: oil cooler sits deep in the V of the engine
  - Repair note: the factory method proved impractical because of a blocking bracket
  - Workaround used: bracket was cut off to allow removal
  - Context note: owner considers the bracket a Discovery 3 carryover with little value on this vehicle
  - Cost: not stated

- Cracked left inlet manifold
  - Timing note: found shortly after the oil cooler repair
  - Repair planning note: owner says it would have been easier to do during the oil cooler job
  - Difficulty note: hidden bolt behind the timing cover on the left side
  - Time: about 10 hours
  - Cost: not stated

## Current Issues And Planned Fixes

### HVAC

- Intermittent air conditioning
  - Suspected cause: faulty solenoid valve
  - Status: replacement part already sourced
  - Planned repair note: owner intends to drain and refill with HyChill 30

### Body And Water Ingress

- Cracked rear high-mount stop light
  - Symptom: heavy rain led to interior flooding
  - Status: replacement sourced from AliExpress
  - Repair note: light is glued in and will be resealed with Sikaflex 258 primeless windscreen adhesive

### Interior And Trim

- Sagging headlining
  - Temporary measure: fabric pigtail pins
  - Final repair: undecided

- Front seat stitching failure
  - Scope: both front seats
  - Repair note: removal is simpler because the seats are not electric

### Locks And Access

- Rear door lock faults
  - Scope: both rear door locks are intermittent
  - Status: parts already sourced

### Paint And Exterior Finish

- Clear coat failure
  - Areas affected: bonnet, roof, rear door handle
  - Expected repair: sanding back and repainting

### Chassis And Ride

- Clunky suspension
  - Suspected cause: worn bushes and sway bar components
  - Status: inspection pending

## Maintenance And Prevention Notes From The Owner

- Engine oil changed every 5,000 km and never beyond 7,000 km
- Filters and fluids are handled as DIY work to control cost and quality
- Frequent oil changes are used as the owner's mitigation strategy against the known crankshaft failure concern on the V6 diesel

## Ownership Economics

- At an estimated value of about AUD $17,000, the owner considers dealer repair costs economically hard to justify
- The case study frames DIY work as the only practical way to keep the vehicle on the road once values fall below about AUD $20,000
