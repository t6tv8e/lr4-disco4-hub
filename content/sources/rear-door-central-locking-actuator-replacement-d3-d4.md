## Summary

This is an updated method for replacing the rear door central locking actuator on a Discovery 3/4. The presenters previously covered this topic but have developed a better technique that involves removing the outer door handle rather than working blind inside the door to swap cables. While removing more parts, they argue this approach is actually easier and produces better results.

The full sequence covered is: remove the door card, drop the window, remove the speaker (4x T20 Torx screws), disengage the window from the regulator mechanism (rear window has only one clip vs two on the front), remove the window regulator assembly, remove the exterior door handle and its cover trim, unbolt the lock mechanism (3x T30 Torx), extract the whole assembly, then disconnect the cables from the old actuator and transfer them to the new one. A clever extraction technique is shown for the window regulator -- it must be slid forward so its top corner passes behind the white window catch, then wiggled out.

## Key Points

- This is an updated/improved method compared to their earlier video on the same topic
- The rear window has only one retaining clip (vs two on the front)
- Tape the window up before disconnecting it from the regulator to prevent it dropping
- Speaker removal: 4x T20 Torx screws, plus disconnect the bottom connector; may need a wedge to pry off double-sided tape
- Window regulator extraction trick: slide it forward so the top corner passes behind the white window catch, then wiggle out
- Thread the regulator cable back through the grommet into the door frame before working on the handle
- Exterior door handle cover has spring clips (coloured orange for the video) -- use a screwdriver to compress them while pulling
- Three T30 Torx countersunk bolts secure the door catch mechanism -- remove these to allow better access to the handle cover clips
- The D-shaped orange slug on the outside has a Torx screw accessed from inside the door through a hole
- Don't forget the electrical connector before extracting the mechanism
- Cable disconnection: two prongs fired into a metal plate, pull clips up and rotate cable to align with the slot
- Estimated job time: about 1 hour including a tea break
- Reassembly is described as easier than disassembly

## Parts Mentioned

- Central locking actuator (sold without cables or door handle -- must transfer from old unit)
- Speaker screws: T20 Torx x4
- Lock mechanism bolts: T30 Torx x3 (short countersunk)
- Door handle cover clips (available from PowerfulUK website)
- Door handle gasket panels (reusable, located on pegs)
- Three purchasing options discussed:
  - Genuine new (expensive, but best quality)
  - Aftermarket new (cheaper, but variable longevity)
  - Secondhand OEM (genuine quality but already has wear)
