## Summary

This video demonstrates how to fit an aftermarket manual release mechanism for the upper tailgate on a Discovery 3/4. The product, made by Land Rover Passion (LRP), is a cast aluminum lever that provides a manual backup to electrically release the upper tailgate from inside the vehicle. The presenter explains the common problem: when the electric actuator fails, the upper tailgate cannot be opened, and the repair is inaccessible because the trim panel is held in place by a plastic strip that can only be removed when the tailgate is open -- a catch-22 situation.

The installation involves removing the lower tailgate interior trim panel (four Phillips screws under caps, plus clips), disconnecting the gas strut stays from their ball joints, removing 13mm bolts for the struts, removing the subwoofer (10mm flanged nyloc nuts), cutting the subwoofer mounting bracket with a grinder, drilling holes through the trim panel using a template, cutting a hole in the carpet for the release lever, routing the cable to the actuator mechanism, and reassembling everything. The presenter notes that cutting into the sealed subwoofer chamber may slightly affect bass response.

## Key Points

- The upper tailgate release mechanism is located inside the lower tailgate, making it inaccessible when the upper tailgate is stuck closed
- Before assuming actuator failure: try the button on the tailgate, try the key fob (press and hold tailgate release), and try with the engine running (raises voltage from 12V to 14V giving the solenoid more power)
- On Discovery 3 you can use a lock/unlock combo to open the tailgate; this does not work on Discovery 4
- The LRP manual release kit includes a cast aluminum pull lever, mounting plate, screws, cable ties, and a horseshoe-shaped bracket
- Remove the four Phillips screws (hidden under caps) on the lower tailgate trim panel
- Gas strut stays are removed by levering the spring clip with a flat-blade screwdriver to pop them off the ball joints -- tie the cables with an overhand knot to prevent them disappearing inside the tailgate
- Use foam padding between the dropped tailgate and the car body to protect paintwork
- Strut mounting balls use 13mm socket; subwoofer nuts are 10mm flanged nylocs
- Subwoofer bracket screws are Torx T30; actuator cover screws are Torx T15
- The template's flat edge aligns with a line in the carpet; the round edge goes near the curve at the top
- Drill 8mm holes through the trim panel for mounting
- The cable clamp uses a central post with one wire on each side, a clamp plate, and a nut
- An end-stop clip must be positioned against a hard point inside the tailgate so the pull mechanism has something to react against
- Cable tie placement near the actuator connector can interfere with the latch -- be careful with routing
- Cutting into the sealed trim panel compromises the subwoofer's acoustic chamber, potentially reducing bass quality
- After reassembly, verify: central locking, key fob release, button release, and manual pull release all work
