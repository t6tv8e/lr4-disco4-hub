## Summary

This video tests whether the new Land Rover Defender L663 18-inch steel wheels can be fitted onto a Discovery 4. The test is prompted by a viewer (Colin, who runs an off-road events business) who wanted to know about compatibility, particularly with the larger brake setup. The presenter confirms that the Defender 18-inch steel wheels do fit on the Discovery 4 on both front and rear axles, with clearance on both the standard 325mm brakes and the larger 360mm diameter discs.

The key to the fitment is the internal profile of the Defender steel wheel -- where the inner flange is welded, it comes back out to a flat profile across the back of the wheel, providing just enough clearance over the brake caliper. While the clearance is tight (approximately 10mm on the larger brakes), it is sufficient. The presenter also checks rear fitment for offset/tyre clearance against the upright and confirms adequate space. The video explains why 18-inch wheels are desirable for off-roading: larger sidewall tyres provide better protection for the wheels and better off-road grip, and steel wheels bend rather than crack on impact.

## Key Points

- Defender L663 18-inch steel wheels fit on the Discovery 4 -- confirmed on both front and rear
- Compatible with both brake sizes: 325mm (standard) and 360mm (larger/upgraded) diameter discs
- Brake caliper clearance is approximately 10mm on the larger 360mm brakes -- tight but functional
- The internal wheel profile is key: the welded inner flange section comes back out flat, providing the clearance needed over the caliper
- Someone mentioned a TUV specification for minimum wheel-to-caliper clearance -- 10mm may not meet that spec; owners should verify for their jurisdiction
- Stud pattern, centre bore, and diameter all match
- Rear fitment checked: the upright has plenty of clearance for the tyre
- The larger D4 brakes (360mm) use the same caliper as the standard setup but with a different carrier that spaces it out for the bigger disc
- Previously, the only known 18-inch wheel fitting the D4 with larger brakes was made by Compomotive
- Benefits of 18-inch wheels for off-roading: fatter tyres absorb impacts, protect wheels, and provide better grip on rocks/mud/trails
- Steel wheels bend on impact rather than cracking like alloys -- more forgiving off-road
- This means Defender and Discovery 4 owners can share/swap wheel sets for off-road use
