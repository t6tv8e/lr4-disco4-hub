## Summary

A short video showing how to fit die-cast aluminium paddle shift extensions to a Discovery 4. This is the final step in a multi-part paddle shift upgrade series — previous videos covered fitting the physical paddle shift mechanisms to the steering wheel and changing the CCF files to enable the functionality. The extensions are a cosmetic/ergonomic upgrade that stick onto the existing paddles using adhesive, making them larger and easier to reach.

## Key Points

- The kit includes two aluminium extensions: one marked negative (downshift) and one marked positive (upshift)
- Made from die-cast aluminium; they grip the original paddles quite snugly even before adhesive is applied
- Clean the back surface of the existing paddle with the included alcohol wipe to remove grease before fitting
- Line up the top/inside edge first, then press into place
- The extensions are designed not to cover the illuminated plus/minus signs on the original paddles
- Same extensions also fit the Range Rover Evoque, Range Rover Sport, and L322 Range Rover with paddle shifts

## Parts Mentioned
