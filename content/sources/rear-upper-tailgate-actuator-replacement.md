## Summary

This video provides a step-by-step guide to replacing the upper tailgate actuator on a Discovery 3/4. The presenter describes it as a relatively easy DIY job that does not require paying a professional. The process involves removing the lower tailgate trim panel, disconnecting the gas strut stays (using a thin 13mm spanner rather than unclipping them -- a viewer tip to prevent the cables from disappearing inside the tailgate), removing the carpet panel, accessing the actuator behind the dust shield, disconnecting the electrical connector and cable, and swapping in a new unit.

The video covers a useful detail about how the cable end connects to the actuator: there is an L-shaped cable end held under a spring-loaded slug mechanism that must be pushed up to release the pin. The presenter demonstrates using a bit of wood to tap the metal retaining piece down to free the cable. When fitting the new actuator, they recommend adding a cable tie around the cable clamp as a precaution, since the plastic clip is known to fail. The video also covers replacing the optional water splash shield (adhesive dust cover) and testing the two-stage tailgate release mechanism.

## Key Points

- Part number for the actuator: FUG 510 (genuine Land Rover)
- Tools needed: Phillips screwdriver, trim tool, thin 13mm spanner, 8mm socket, Torx T10, small flat-blade screwdriver, cable tie
- Four Phillips screws (under orange/plastic caps) hold the lower trim panel
- Tip: use a thin 13mm spanner to remove the gas strut stay bolts instead of unclipping the stays -- this prevents them from disappearing inside the tailgate
- If you do unclip the stays, tie a knot in the cable immediately to stop it vanishing
- Use foam padding to protect paintwork when the tailgate rests without limit stops
- The actuator is held by two bolts (8mm) on slotted holes -- loosen and slide to remove
- The electrical connector has a push tab on the bottom (feels like it's on top when reaching in)
- Actuator cover screws are Torx T10
- The cable end has an L-shape held by a spring mechanism with two plastic arches -- push the slug upward to release the pin
- Add a cable tie around the new actuator's cable clamp as insurance against clip failure
- The tailgate release is a two-stage mechanism: first press unlocks, second press (via button on tailgate) fully opens
- Use a screwdriver to emulate the locking rod position when testing before full reassembly
- A rubber adhesive removal wheel helps clean off the old dust shield adhesive
- The car in the video is 16 years old and still on its original actuator
- Best to replace the actuator proactively before it fails completely, as accessing it with the tailgate closed is extremely difficult

## Parts Mentioned

- Genuine Land Rover actuator: FUG 510 (slightly more expensive than aftermarket copies)
- Water splash shield (dust cover): approximately £15 from Land Rover -- optional, mainly for professional finish
