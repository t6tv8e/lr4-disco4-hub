## Summary

This video shows the fitting of a rear ladder to a Land Rover Discovery 3/4. The ladder consists of a top clip that hooks under the roof edge near the brake light, a bottom support plate, and the ladder itself with fixing bolts. The presenters install the top clip first, adding a self-drilling screw for extra security beyond the hook-only design, then fit the bottom bracket with coach bolts and stainless steel nyloc nuts.

The installation is done top-down. The top clip has a foam padding on the contact surface and should be positioned as close to the brake light as possible without covering it. Silicone sealant is applied around the screw hole for waterproofing, though any water that did enter would only run down the outside gutter channel. The bottom bracket uses plates that keep the ladder off the tailgate door surface. The presenters note the ladder is more style than function, and that the fitting is identical for both Discovery 3 and Discovery 4.

## Key Points

- Install top-down: fit the top clip first, then the bottom bracket
- Top clip hooks under the roof edge; position it close to the brake light without covering it
- An additional self-drilling screw is recommended through the top clip for security (drills its own hole, no pre-drilling needed)
- Apply silicone sealant around the screw hole and on the clip to prevent vibration and sliding
- Any water ingress would only run down the external gutter behind the weather seal
- Bottom bracket uses coach bolts (head prevents rotation) with stainless steel nyloc nuts and penny washers
- The ladder sits on the bottom plates, not directly against the tailgate door
- Centralize and square the ladder before final tightening
- The tailgate will be heavier with the ladder fitted; weak gas struts may need replacing
- Apply white grease to tailgate hinges while you are working in the area
- The fitting is identical for Discovery 3 and Discovery 4
