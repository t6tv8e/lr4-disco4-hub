## Summary

A short, straightforward video demonstrating how to remove and refit the rear number plate lamp units on a Land Rover Discovery 3 and 4. The lamps are located in a housing above the rear number plate, with two units in total. Over time, the housings get dirty, and the spring clips that hold them in place can lose tension or snap, especially with repeated bulb changes.

The removal is a simple two-minute job requiring only a flat-blade screwdriver. You locate the small notch/gap on one side of the lamp housing, insert the screwdriver to lever and pop it out, pull down the spring side, then disconnect the electrical connector by pushing in the release peg. Refitting is the reverse: connect the plug, insert the spring side first, then push the other side up and lever it home with the screwdriver.

## Key Points

- The lamp housings are above the rear number plate; there are two units
- Look for the small gap/notch on the periphery of the housing -- this is the only prying point
- Insert a flat-blade screwdriver into the gap to lever the unit out
- Pull the spring side down to release
- The electrical connector has a small peg that must be pushed to disconnect
- When refitting, snap the connector on first, then insert the spring side, push up, and lever closed
- The spring clips lose tension over time and can snap with repeated use
- The whole job takes about two minutes
