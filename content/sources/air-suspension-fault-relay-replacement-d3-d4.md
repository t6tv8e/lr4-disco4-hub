## Summary

This video covers diagnosing an air suspension fault on a Land Rover Discovery 3 (also applicable to the Discovery 4). The vehicle displays a "Suspension fault - Normal height only" error message after the compressor fails to reach pressure within the allowed time window. The presenter first attempts the cheapest possible fix -- replacing the air suspension compressor relay -- before moving on to consider compressor replacement.

The relay (position R7 in the engine bay fuse box next to the battery) is a simple plug-in component that can only be inserted one way. Unfortunately, in this case, replacing the relay did not resolve the fault, indicating the compressor itself had failed. The video notes the compressor is located behind a plastic cover at the rear of the vehicle near the rear wheel. The presenter acknowledges this is primarily a diagnostic/relay replacement guide and that dedicated compressor replacement videos exist elsewhere.

## Key Points

- Symptom: "Suspension fault - Normal height only" warning message on the dashboard, with the suspension inactive indicator light
- The system works on a timer -- the compressor runs and if it cannot reach the required pressure within a certain time, the fault is triggered
- You can hear the compressor running when it is attempting to build pressure
- The air suspension compressor relay is at position R7 in the engine bay fuse box (next to the battery, under the battery cover)
- The relay has a small pull-tab on top -- pliers can help remove it
- The relay can only be inserted one way due to pin configuration
- The original relay had "Ford" written on it; the new genuine Land Rover replacement looked slightly different but had identical pin layout
- Replacing the relay is inexpensive and worth doing as good practice, even if it does not fix the problem
- Some aftermarket compressor kits include a new relay
- In this case, the relay swap did not fix the fault -- the compressor itself needed replacement
- The compressor is located behind a plastic cover at the rear of the vehicle, near the rear wheel
- Other dashboard warnings (HDC system fault, engine fault) may also appear alongside the suspension fault

## Parts Mentioned

- Air suspension compressor relay (position R7, inexpensive)
- Genuine Land Rover replacement relay (new part looks different from original Ford-branded one but is functionally identical)
- Air suspension compressor (needed when relay replacement does not resolve the fault)
- Located behind plastic cover at rear of vehicle near rear wheel
