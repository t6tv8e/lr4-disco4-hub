## Summary

This video demonstrates how to remove the front grille and headlamps on a Discovery 4 as the first step in a pre-facelift to facelift conversion project. The grille removal is straightforward -- spring-loaded tabs along the top and ball-end clips along the bottom. The headlamp removal involves sliding two retaining tabs and disconnecting a 16-way electrical connector from each headlamp.

The presenters work through the process live without prior practice, which helpfully reveals the real-world difficulties. One headlamp came out easily while the other was very tight, requiring wiggling and pushing the headlamp in before pulling it out. They note that the ball-end grille clips can deteriorate after repeated removal and are worth replacing, and that they sell replacement clips on their website.

## Key Points

- **Grille removal:**
  - Push-down spring tabs along the top edge (they have a ramp that clips in)
  - Ball-end pull clips along the lower part -- they dock into receptacles on the body
  - The lower clips can come out with the grille or stay in the body -- they deteriorate over time and are worth replacing
  - Replacement clip inserts available from PowerfulUK website

- **Headlamp removal:**
  - Two sliding retaining tabs per headlamp -- they have a round bit that needs to be lined up to slide out
  - Each headlamp has a 16-way electrical connector with a push-button release on top
  - Press the release button and pull the connector out
  - If the headlamp is tight, push it in first and wiggle it in and out to free it
  - One side may be tighter than the other, especially on vehicles that have been used off-road
  - The tabs may be at different heights on each side, particularly if one headlamp has been removed before

## Parts Mentioned

- Grille spring tabs (top edge)
- Grille ball-end clips (lower edge) -- available as replacements from PowerfulUK
- 16-way headlamp electrical connector (one per side)
- Headlamp retaining slide tabs (two per side)
