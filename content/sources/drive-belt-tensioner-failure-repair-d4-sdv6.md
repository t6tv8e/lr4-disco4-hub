## Summary

This video documents the diagnosis and repair of a failed drive belt tensioner on a Discovery 4 SDV6 3.0L with approximately 200,000 miles. The vehicle was making a terrible noise from the front of the engine. Upon inspection, the idler bearing on the tensioner was completely destroyed -- worn flat and rubbing against the engine block, creating red dust everywhere and causing the belt to overhang its pulleys.

The repair involves removing the engine cover, air intake piping, the viscous fan (which requires a specialist tool and has a reverse/left-hand thread), and the fan cowling to access the tensioner. The tensioner is held by a single 15mm bolt and locates via two tabs. The video also covers removing and rerouting the serpentine belt using a 3/8" drive on the tensioner to release tension, and reassembly. The presenters note differences between the 3.0 TDV6 and 2.7 TDV6 belt setups.

## Key Points

- Symptoms: loud noise from front of engine, red dust around pulleys, belt overhanging pulleys
- The tensioner bearing was completely destroyed -- worn flat and had been rubbing against the block
- The viscous fan uses a reverse thread (clockwise to loosen) -- requires a specialist fan removal tool
- Three bolts hold the fan to the hub; the fan spanner holds these while you turn the hub nut
- Remove the fan cowling (has a hidden pin clip, a long bolt marked in orange, and cable connections) to gain access
- The belt tensioner uses a 3/8" drive square hole to release tension
- Slip the belt off a smooth (non-ribbed) pulley when removing
- Tensioner is held by a single 15mm bolt plus two locating tabs
- Belt spec for 3.0 TDV6: 6PK 2102 (6 ribs, 2102mm length)
- Belt spec for 2.7 TDV6: 8PK 2092 (8 ribs, 2092mm length) -- wider belt, slightly different length
- The 3.0L tensioner pulley is thinner than the 2.7L version
- Belt routing diagram is available online -- over the top pulley, under, around, over, around tensioner, around alternator
- Ideally a two-person job due to effort needed on the tensioner
- This vehicle was bought cheap at 200,000 miles (~300,000 km) and the tensioner failure may have been a pre-existing issue
