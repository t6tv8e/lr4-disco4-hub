## Summary

A step-by-step guide to removing the front door card (interior trim panel) on a Discovery 4. This is presented as part one of a three-part series covering door card removal, window glass removal, and central locking actuator replacement. The video covers all screw locations, clip types, and the order of electrical disconnections needed to fully remove the door card.

The presenters highlight that door card removal is needed for many common jobs: central locking actuator replacement, broken cable repair, window glass removal, speaker upgrades, and wing mirror replacement. They walk through each fastener location methodically, noting that most screws are the same size (cross-head) except for two long ones at the bottom of the door card. After the screws are out, the card pulls straight out with clips along the top and edges. Several electrical connectors must then be disconnected in sequence before the card is fully free.

## Key Points

- Screw locations (all cross-head unless noted):
  - 1 screw behind the small plug at the door edge
  - 1 screw behind the door handle blank (push bottom corner in to flip it out)
  - 4 screws hidden behind the two silver grab handle embellishers (2 per side)
  - 2 long screws at the bottom of the door card (distinctly longer, can't be mixed up)
- Total: 8 standard screws + 2 long screws = 10 fasteners
- Silver grab handle embellishers: have a double metal spring clip and locating pins; lever off with a trim tool -- they fight you a bit
- An extending magnetic pickup tool is essential for retrieving screws from cavities
- The door card pulls straight out towards you -- pull from a corner rather than the middle for better leverage
- Be careful not to pull too hard as cables are still attached

### Electrical disconnections (in order):
1. Green connector: press button and extract
2. Grey connector: press release tab underneath
3. White plastic collar: swing out, lift up, and remove the metal slug
4. Control panel: two connectors at bottom, press the spring-loaded lug on the side
5. Electric window switch: press tab on underside to release
6. Speaker connector: just pulls apart (two-piece in the middle)

- Door cable reattachment tip: the cable sits in a white clip that pops out; when reassembling, insert the slug first, route the wire, then clip the white piece back in
- The door mirror screws and connector are accessible once the door card is removed

## Parts Mentioned

- Cross-head screws x8 (standard length, all the same)
- Long cross-head screws x2 (bottom of door card)
- Extending magnetic pickup tool (highly recommended)
- Trim removal tool / lever tool
- Door card clips (along top edge and sides -- slot-type at top, push-fit around edges)
