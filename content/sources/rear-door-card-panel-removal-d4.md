## Summary

A walkthrough of removing the rear door card (interior trim panel) on a Discovery 4. This procedure is needed for central locking actuator replacement, speaker replacement, and window regulator work. The rear door card is simpler than the front, with fewer screws: one behind a pop-out cover near the door lock, four behind the grab handle chrome covers, and two longer screws at the bottom corners.

The video covers each fastener location, the technique for removing the chrome grab handle covers without breaking them, and the careful sequence for disconnecting the electrical connectors once the panel is pulled free. A detailed off-car demonstration shows how the door release cable and its white clip mechanism work, making reassembly much clearer.

## Key Points

- Screw locations (all Phillips cross-head):
  - 1 screw behind a pop-out circular cover near the door lock area (push one end to flip the other side out)
  - 4 screws behind the two chrome grab handle embellishers (2 per side)
  - 2 longer screws at the bottom corners of the door card
- Total: 7 screws (5 standard + 2 long)
- Chrome grab handle covers pull straight off towards you -- use a trim tool to lever; they are tough and unlikely to break
- The door card pulls straight off (directly out, not up) -- clips along the top also release straight out
- Start pulling from one corner rather than the middle
- Pull hard enough to release clips but stop immediately -- wires and a cable for the door release are still connected

### Electrical disconnections:
1. Door release cable: push the white section square (not at an angle) to extract it, then rotate to clear the slot and remove the metal slug
2. Grey connector: press the tab underneath to release
3. Controller module: press the tab on top to pull the connector out
4. Electric window switch: blue connector with tab underneath
5. Speaker wire: just pulls apart

- Interior courtesy light may fall off or need disconnecting when the card is pulled
- A magnetic extractor tool is useful for retrieving screws from cavities
- Reassembly tip for the door cable: thread the slug into the slot first, then click the white clip back by aligning the groove

## Parts Mentioned

- Phillips cross-head screws x5 (standard length)
- Phillips cross-head screws x2 (longer, bottom corners)
- Door card clips (top edge and around perimeter)
- Chrome grab handle embellishers with double-sided metal spring clips
- Magnetic extractor tool (recommended)

## Notes

- The rear door card has fewer screws than the front door card (7 vs 10)
- The rear card pulls straight off; it does not slide up like some vehicles
