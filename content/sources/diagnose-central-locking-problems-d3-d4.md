## Summary

This video walks through diagnosing central locking issues on a Discovery 4 project car, where the rear doors are not responding to the key fob lock/unlock commands. The presenter starts with basic checks -- verifying which doors work and which don't -- then moves through a logical diagnostic process: checking fuse 39 (a 25-amp white/brown fuse in the passenger footwell fuse box), trying a forum-suggested manual lock/unlock reset trick, and eventually removing the rear door card to visually inspect the locking actuator mechanism.

After removing the door card (three screws plus two hidden screws behind silver caps on the grab handle), they keep the main connector plugged in so the central locking system still recognises the door. They then observe the actuator cable while triggering the key fob -- the cable barely moves, confirming the actuator lacks sufficient force ("Mojo") to operate the lock. The diagnosis concludes that the door lock actuators need replacing, which is covered in a separate follow-up video.

## Key Points

- Check fuse 39 first -- it's a 25-amp white/brown fuse located in the passenger footwell fuse box (lower panel must be removed)
- Pulling and reinserting the fuse can act as a soft reset for the central locking system
- A forum trick involves holding the interior lock button while pulling the door handle, but results were inconsistent
- The childproof lock switch on the rear door edge (horizontal = child lock on, vertical = unlocked) does not affect central locking
- When removing the door card, there are 3 visible screws plus 2 hidden screws behind silver caps on the grab handle
- The silver caps hinge out from the top -- lever them from the bottom
- Keep the main door connector plugged in during diagnosis, otherwise central locking and windows won't function
- With the door card off, you can observe the actuator cable: if it barely moves when the key fob is pressed, the actuator needs replacing
- Open all windows before messing with central locking to avoid locking yourself out
- This is described as a common problem on Discovery 3/4 models

## Parts Mentioned

- Fuse 39: 25-amp (white/brown colour) for central locking
- Door lock actuator (replacement needed when weak/failed)
- Fuse removal tool (located at top of fuse box)
