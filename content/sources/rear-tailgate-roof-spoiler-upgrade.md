## Summary

This video demonstrates how to fit a Powerful UK aftermarket blow-molded rear tailgate spoiler on a Discovery 3/4. The spoiler is available in primer or gloss black and replaces the look of the rear tailgate area by covering the existing third brake light and incorporating a later-model Evoque/Range Rover Sport style smoked LED brake light. The kit also includes a replacement washer jet nozzle with an extended hose that routes through the spoiler.

The installation involves removing the upper tailgate trim panel, extracting the existing washer jet (which has a tricky internal clip), disconnecting the brake light connector and cutting the wires with maximum length preserved, drilling holes for two mechanical fixings using supplied templates, drilling through the existing brake light housing to route the new brake light wire, assembling the spoiler with brake light and washer jet, trial fitting to check alignment, applying adhesive bond-and-seal, threading wires and hose through while positioning the spoiler, securing with M6 grub screws and conical washers, and clamping the center section down while the adhesive cures. Electrical connections are made using wago-type quick connectors (supplied) with optional soldering for corrosion resistance.

## Key Points

- Lower your air suspension before starting -- all work is done at roof height
- The OEM Land Rover spoiler required removing the bonded-in brake light, which is extremely difficult and often breaks the light
- The upper tailgate trim panel pulls straight down and is held by clips
- The existing washer jet has an internal clip that is very difficult to release from outside -- the kit includes a new nozzle with longer hose
- The washer jet tube has a one-way valve; disconnect from inside the tailgate cavity
- The brake light wire (green and black) runs through a clip inside the tailgate -- tug to find the connector, no special release mechanism
- Cut the brake light connector off with maximum wire length preserved -- this connects to the new light without cutting the car's wiring loom
- Drill holes for mechanical fixings using supplied templates; reference the template's square edge to the corner of the tailgate; hole should be approximately 38mm from back edge
- M6 grub screws go through spoiler into drilled holes (7-8mm drill for clearance); the drill goes through two skins
- The drilled holes exit outside the weather seal so water ingress is not a concern for the interior
- Drill a ~10mm hole through the existing brake light housing to route the new brake light wire into the cavity
- A template is provided showing where to drill through the brake light area, avoiding the metal surround
- Trial fit BEFORE applying adhesive -- check grub screw alignment first
- Apply adhesive along the top/back edge and around fixing holes; not too much along the bottom edge where there is a gap
- Fit one grub screw first to guide positioning; fit the second after the spoiler is placed
- Conical washers: flat side against bodywork, narrow end of cone goes on first
- Put masking tape on the back edge of the spoiler and the roof to prevent scratching during fitting
- Use a plastic spacer wedged between the open tailgate and the roof to clamp the center section down while adhesive cures
- Wire connections: use supplied wago-type connectors; match brown-to-brown and blue-to-blue (or red-to-red, black-to-black)
- Soldering the wire ends before inserting into connectors prevents corrosion and adds mechanical strength
- Wrap the connector area in foam to prevent rattling
- Test brake light and washer jet before refitting the trim panel

## Notes

- The Discovery 3 and Discovery 4 share the same tailgate, so the kit fits both
- The presenter admits to doing some steps out of optimal order -- recommend fitting mechanical fixings and trial fitting before bonding the brake light into the spoiler
