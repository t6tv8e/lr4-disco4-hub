## Summary

This video provides a detailed side-by-side comparison of the 2020 Land Rover Defender 110 (L663) and the Discovery 4. The presenter parks both vehicles nose-to-nose and walks around them, comparing styling, dimensions, interior space, load capacity, and specifications. The video highlights how remarkably similar the two vehicles are in many respects -- both designed by Gerry McGovern -- suggesting Land Rover positioned the Defender as a natural successor for Discovery 4 owners.

The comparison reveals that while the vehicles are nearly identical in length, width, and performance, the Discovery 4 has a significantly larger boot space (110cm floor vs 87cm in the Defender) and can fit a standard pallet with rear seats folded. The Defender is lighter by 300kg thanks to its aluminium monocoque construction vs the D4's body-on-chassis design. The Defender wins on interior technology and infotainment, while the D4 excels in practical load space, storage compartments, and the versatility of its split tailgate design.

## Key Points

- Both vehicles designed by Gerry McGovern
- Discovery 4 is wider and longer; Defender is slightly higher
- Similar clamshell bonnet design with blunt front edge on both
- Windscreen rake is nearly identical; Defender windscreen sits slightly higher
- D4 has distinctive "stadium seating" -- rear seats are higher for better visibility; Defender is flat front to back
- D4's alpine windows and stepped roofline are not present on the Defender
- Side profiles are remarkably similar: door pillars, quarter light panels, and window lines nearly align when parked side by side
- Both have seven-seat options
- D4 engine: 3.0 SDV6 diesel, 242 bhp; Defender comparable diesel: 2.0L, 234 bhp
- Fuel economy: both low 30s mpg
- 0-60: Defender 8.7s, D4 9.0s -- D4's extra 300kg accounts for the difference
- D4 is body-on-chassis construction; Defender is aluminium monocoque (body is the structure)
- Boot floor measurement: D4 = 110cm, Defender = ~87cm
- Total load length (rear seats folded to boot lip): D4 = ~192cm, Defender = ~160-170cm
- A standard pallet fits in the D4 but not the Defender (must go in at a steep angle)
- D4 split tailgate allows standing on the lower section to access the roof rack without a ladder
- Defender rear door opens sideways; D4 has the upper/lower split tailgate
- D4 has more storage bins and pockets, especially in the rear section
- Defender wins on USB/charging points and infotainment technology
- Defender loses some boot length because total vehicle length includes the rear spare wheel
