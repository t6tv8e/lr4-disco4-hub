## Summary

A straightforward guide to replacing the cabin pollen filter on the Land Rover Discovery 4. The presenter explains that there are two types of replacement filter available: a standard white paper pollen filter or a carbon-impregnated pollution filter (which appears black). This video uses the carbon type. The job requires no tools and takes just a few minutes.

The filter is accessed behind the glove box. By pressing the spring clips on either side of the glove box, it drops down fully to reveal the blower motor area. A small door with a tab opens to expose the filter element. The presenter demonstrates removing the old (visibly dirty) filter, noting the airflow direction marking, and installing the new one with the airflow arrow pointing downward. The small door clicks back into place and the glove box pops back up to its normal position.

## Key Points

- Two filter types available: standard pollen filter (white paper) or carbon pollution filter (black, carbon-impregnated)
- No tools required -- entirely a hand job
- Access is behind the glove box: push back the spring clips on each side of the glove box (higher up where the thumbnail is works best) to release it and drop it down fully
- Dropping the glove box also exposes the fuse box if needed
- The filter door has a small tab to press to open it; it has a hinge on one side and two small ears on the other
- Open the door wide enough to clear the two ears from behind their locating points
- The airflow direction arrow on the filter must point downward when installed
- Push the new filter upward into the housing at the top before closing the door
- Note the small ramps on the back of the door -- the filter must be pushed up past them or the door won't close
- The door clicks shut, then the glove box pops back up on its own
- A good first job if you have just bought a used D4 and it smells a bit stale
