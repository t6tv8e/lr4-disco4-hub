## Summary

This video demonstrates the complete process of removing the front bumper from a 2010 (pre-facelift) Discovery 4 and fitting a later 2014 facelift-style bumper. The presenters walk through all the fixings, clips, and electrical connections that need to be disconnected, including parking sensor plugs, fog light wiring, and headlight washer hoses.

The video also covers the differences between the early and late Discovery 4 tow eye covers, fog light bezels, and washer jets, noting which parts are interchangeable and which need to be replaced when doing an early-to-late bumper conversion.

## Key Points

- Prerequisites: headlights, grille, and wheel arch liners must be removed before the bumper can come off
- Centre fixings are M8 headed bolts; side fixings use Torx T30 screws
- There are small self-tapping screws in the wheel arch area that hold the arch liner to the bumper
- The tow eye cover is removed by rotating quarter-turn fasteners (thread runs front to back)
- On one side there are two electrical plug connections (parking sensors and fog lights) with push-release connectors
- On the other side there are two headlight washer hoses with inline connectors
- Four large round mounting points on the underside of the bumper (note: these may snap off on older cars)
- The facelift bumper uses peg/ramp "docking station" locators that clip into place
- Early D4 tow eye covers have squarer internal mouldings; late D4 covers have round mouldings
- Early D4 fog lights are round; late D4 fog lights are squarish

## Parts Mentioned

- Early-to-late D4 conversion requires: new washer jets (flush style vs. protruding), new fog light bezels, new tow eye cover
- Fog lamps are the same between early and late D4
- Parking sensors and parking sensor wiring are the same between early and late D4
- Wheel arch liners are the same between early and late D4
- Discovery 3 to D4 conversion is more complex: requires new parking sensors, new parking sensor loom, and new fog lights

## Notes

- The presenters note that their car's underside bumper bolts had snapped off, which is apparently common on older vehicles
- If converting from a Discovery 3 (rather than early D4 to late D4), a separate more complex video is referenced
- The video is part of a series covering front-end work on the Discovery 4
