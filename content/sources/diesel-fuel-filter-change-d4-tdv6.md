## Summary

This video demonstrates a diesel fuel filter replacement on the Land Rover Discovery 4 3.0 TDV6 engine. The filter is located underneath the vehicle, behind a heat shield near the gearbox crossmember. The presenters work through the process for the first time, providing a genuine learning experience with practical tips along the way.

The filter housing uses a bayonet fitting with three tabs that lock into place by rotation. The job involves removing a small heat shield plate (two 10mm bolts), disconnecting an electrical connector, rotating the bayonet to release the old filter canister, swapping over the drain plug assembly to the new canister, fitting new O-rings (large and small), and reinstalling. The video also covers the self-priming process -- cycling the ignition on (without starting) several times to let the fuel pump prime the new filter before starting the engine.

## Key Points

- The fuel filter lives underneath the vehicle, behind a heat shield near the gearbox crossmember
- Remove the under body guard to access the filter area
- Two 10mm bolts hold the heat shield plate (should be three, but one was missing from a previous service)
- The filter uses a bayonet fitting -- look for the arrow and white mark alignment indicators for lock/unlock positions
- Disconnect the electrical connector before removing the filter (press the clip to release)
- The large rubber O-ring in the middle of the filter makes it quite tight to push up into place -- requires firm pressure and wiggling
- Transfer the drain plug assembly from the old canister to the new one -- no new O-rings are supplied for the drain plug itself
- Two new O-rings are included in the kit: one large, one small
- The filter housing has a built-in stop to prevent over-tightening
- Self-priming: turn the ignition on without pressing the brake pedal (do not start the engine) -- repeat 2-3 times to let the fuel pump fill the new filter
- Listen for the fuel pump whirring noise during priming -- it decreases as air is purged
- Check underneath for leaks after starting the engine

## Parts Mentioned

- Britpart fuel filter (part number LR009705)
- Large O-ring (included in kit)
- Small O-ring (included in kit)
- Flat washer on drain plug (reuse from old canister)
