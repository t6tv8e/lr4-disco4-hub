## Summary

This video demonstrates the fitting of a custom-designed MOLLE plate to the rear side panels of a Land Rover Discovery 3/4. The presenters developed this product to bring Defender-style accessory mounting capability to the Discovery. Unlike the Defender, which has removable waist rail trim with captive nuts for attachments, the Discovery requires a custom mounting solution using 3D-printed blocks, riv nuts, and the existing roof rail channel.

The installation involves mounting slider hooks into the roof rail channel (wide side towards the centre of the car), drilling two 9.5mm holes in the rear body panel for riv nuts (locations chosen to avoid wiring), and bolting 3D-printed mounting blocks to the body. The MOLLE plate then hooks over the top roof rail mounts and bolts to the lower blocks. A template is provided for marking drill holes. The presenters recommend masking tape to protect paint and using ACF50 anti-corrosion fluid on the drilled holes. An adhesive-only option is mentioned for those who do not want to drill.

## Key Points

- Available as left, right, or pair; same kit works for both sides
- Top mounts use existing roof rail channels with slider hooks (wide side of slider faces centre of car)
- Bottom mounts require drilling two 9.5mm holes and installing riv nuts
- 3D-printed mounting blocks bolt to the riv nuts via M6 bolts with nyloc nuts
- The plate is designed to spread load across the panel, especially near the window where the body is stronger
- A template is provided for marking drill positions
- No wiring runs behind the chosen drill locations (verified by the presenters)
- Use ACF50 anti-corrosion fluid on drilled holes to prevent rust
- The plate is angled to match the door line
- All fittings are stainless steel
- Can be fitted with double-sided adhesive instead of drilling (mount blocks first, then lower plate down)
- Provides added security by obscuring rear window contents

## Parts Mentioned

- MOLLE plate (left and right versions, powder coated)
- 3D-printed mounting blocks
- Coach bolts and nyloc nuts
- M6 bolts
- Spacer washers
- Slider hooks for roof rail
- Riv nuts (9.5mm hole)
- Riv nut installation tool (Ceely brand mentioned)
- ACF50 anti-corrosion fluid
- MOLLE clips/hooks (available on their website)
- Land Rover boot tie-down hooks (cheap on eBay, can be mounted in four orientations)
- Land Rover Defender-style activity/lunchbox (can be mounted using six specific holes)
- 2-inch masking tape for paint protection

## Notes

- Compatible with Discovery 3 and Discovery 4
- The plate has mounting holes for work lights, spotlights, or DRL lights along the top for camping illumination
- Four cutout points accept Land Rover boot tie-downs in any 90-degree orientation
- Six holes are specifically placed to accept the Land Rover Defender lunchbox/activity box
- The presenters plan to sell pre-mounted activity box kits painted black
- If fitting with adhesive only, mount the blocks to the plate first, then lower the assembly onto the car
