## Summary

This video walks through the removal of the front wheel arch liners on a Discovery 4. The presenters have colour-coded the fasteners orange on their car to make them easier to identify on camera. The process involves removing a cross-head screw near the headlight area, two pop rivets (poppers) under the wheel arch at the rear, and potentially an additional self-tapping screw that may be present on one or both sides.

The headlights need to be removed first to access one of the screws. Once all fasteners are out, the arch liner pops off by pulling from the front edge and sliding it upward. The video also shows the mud and debris that accumulates behind the liner on cars that have been off-roaded.

## Key Points

- Headlights must be removed first (described as a "two second job" with a link to a separate video)
- One cross-head (Posi) screw at the front near the headlight area
- Two pop rivets (poppers) under the rear of the wheel arch, released by finding a small slot and levering with a flat screwdriver
- An additional self-tapping screw may be present further in; one side of the car had a broken tab while the other had the screw intact with a washer
- Turning the front wheel can give better access to the inner fixings
- The liner clips in and needs to be slotted upward slightly when removing
- Significant mud and debris accumulates behind the liner, so cleaning is recommended while it is off

## Parts Mentioned

- Pop rivets / push-fit clips (two per side, reusable if kept together)
- Self-tapping screws (cross-head / Posi drive)
- Headlight removal referenced as a prerequisite

## Notes

- The presenters note that the fixings can vary slightly between sides of the car, possibly due to age, previous work, or broken tabs
- This is part of a series of front-end disassembly videos; removing the arch liners is a prerequisite for front bumper removal
- Colour-coded fastener markings are a presentation aid only and will not be present on the viewer's car
