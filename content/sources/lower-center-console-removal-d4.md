## Summary

A quick walkthrough of removing the lower centre console (around the gear selector area) on a Discovery 4. The presenters are doing this to search for a factory-fitted cable used for retrofitting a reversing camera, though they note their particular vehicle doesn't have the cable. The procedure involves removing the cup holder insert, two self-tapping cross-head screws, the gear knob, and then lifting the whole console assembly up and away.

The video is relatively short and straightforward. One important tip is to disconnect the battery before unplugging the various connectors underneath, as the vehicle can get upset about things like the handbrake being disconnected. The presenters also show the gear change cable, handbrake release cable, and other components visible once the console is removed.

## Key Points

- Remove the cup holder insert first -- it sits on metal clips and has a slot that must align when reinstalling
- Two self-tapping cross-head screws are hidden under the cup holder area (one each side)
- Move the gear selector to neutral before removal: start engine, foot on brake, shift to neutral, stop engine, keep foot on brake
- Remove the gear knob: press the button in and pull up firmly -- it just pulls off
- A trim piece on the side needs to be released before the console lifts up
- Disconnect the battery before unplugging connectors to avoid warning lights/errors (especially the handbrake sensor)
- Various cables and connectors are visible once removed: gear change cable, handbrake release cable, and electrical connectors
- Some vehicles have a factory cable routed under the console for retrofitting a reversing camera -- but not all vehicles have it
- Reassembly is the reverse of removal

## Parts Mentioned

- Self-tapping cross-head screws x2 (both the same)
- Gear knob (press-button pull-off type)
- Cup holder insert (metal clip retention with alignment slot)
- Gear change cable
- Handbrake release cable
