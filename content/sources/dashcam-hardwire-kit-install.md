## Summary

This video demonstrates how to install a dashcam hardwire power kit on a Range Rover Sport L320 (2005-2012), Discovery 3, or Discovery 4. The kit taps into the interior light connector in the headliner to provide a switched 12V power supply. The interior light loom carries signals for door-open light activation, power, earth, and other functions. The kit intercepts the ignition/unlock live wire and earth to provide power that turns on when the car is unlocked and off when locked.

The installation involves removing the centre console light housing from the headliner (pull from the front spring clips, not the rear legs), unplugging the existing connector, plugging in the tap-in loom between the car and the light unit, and tucking the power converter into the space above the headliner. The cable can be routed to the windscreen using a flexible ruler pushed through behind the headliner. The kit is available in three variants: a bare tap-in loom (provides 12V for DIY use), a USB-A kit, or a USB-C kit, each with an inline fuse and a 12V-to-5V converter.

## Key Points

- The kit taps into the interior light connector in the headliner -- no cutting of wires required
- Power is switched by the car's unlock/ignition system: live when unlocked, off when locked
- This is NOT a permanent battery live -- the dashcam will not record while the car is locked/parked
- Three kit options: bare 12V tap-in loom, USB-A kit, or USB-C kit
- The 12V-to-5V converter brick and inline fuse tuck into the space above the headliner
- To remove the headliner light console: pull down from the front edge (spring clips), NOT from the rear (legs sit under a metal frame)
- Be careful not to pull the metal frame out of the headlining
- The electrical connector has a push-tab in the middle to release it
- Route cables to the windscreen using a flexible ruler behind the headliner
- Mount the dashcam on the passenger side to avoid obstructing the driver's view
- Use Velcro or foam to secure the converter brick and prevent rattling
- The power is derived from a fuse (fuse number to be noted on screen in the video)
- Some dashcams (e.g., BlackVue) take 12V directly; most use 5V USB
