## Summary

This video covers the fitting of genuine Land Rover rear light lamp guards (part number VPLAP0009) to a Discovery 4. The presenter notes that while the overall rear light housing is the same between Discovery 3 and 4, the light guard shapes differ to match the different lens patterns -- the D4 guards have more curvature to suit the round-style lenses. The kit includes brackets, an Allen key, sticky foam pads, riv nuts, screws, and washers.

The installation requires removing the rear light, assembling brackets onto the guard (four identical brackets with welded nuts, plus an S-shaped bracket for the bottom corner and an "Olympics logo" style bracket for the top), fitting foam spacer pads, marking and drilling two holes per side for mounting, and reassembling everything with the light back in place. The presenter opts to skip the riv nut method and instead uses self-drilling roofing-grade screws for a quicker installation. Two of the four brackets stay on the guard while two mount to the car body and act as locating points when the guard is offered up.

## Key Points

- Genuine Land Rover part number VPLAP0009 (Discovery 4 specific)
- Discovery 3 and 4 light guards are different shapes despite the light housings being interchangeable
- The rear light must be removed first before fitting
- Four identical brackets have welded nuts and ridges to prevent wobbling
- The S-shaped bracket goes in the bottom square corner
- Foam spacer pads are stuck to the inner edge of the guard as spacers
- Two holes need to be drilled per side for the outer mounting screws
- The presenter used self-drilling roofing-grade screws instead of the supplied riv nuts for simplicity
- Use masking tape behind drilling points to see your marks and prevent drill wander on dark paint
- Apply rust prevention (aerosol paint, silicone, or grease) to drilled holes to prevent corrosion
- Ensure uniform gap all around the guard and that the bottom edge runs parallel to the bumper line
- Use Torx head screws with plastic caps over the light mounting screws to deter theft
- Hand-tighten screws to avoid cross-threading
- Check that the guard does not foul the boot lid when closing
- Cost was approximately 140 pounds for the genuine kit
