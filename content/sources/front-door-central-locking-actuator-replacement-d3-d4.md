## Summary

This is a detailed walkthrough of replacing the central locking actuator on the front passenger door of a Discovery 4 (also applicable to Discovery 3). The presenters demonstrate two different failure modes: one door that locks but won't unlock, and another with the opposite problem. The video covers the full disassembly sequence: removing the door card, extracting the window glass, removing the exterior door handle, unbolting the lock mechanism, and swapping out the actuator assembly.

The presenters use a secondhand replacement actuator (complete with cables) rather than a genuine Land Rover part, noting that aftermarket replacements tend not to last long and genuine parts are expensive and don't include cables. The video shows the full cable disconnection technique -- each cable has a specific clip-and-rotate release method -- and recommends swapping cables one at a time from old to new. A useful testing tip is shared: before reassembling the door fully, screw the control module off the door card, bolt it to the door, close it, and test the central locking with the key fob.

## Key Points

- Two failure modes demonstrated: door locks but won't unlock, and vice versa
- Recommended disassembly sequence: door card off, window glass out, exterior handle off, then unbolt the mechanism
- Removing the window glass first makes accessing the actuator bolts much easier -- only takes a couple of minutes
- The emergency lock barrel (key-operated, driver's side) is secured by a T20 Torx screw hidden behind a small rubber grommet on the door edge
- The original Torx screw head is too large to extract through the grommet hole -- a self-tapper replacement can solve this
- An orange clip/slug on the outside prevents the mechanism sliding out -- remove its Torx screw from inside the door through an access hole
- The inner mechanism is held by small push-in pegs that must be depressed to slide the assembly rearward
- Three T30 Torx screws secure the lock mechanism to the door frame -- they are quite short and tricky to reinstall
- Don't forget the electrical connector inside the door before pulling the mechanism out
- Each cable uses a clip-and-rotate release: press the retaining clips, then rotate the cable end through a triangular notch to remove
- Test the new actuator before full reassembly: mount the control module from the door card onto the door, close the door, and test with key fob
- Remember double-unlock: first press unlocks driver's door only, second press unlocks all doors

## Parts Mentioned

- Central locking actuator assembly (complete with 3 cables) -- secondhand used in this video
- Genuine Land Rover actuator available but expensive and does not include cables
- Two types exist: with deadlock and without deadlock
- T20 Torx screw (emergency lock barrel)
- T30 Torx screws x3 (lock mechanism mounting)
- Door handle spring clips (available from PowerfulUK website)
- Door seal grommets/gaskets (reuse from original)
