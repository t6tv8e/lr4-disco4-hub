## Summary

This video demonstrates how to fit roof rack cross bars to a Discovery 3 or 4, applicable to both the extended (full-length) and shorter (half-length) roof rail types. The cross bars slot into notched positions along the roof rails and can be adjusted both in width (using an internal hex key) and in fore-aft position by sliding to different groove locations. Each end features a locking cam mechanism operated with a key.

The process is straightforward: locate the V-shaped notches on the roof rails (the rear cross bar aligns with the rear pillar), slide the bar into position, adjust the width using the hex screw, engage the locking dog into a groove, and lock with the key. The video shows fitting from start to finish with two people, one on each side of the vehicle.

## Key Points

- Cross bars fit the same way on both extended and short roof rack rails
- The roof rails have notches/grooves at regular intervals where the cross bars can lock in
- The rear cross bar position aligns with the rear pillar of the vehicle, identified by two notches with a V-shape
- Each cross bar end has a plastic cap covering a hex key fitting that allows width adjustment by loosening and sliding the rail sideways
- Get the back edge of the cross bar into the rail notch first, then seat the front
- The locking mechanism uses a cam with a small dog that engages the notches -- the lock can only be operated when the bar is properly seated in a groove
- After positioning, tighten the Allen screw, fit the cap, and lock with the key
- Remove the blue protective film from the stainless steel lock face before fitting
- Two people make the job easier (one per side), but it can be done solo

## Parts Mentioned

- Roof rack cross bars (with locking cam ends and keys)
- Allen key / hex key (for width adjustment, accessed under plastic cap)
- Locking keys (supplied with cross bars)
