## Summary

Vera's Discovery 3 gets a full CB radio installation with an Albrecht compact unit, a 3D-printed dash bracket (design from Roland Foxel), a custom aluminium angle-iron bracket, and an externally mounted antenna. The RG-58 C/U coax cable is routed through an existing body grommet sealed with silicone, and the radio is wired to switch on and off with the ignition. At a Land Rover club outing, Karl-Heinz inspected the antenna grounding and approved the installation, though SWR tuning still needs to be completed with a dedicated meter. The key practical takeaway: CB radio remains essential for overlanding convoys in areas without cell phone reception.

## Notes

- RG-58 C/U coax can be cut to any length without affecting reception; antenna length must not be changed
- Radio goes on/off with ignition — no parasitic drain
- Squelch control eliminates background noise when no signal present
- Cable routed through existing grommet, sealed with black silicone
- Antenna adjustment screw underneath for fine-tuning
- SWR measurement still pending — functional enough for convoy use without it
- 3D-printed bracket design available in video description
