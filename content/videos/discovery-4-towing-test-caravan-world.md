## Summary

Philip Lord from Caravan World Magazine (Australia) tests the facelifted Discovery 4 3.0 TDV6 HSC as a tow vehicle with a 1,500 kg tandem-axle caravan. Key technical highlights include the twin sequential turbo system delivering 180 kW and 600 Nm with 500 Nm available just off idle, virtually eliminating turbo lag.

Towing fuel consumption measured at 15 L/100km, compared to ~9 L/100km on solo highway driving. The D4 comes with towbar and wiring as standard. Land Rover specifically does not recommend load levellers as the air suspension handles load management, though the ride does firm up noticeably when towing.

Overall assessment is extremely positive — "a really comfortable, easy vehicle to tow with" — with excellent acceleration, stability, engine braking, and the five Terrain Response modes adding capability for off-road towing scenarios.

## Notes

- Land Rover's official position: no load levellers — the air suspension handles load distribution
- Air suspension ride quality trade-off: lush when unladen, firmer (but not uncomfortable) when towing
- Towbar and trailer wiring come as standard equipment (Australian market)
- The twin sequential turbo system provides near-instant response from idle
- D4 described as feeling wide on the road but easy to drive despite size
- Interior layout praised: rear seats fold completely flat for flat cargo floor
- Five Terrain Response modes allow the vehicle to be optimised for various towing environments
