## Summary

Robert Pepper (automotive journalist and 4x4 instructor) delivers a masterclass on understanding Land Rover's Terrain Response system and how to actually use it effectively. Rather than just describing what each mode is called, he explains the underlying principle: every terrain is a combination of three characteristics — **soft**, **rough**, and **slippery**.

### Key Concept: Think Soft/Rough/Slippery

- **Sand** = typically soft (not slippery, not rough)
- **Ice** = super slippery (not soft, not rough)
- **Rock** = rough (not soft, usually not slippery)
- **Mud** = combination of all three depending on depth

Even within one terrain type, conditions vary enormously — hard-packed beach sand needs no special mode, while deep powder sand demands Sand mode.

### What Terrain Response Actually Changes

| System | Grass/Gravel/Snow | Sand | Rock Crawl | Mud/Ruts |
|--------|------------------|------|------------|----------|
| **Throttle** | Less responsive | Tapered (starts slow, builds) | Less responsive | Similar to Sand |
| **Gear shifts** | 2nd gear start, early upshift | Holds gears | Holds gears | Holds gears |
| **Brake traction control** | Slow response (allows slip) | Tapered response | Very fast (minimum spin) | Similar to Rock |
| **Centre diff** | Increased preload | Increased preload, quick response | Maximum preload, quick response | — |
| **ESC** | Reduced | Reduced | Reduced further | Reduced |
| **Hill Descent** | On | Off | On | On |
| **Air Suspension** | — | — | Raised | — |

### Additional Mode Effects
- **Mud/Ruts**: Extra headlamp washing
- **Sand**: Extra fan cooling operation
- **Rock Crawl**: Brake pre-charge (applies brakes preemptively when wheel lifts off rock)
- **Wade mode**: Wipes brakes after exiting water (DIY tip: left-foot brake briefly after wading if your car lacks this)

### Practical Mode Selection

| Terrain | Recommended Mode |
|---------|-----------------|
| Deep soft sand/dunes | Sand |
| Rutted sand dunes | Mud/Ruts |
| Very slow extraction (max tracks) | Rock Crawl |
| Shallow slippery mud | Grass/Gravel/Snow |
| Deep thick mud | Mud/Ruts or Rock Crawl |
| Steep hills | Always Rock Crawl (locks centre diff) |
| Most general off-road | Auto or Mud/Ruts |

### Expert Tips
- **Auto mode** does a "pretty good job" and is fine for most driving
- **Closed-loop system**: even in the wrong mode, the car will eventually compensate — it just won't be as efficient
- **Rock Crawl for hills**: always use this for gnarly hills to lock the centre differential — important safety consideration
- **Experiment**: try the same obstacle in different modes to learn how the car responds

## Notes

- Robert Pepper previously used Mud/Ruts as his default mode on a Discovery 3
- Land Rover is "the OG" of terrain response systems — most comprehensive implementation
- Other manufacturers (Jeep Select Terrain, Nissan All-Mode, Ford Drive Modes, Mitsubishi MTS) have similar but generally less sophisticated systems
- Ineos Grenadier deliberately chose NOT to include an ATS system — "just want to keep the vehicle simple"
- The presenter feels many manufacturers make only "token changes" and add marketing names without meaningful calibration differences
