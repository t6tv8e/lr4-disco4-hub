## Summary

Christian and Vera begin a comprehensive preventive overhaul of their fully-running Discovery 3 at 270,000 km, performing a complete body-off procedure to access the engine, chassis, and drivetrain. The scope includes glow plugs, timing chains, valve stem seals, hydraulic lifters, head gaskets, pistons, engine bearings, turbocharger, EGR valves, dual-mass flywheel, and clutch. The initial budget of 2,500 EUR grew to 3,600 EUR once all parts were sourced. During disassembly, several issues were uncovered: an oil leak between the transmission and transfer case, a failed aftermarket axle, deteriorated driveshaft boot, worn air shocks, chassis rust and cracking, and an EGR valve with the concerning 4R8Q FoMoCo prefix. The video documents the full body-off sequence with practical tips, including the critical step of opening the rear hatch before disconnecting the battery, and tracking AC oil loss during disassembly.

## Notes

- This is the second body-off the team has performed (first was on a Discovery 4); a time-lapse camera was set up to address viewer criticism about the first attempt
- The vehicle has a manual transmission, which adds the complication of a rotten hydraulic clutch actuation line during body separation
- The Discovery 3 body-off has different interference points than the Discovery 4 (air tank and compressor housing)
- Electrical connectors untouched for 16 years were found in perfect condition
- Fuel-burning heater was left in place (unlike the D4 build where it was removed)
- BF Goodrich KO2 tyres desired but cannot fit on 18-inch rims; 20-inch rims being swapped out
