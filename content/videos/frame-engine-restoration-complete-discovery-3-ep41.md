## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME reassemble the engine and drivetrain for their Discovery 3 body-off restoration project. This episode covers the complete installation of the dual mass flywheel, clutch, and engine back into the frame, followed by cooling system and ancillary component reassembly.

The dual mass flywheel installation is shown in detail with critical technical points: the flywheel has rotational slop and no dowel pin, so the crankshaft timing pin must be inserted through the starter motor aperture to ensure correct timing alignment. The 3-stage torque procedure (50 Nm, then +45 degrees, then +45 degrees to a minimum of 80 Nm) is demonstrated with cross-pattern tightening, followed by a dial indicator runout check (specification: 0.15 mm maximum). New M10x47 bolts from Land Rover are required as the originals stretch during torquing.

A custom clutch alignment arbor was machined on a lathe since no standard tool exists for this application. Key technical insights include: never apply brake cleaner to the DMF internals (the spring mechanism is sensitive), never over-lubricate the inner DMF bearing (factory grease is sufficient), and apply a thin coat of Metaflux anti-seize to the input shaft spline despite Land Rover's dry-assembly specification — this prevents fretting rust that causes stiff shifting, mirroring a Dodge Challenger recall fix.

The episode also covers cooling system reassembly with a donated genuine Land Rover thermostat (two variants exist for EU3 vs EU4 engines), A/C dryer replacement (mandatory after extended system opening), and the critical assembly order for the crossover pipe (flanges first, then bracket — reversing this cracks the pipe).

## Notes

- Transmission bolts torqued to 40 Nm; engine mount bolts to 90 Nm
- 50% new coolant hoses installed from a viewer donation; every hose, tie wrap, and cable route double-checked
- The starter motor bracket modification (filing a small section) makes starter installation dramatically easier and can be done in-vehicle with a die grinder
- Do not use a metal pick on aluminum A/C fittings — use a plastic pick (iPhone repair tool) to avoid scratching sealing surfaces
