## Summary

Peter repaints the front grille and side air inlet on his Discovery 4 from the stock silver finish to a steel gray metallic that matches the door handles, creating a unified exterior look. The side inlet pops out with a plastic pry tool, and the front grille releases via four top clips, with its inner mesh and outer frame separating into two pieces for cleaner painting. The process involves sanding with 240 and 600-grit paper, cleaning with brake disc cleaner, applying two color coats plus two clear coats of Duplicolor paint, and reassembling everything with clips and adhesive.
