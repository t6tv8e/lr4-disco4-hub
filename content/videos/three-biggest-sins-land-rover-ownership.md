## Summary

Christian from LR TIME delivers a direct lecture on the three most critical mistakes Discovery 3 and 4 owners make. Sin 1: not owning a diagnostic scanner — dash warning lights only indicate something is wrong, not what. The iCarSoft (under EUR 100) reads and resets fault codes and should always be in the car and always brought when inspecting a used vehicle for purchase. Sin 2: not knowing whether the diesel engine has the updated oil pump — early D3 models (2005 onward, some as late as 2009) have a weak casting that fails catastrophically. Three pump generations exist, and only the latest with reinforced casting AND a 15mm rotor (same as fitted to D4) is safe; intermediate versions with a 13.5mm rotor despite updated casting are still at risk. Sin 3: not knowing the vehicle's complete maintenance history — timing belt, oil pump status, EGR valves, suspension bushings, alternator, battery, oil grade (5W-40 recommended over 5W-30 for out-of-warranty vehicles).

## Notes

- The oil pump failure is the only commonly preventable catastrophic engine failure on the Discovery 3 diesel
- Land Rover issued a service bulletin about the weak oil pump — authorised workshops should know about it
- Weekly Facebook posts still appear showing engine failures from unfixed oil pumps — despite the issue being over a decade old
- Christian recommends using 5W-40 oil instead of the factory-specified 5W-30 on out-of-warranty vehicles
- The video was produced while driving — conversational format covering 3 of 11 items on a list (remaining items not covered)
