## Summary

ReDriven provides arguably the most comprehensive Discovery 4 buyer's guide available, combining a professional mechanic's assessment with an automotive journalist's review. Their mechanic Jim bluntly states that "statistically Land Rover for the last 10 years is the most unreliable car brand" and lists the common problems: air suspension failure, electronic parking brake seizure, oil leaks, electrical gremlins, fuel leaks, gearbox problems, airbag faults, turbo soot clogging, fuel gauge sensor failure, suspension arm wear, and a fragile cooling system.

Despite this, the driving impression is glowing: the command seating position, superb visibility, air suspension ride quality, and blend of on-road comfort with genuine off-road ability are described as nearly perfect. The 7-seat layout is genuine full-size (unlike BMW X5's "5+2"). Australian pricing ranges from $20K (base spec early model with probable issues) to $90K (2016 top spec HSE/Landmark with almost no depreciation).

Their conclusion: "we so want to say yes because when these are working at 100% they're spectacular... but the potential for things to go horribly wrong is just way too high." They recommend full service history as essential and warn that ownership without a financial buffer for repairs is risky.
