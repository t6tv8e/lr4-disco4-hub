## Summary

A step-by-step guide to removing the front door card (interior trim panel) on a Discovery 4. This is presented as part one of a three-part series covering door card removal, window glass removal, and central locking actuator replacement. The video covers all screw locations, clip types, and the order of electrical disconnections needed to fully remove the door card.

The presenters highlight that door card removal is needed for many common jobs: central locking actuator replacement, broken cable repair, window glass removal, speaker upgrades, and wing mirror replacement. They walk through each fastener location methodically, noting that most screws are the same size (cross-head) except for two long ones at the bottom of the door card. After the screws are out, the card pulls straight out with clips along the top and edges. Several electrical connectors must then be disconnected in sequence before the card is fully free.
