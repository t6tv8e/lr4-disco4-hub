## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME demonstrate a front wheel bearing replacement on their Discovery 3 during a lunch break, completing the job in approximately 35 minutes. The bearing had lost all its grease and showed noticeable play with the wheel mounted. This was the second bearing failure in under 40,000 km — the couple suspects their wheel spacer extensions are accelerating bearing wear. A cheap bearing lasted only 20,000 km vs 60,000 km for an expensive one, leading them to plan a Timken-quality replacement next time.

A key time-saving technique demonstrated for the first time: removing the entire caliper bracket with pads as one piece, rather than disassembling the caliper first. Copper grease applied to the hub spline and face ensures clean removal at the next service. Torque specifications from the Haynes manual: hub mounting bolts 115 Nm, driveshaft retaining nut 230-350 Nm (Land Rover specifies 350), caliper bracket bolts 275 Nm, road wheel nuts 140 Nm. The driveshaft nut must be staked with a round chisel after torquing.

## Notes

- Wheel spacer extensions may significantly reduce wheel bearing lifespan — budget for more frequent replacements if running them
- Cheap Chinese breaker bars can snap under load — always carry quality tools (Craftsman or equivalent) on road trips
- A spare wheel bearing should be part of the road trip spares kit — this job is feasible in the field with basic tools
- Brembo brake discs on this vehicle were still in good condition — no replacement needed
- Air suspension fuse must be pulled before lifting and reinserted after lowering
