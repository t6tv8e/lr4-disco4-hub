## Summary

LR TIME documents a full brake line replacement on a Discovery 3 after a TUV inspection flagged corroded lines — brake fluid was already dripping from a corroded connector, confirming the lines were at near-failure. The video covers the complete fabrication process: bending steel lines alongside the originals (copper lines are illegal in Germany without a manufacturer service bulletin), proper flaring technique with the critical reminder to thread the flare nut before flaring, and the importance of using Land Rover-spec flare nuts with fewer threads. The job expanded in scope when a completely disintegrated driveshaft center bearing was discovered hidden under the heat shield, along with heavy rear sway bar corrosion and a recurring CV boot failure. The muffler removal alone took approximately 2 hours requiring grinding and heating to red-hot. Total steel brake line material cost was approximately 15 EUR for 5 meters.

## Notes

- Copper brake lines are illegal in Germany unless the manufacturer has issued a specific service bulletin — Land Rover has not, so steel lines are mandatory.
- Steel brake line material costs approximately 15 EUR for 5 meters, making DIY replacement very economical.
- Always thread the flare nut onto the line before flaring — skipping this step requires redoing the flare.
- Land Rover uses flare nuts with fewer threads than standard — standard full-thread nuts will not work correctly.
- Pre-bend new lines alongside originals using tie-wraps to replicate bends before cutting old ones out.
- Pressure test the system at 20 PSI before completing reassembly.
- Worst corrosion occurs at muffler/heat shield junction where trapped heat accelerates rust.
- Center bearing failure was hidden under heat shield and only discovered during this repair — inspect while access is available.
- Rear sway bar can only be replaced with the body off.
- Muffler removal took approximately 2 hours with grinding and heating to red-hot. Grind the pipe interior before reinstallation to ease future removal.
