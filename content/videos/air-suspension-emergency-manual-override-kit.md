## Summary

Far Reach Engineering builds an emergency manual inflation system for an LR3 (same system as LR4) that has completely deflated on all four corners. The concept is simple: splice T-fittings with Schrader valves directly into the air lines after the transfer blocks, allowing manual inflation of each strut using any air compressor or even a bicycle pump.

The critical design decision is splicing after the transfer blocks rather than before — the solenoid valves in the transfer blocks are normally closed and require electrical power to open, so in an electrical failure scenario, air pumped into the supply line would be blocked. By going directly to the individual strut lines, the system bypasses all electronic controls.

The front transfer block's air line color coding is documented: green = driver side, black (top) = passenger side, white stripe = air supply. Testing showed the system successfully raised one side by over an inch, though full inflation depends on the compressor's pressure capability. An initial leak was traced to a loose Schrader valve core — proper tightening resolved it.

## Notes

- This is an overlanding/emergency preparedness solution, not a permanent repair
- The vehicle shown is an LR3, but the system is identical on LR4/Discovery 4
- The presenter notes this is "not completely reversible" since the original air lines are cut, but the T-fittings maintain the original circuit path
- Rear axle has its own transfer block with similar line arrangement
