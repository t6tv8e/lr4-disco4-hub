## Summary

Christian and Vera tackle the replacement of failed rear lower suspension arm bushings on their Discovery 3 after a TUV inspection flagged them. The bushings had failed after only about 80,000 km, likely because they were torqued at the wrong ride height during the previous installation -- suspension bushings must be tightened at exactly 485 mm measured from the ground to the hub centre.

The video demonstrates a no-special-tools bushing removal method: drill through the rubber core with a half-inch drill bit, then cut a slot with a reciprocating saw to collapse the sleeve, taking about 10 minutes per bushing. For installation, the critical detail is the 8-degree offset built into the rear suspension arms -- the new bushing must be pressed in at this angle with the alignment arrows oriented so the vehicle's weight sits in the rubber, not the hollow void.

A key warning is issued against using polyurethane bushings on Discovery 3 or 4 vehicles. Polyurethane bushings lack the outer metal shell present in OEM-spec bushings, and without this shell, stress transfers incorrectly, potentially causing structural issues.

## Notes

- Deflate air suspension fully before starting using GAP 2D diagnostics tool
- Pull the third fuse from the bottom to disable the suspension ECU during work
- Pre-soak bolts with penetrating solvent days ahead if possible; copper grease on previous installations allowed some bolts to be removed by hand
- Two-post lift or jack stands both work; the lift simply makes the job more comfortable
- Homemade press tools were fabricated from workshop stock
- Only lower arm bushings replaced; upper bushings were still serviceable and left in place
- This is Part 1; Part 2 covers reassembly, torquing, and TUV re-inspection
