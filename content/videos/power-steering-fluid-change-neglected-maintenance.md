## Summary

Christian and Vera perform a full power steering fluid flush on a Land Rover Discovery 3 with 250,000 km on the original fluid — a maintenance item that Land Rover never included in the official service schedule. The key innovation is a custom-machined funnel adapter with an O-ring seal that mounts directly onto the reservoir and holds 4 liters of fluid, preventing the system from running dry mid-flush (which would introduce air bubbles). They deviate from the Land Rover manual by installing the new reservoir first rather than last, ensuring all flushed fluid passes through the clean integrated filter. The old reservoir's internal filter was visibly clogged, and the replacement cost only 29 EUR versus a feared 250 EUR. The procedure requires two people: one rotating the steering wheel lock-to-lock and one monitoring the fluid level in the funnel.

## Notes

- Power steering fluid is not on the Discovery 3 official maintenance schedule — Land Rover apparently expected the vehicle would not last long enough to need it.
- Minimum 4 liters of fluid required for a full flush. With only 3 liters you will run dry — turning the steering wheel fully one direction consumes approximately 2 liters.
- The OEM reservoir includes a non-serviceable integrated filter that cannot be cleaned or replaced separately.
- Install the new reservoir before flushing (not after, as the Land Rover manual states) so flushed fluid passes through the clean filter.
- Steering wheel rotation speed during flush: approximately one full lock-to-lock cycle every 5 seconds.
- Used oil disposal in Germany: free community waste mobile visits every approximately 8 weeks accepting oil, coolant, power steering fluid, and brake fluid.
- Fabian's engine rebuild referenced as running well at 12,000 km post-rebuild.
