## Summary

LR TIME walks through nine things they discovered after buying a Discovery 4, comparing it to the D3. The most significant finding is the oil level sensor design flaw: Land Rover engineers installed the sensor inside the oil sump rather than externally, creating two failure modes (connector slip-off and capillary oil wicking) that require an 8-hour repair involving differential removal. The improved version with a sheet metal retaining clip was not introduced until the third production year, after thousands of failures. The remaining eight items cover emergency procedures (manual neutral release, parking brake release), convenience features (valet mode, key fob window close, door unlock toggle, remote window close), the diesel misfuelling prevention device, the car wash neutral behavior (auto-returns to park after 10 minutes), and the hidden diagnostic menu accessible by long-pressing the touchscreen corner.

## Notes

- The oil level sensor is manufactured by Hella (a German lighting and electronics company).
- The car wash neutral trick: engine off in neutral stays in neutral for exactly 10 minutes, then auto-shifts to park -- dangerous for hand-wash car washes that take longer.
- Valet mode prevents access to home address, phone numbers, and garage door opener -- referenced as a real security concern (thieves use valet parking to access home address, then use the car to drive to and burgle the home).
- The D4 comes with a basic screwdriver in the toolkit specifically for the emergency parking brake and neutral release procedures.
