## Summary

A five-hour DIY repair replacing both worn half shafts and the driveshaft on a Discovery 3, documented with honest mistakes shown on camera. The clicking noise from worn CV joints is resolved by replacing both sides simultaneously rather than risking a single-side replacement with the other already compromised. The video provides comprehensive torque specifications: wheel spacer nuts 140 Nm, upper knuckle 70 Nm, sway bar 115 Nm, ball joint 76 Nm, cross member 115 Nm, and half shaft nut 230 Nm (reduced from older 300 Nm spec to protect the wheel bearing). The front differential is refilled by quantity (0.61 liters), not by level. A notable warning is raised about Chinese replacement shafts having visibly smaller tripod and CV joints than the originals.

## Notes

- Ball joint procedure lesson: unwind the nut completely first, then break the taper loose -- doing it in reverse leaves the nut stuck on the thread; apply heat to the nut to expand it if stuck
- Always open the fill plug before the drain plug on the differential to ensure you can refill
- On 2008-2009+ Discovery 3 models, the half shaft design differs from earlier models -- verify compatibility before ordering
- The 22-minute video compresses 5 hours of actual work (1pm-6pm) with many off-camera do-overs
- Copper grease on splines during reassembly makes the next removal easier
- Half shaft nut is crimped with a rounded chisel after torquing to protect the wheel bearing
- A camshaft seal leak was discovered incidentally during the repair (approximately 20ml seepage)
