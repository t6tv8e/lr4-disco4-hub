## Summary

Lost Cause Ranch investigates an LR4 5.0 V8 that's billowing white smoke on cold starts — a symptom that normally screams "head gaskets." However, the tailpipe doesn't smell sweet (ruling out coolant), and the smoke is intermittent (every other start after sitting).

The real culprit: two torn PCV (positive crankcase ventilation) diaphragms sitting on top of the passenger-side valve cover. These diaphragms are supposed to separate the crankcase (full of oil vapor) from the intake manifold (under vacuum). When torn, engine vacuum sucks oil straight into the intake, where it burns and produces the white smoke. The torn diaphragms also created a distinctive "walrus barking" noise from the crankcase vent area.

The fix takes about 15 minutes and costs $40-50 for new diaphragms — compared to a head gasket job which would be a major engine teardown. The random misfire codes (appearing on different cylinders each start) were caused by oil fouling and cleared up after the repair.

## Notes

- Key diagnostic distinction: sweet exhaust smell = coolant/head gasket; oil smell = PCV or valve seal issue
- The intermittent nature (every other cold start) is characteristic of PCV failures — oil accumulates while sitting
- This vehicle had already had its timing chains replaced ~10,000 miles prior
- After repair, expect some continued smoke as residual oil burns out of cats and mufflers — highway driving clears it
- "It took longer to order the wrong part than it did to fix the problem" — initially ordered a vacuum pump ($$$) before discovering the cheap PCV diaphragms were the issue
