## Summary

A straightforward guide to replacing the cabin pollen filter on the Land Rover Discovery 4. The presenter explains that there are two types of replacement filter available: a standard white paper pollen filter or a carbon-impregnated pollution filter (which appears black). This video uses the carbon type. The job requires no tools and takes just a few minutes.

The filter is accessed behind the glove box. By pressing the spring clips on either side of the glove box, it drops down fully to reveal the blower motor area. A small door with a tab opens to expose the filter element. The presenter demonstrates removing the old (visibly dirty) filter, noting the airflow direction marking, and installing the new one with the airflow arrow pointing downward.
