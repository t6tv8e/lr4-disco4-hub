## Summary

This video demonstrates a diesel fuel filter replacement on the Land Rover Discovery 4 3.0 TDV6 engine. The filter is located underneath the vehicle, behind a heat shield near the gearbox crossmember. The presenters work through the process for the first time, providing a genuine learning experience with practical tips along the way.

The filter housing uses a bayonet fitting with three tabs that lock into place by rotation. The job involves removing a small heat shield plate (two 10mm bolts), disconnecting an electrical connector, rotating the bayonet to release the old filter canister, swapping over the drain plug assembly to the new canister, fitting new O-rings (large and small), and reinstalling. The video also covers the self-priming process -- cycling the ignition on (without starting) several times to let the fuel pump prime the new filter before starting the engine.
