## Summary

This is the final video in a three-part series on Discovery 3/4 tailgate actuator issues. It addresses the worst-case scenario: the upper tailgate actuator has failed completely and the tailgate is stuck closed. The problem is a catch-22 — the carpet trim panel hiding the actuator is held in by plastic trim that can only be removed when the tailgate is open. The presenter and Tyler lock themselves inside the car boot and demonstrate how to break through the trim from inside to access the actuator cable and manually release the tailgate.

The method involves pulling back the carpet trim from the top edge, wedging it open with wooden blocks, ripping into the foam backing to expose the actuator, then using a tool and hammer to push the Bowden cable out of the actuator from behind. The second half of the video provides an excellent bench demonstration of the actuator mechanism, showing the two common failure modes: solenoid weakening and spring clip breakage.
