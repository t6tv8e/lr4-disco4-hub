## Summary

Christian builds a fold-away sleeping platform for their Discovery 3 that converts the cargo area into a flat sleeping surface while doubling as a cargo barrier during daily driving. The platform uses 12 mm epoxy resin-coated plywood (chosen over 15 mm for weight), custom-machined steel tube hinges with 1 mm clearance that self-lock when raised, and piano hinges routed flush into grooves for a perfectly flat sleeping surface. The entire system mounts to existing rear seat brackets found in all Discovery 3 and 4 models, with M6 bolts and steel backing plates through the interior plastic. Total build cost was approximately 200 EUR over about 9 hours of construction time. A secondary modification narrows the camping table by 31 mm to slide into storage rails beneath the platform.

## Notes

- 12 mm resin-coated plywood: flexes slightly under body weight but always springs back
- Steel tube hinges: 20 x 20 mm, require tubing with good straightness and no internal seam
- Self-locking hinge: tube slides down when platform is raised, preventing forward collapse
- M6 bolts with steel backing plate: drilled through interior plastic; rivet nuts in plastic failed
- All seat mounting brackets are shared between Discovery 3 and Discovery 4
- Table storage: cut down 31 mm on table saw, slide rails with 6 mm clearance
- Ozone generator purchased for interior odour control (mentioned in passing)
