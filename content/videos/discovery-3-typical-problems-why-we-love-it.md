## Summary

After five-plus years and 90,000 km of ownership, Christian and Vera present an honest assessment of the Discovery 3 as a budget luxury SUV. Purchased for significantly more than a comparable Mercedes C-Class, the D3 now sells for around 10,000 EUR with 160,000 km in Germany. The core argument: spare parts are cheap and widely available from the UK, the car is easier to fix than its reputation suggests, but the absolute rule is to avoid Land Rover dealerships where every repair starts at 1,000 EUR and major work reaches 3,000-4,000 EUR. The video catalogues every known failure point with approximate mileage intervals: front lower suspension arms past 100,000 km, oil pump housing on 2006-2008 builds, timing belt at 160,000 km, EGR valves around 100,000 km, rear hatch lock cable, turbo linkage at 230,000 km, electric parking brake within 6-12 years, rear HP diesel pump belt, wheel bearings, brake pads (one full set per year due to 2.4-2.5 tonne weight), swelling lug nuts, and turbo hoses. The air suspension remains original with proactive care. They note that the manual gearbox (available on certain build years) eliminates transmission failure risk.

## Notes

- Market price in Germany: approximately 10,000 EUR with 160,000 km
- Spare parts shipped from UK for 12-14 EUR
- Every dealership repair minimum 1,000 EUR; major repairs 3,000-4,000 EUR
- Brake pads: approximately 35 EUR per set; discs 40-55 EUR each; one full set consumed per year
- Vehicle weight approximately 2.4-2.5 tonnes
- Manual transmission available on certain build years only
- Repairs described as predictable: "you can turn your speedometer to show you when it's going to break"
- Air suspension longevity attributed to: cleaning shock absorber rubber boots, preventing hose chafing, never pressure-washing near valve control modules
