## Summary

LR TIME evaluates whether the Discovery 4 is a genuine upgrade over the Discovery 3 for used buyers. They examine three categories: price and market value, manufacturing quality, and features/functions. The D4 commands 5,000 to 10,000 euros more than equivalent D3s in Germany. Manufacturing quality did not improve with the facelift, as Land Rover focused on cost reduction and sales rather than build quality. The SDV6 engine (256 hp, 600 Nm) with ZF 8HP transmission is the standout upgrade, but it comes with higher maintenance costs and the elimination of the dipstick in favour of an unreliable oil level sensor. Off-road geometry is worse across all angles, the vehicle is heavier, and payload capacity is reduced. Infotainment looks fancier but adds no real additional functions. The conclusion is that the D4 is only worth the premium if engine power matters to you; otherwise the D3 is the better used buy.

## Notes

- D4 costs 5,000-10,000 euros more than D3 with equivalent mileage in Germany
- D3 resale values are stabilising and rising as vehicles disappear from the market; D4 values still depreciating
- SDV6 produces 256 hp and 600 Nm torque vs TDV6 at 190 hp and 440 Nm
- ZF 8HP transmission with steering wheel paddle shifters is a genuine mechanical upgrade
- Facelift manufacturing quality is not an improvement; parts sourced from cheaper suppliers
- SDV6 reliability and maintenance costs are significantly higher than standard TDV6
- No dipstick on SDV6; oil level sensor failure is a common and expensive (1,500 euro) problem
- Off-road mode height is 51 mm taller but ground clearance did not improve
- Brakes are significantly stronger on the D4
- Bluetooth music is a genuine improvement; USB/iPod ports have compatibility issues with modern iPhones
- Push button start replaces key ignition; can cause issues (key walks away while car is running)
- Heated seats are improved; ambient lighting added; warning sound volume is now adjustable
- D3 owner's manual includes off-road driving instruction section that was removed from D4 manual
- D4 gross vehicle weight increased only 10 kg (6740 vs 6730 kg) despite 68 kg heavier kerb weight
