## Summary

Christian and Vera assemble the short block of a 2.7L TDV6 Discovery 3 engine with 270,000 km. New Nural (Federal Mogul) pistons are weighed and matched at 780-781 grams — consistent with the originals. A custom piston installation sleeve is machined to within 10 microns of the cylinder bore diameter with a 3-degree taper, replacing the frustrating snap ring/spanner band method.

All six connecting rod big-end clearances are tested with plastigauge, yielding results of 0.038-0.042 mm (within spec). Each measurement is photographed and recorded in a custom Excel spreadsheet that validates against Ford manual tolerances. The critical final step is piston protrusion measurement: 24 total readings (each piston measured at 2 positions, twice) determine the correct head gasket grade. A key finding is that even OEM-equivalent new pistons shift the required gasket grade — the pre-purchased Grade 3 gaskets were wrong, requiring Grade 2 instead, wasting approximately 70 EUR.

## Notes

- Part of the LR TIME engine rebuild series (S4-Ep35) for Discovery 3
- TDV6/SDV6 engine blocks and cylinder heads CANNOT be machined — gasket grade selection is critical
- King bearing back coating lightly treated with Scotch-Brite to correct overly tight clearances — bearing running surface never touched
- Custom piston protrusion gauge tool has its own dedicated video on the LR TIME channel
- Excel spreadsheet for 2.7L and 3.0L TDV6 rebuild data available on Patreon — auto-validates all measurements against Ford specs with green/red colour coding
- Torque to rotate with all pistons installed: 15 Nm (max spec 25 Nm) — confirms no binding
