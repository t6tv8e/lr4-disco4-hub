## Summary

Christian and Vera finalise their Discovery 4 rebuild after a crankshaft replacement, walking through every step of making the vehicle road-ready. The video covers a critical main fuse replacement that the local Land Rover dealer could not even identify, a thorough post-break-in oil and filter chip inspection at 1500 km that confirms healthy bearings, and the switch from break-in 5W-30 to long-term 5W-40 C3 oil. Practical setup includes interior detailing tricks, a 45 EUR DAB+ radio retrofit, custom roof tent mounting, and phone mount fabrication. The video also notes the Discovery 4's design quirk of requiring front differential removal just to access the oil pan, and the dangerously low designed oil pressure of only 0.5 bar.

- Vehicle originally had 5 seats; 7-seat conversion done by previous owner with upgraded belts and title change
- At 160 km/h the Discovery automatically lowers suspension by 22 mm, returning to normal below 140 km/h
