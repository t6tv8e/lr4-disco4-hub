## Summary

This video traces and repairs a persistent water leak into the footwell of a Discovery 3. The root cause is a factory assembly defect: Land Rover's own service bulletin (dated July 7, 2005) specifies that a plastic locator stud on the lower A-pillar trim must be cut off flush to prevent it from protruding and breaking the seal against the bodywork. The factory never performed this step, leaving a gap for water ingress. Evidence from the vehicle confirms the defect -- the side where a Land Rover technician had previously installed a snorkel did have the stud correctly cut, while the original untouched side still had the intact stud causing the leak.

The fix uses Land Rover's official repair kit (approximately 24 euros), which includes replacement clips for both sides and an adhesive sealing patch (part DHB500400). The process involves removing the A-pillar trim and wiper arms, cutting the stud flush, cleaning with acetone, applying the sealing patch, and reinstalling with new clips. Total repair time is about 30 minutes.

The video emphasises that this is a 24 euro fix that, if left unaddressed, can corrode wiring harnesses and cascade into catastrophic electrical failure. Land Rover never proactively informed customers about the service bulletin, despite knowing about the defect since 2005.

## Notes

- Mark wiper arm position before removal -- the fine-tooth spline offers multiple positions and incorrect reinstallation misaligns the wipe pattern
- Freezing temperatures increase the risk of snapping clips during removal; the kit supplies replacements
- The owner had been parking uphill and tilted left to limit water pooling to one corner as a workaround
- This vehicle had no sunroof, which ruled out the other common water leak path on Discovery 3
- Bosch wiper blades recommended (550 mm driver / 530 mm passenger for Discovery 3)
- Wiper arm torque: 24 Nm
- Part number for sealing patch: DHB500400
