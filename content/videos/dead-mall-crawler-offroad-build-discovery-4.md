## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME acquire a heavily-armored 2011 Land Rover Discovery 4 nicknamed "Bankrupttos Rex" — so named because the previous owner spent approximately 75,000 EUR on it before giving up after the crankshaft snapped for the second time (this time on a supposed Land Rover exchange engine, after only approximately 30,000 km).

The teardown reveals layers of amateur workmanship disguised by expensive workshop invoices: a dangerous exposed cable near the battery covered with a metal plate rather than fixed, every section of underbody armor using different bolt sizes and head types, flathead screws on underbody components, an improvised cage nut held with wire, an oil cooler line misrouted, and a spare wheel carrier installed so incorrectly that its cable was rubbing the chassis and creating a fire hazard. The approximately 100 kg of underbody armor included 8mm aluminum plating, armored suspension arms, and covers for the air tank and compressor.

This is notably the third Discovery in LR TIME's fleet where an exchange/replacement engine failed again — a recurring warning for buyers of pre-repaired vehicles. The suspected cause is a cheap Chinese crankshaft in the replacement engine, supported by the surprisingly low 17,000 EUR receipt for what was claimed to be a genuine Land Rover exchange engine job.

The rebuild plan includes full body-off restoration to their standard: every bolt replaced with correct OEM-spec hardware, uniform fastener sizes throughout, proper wiring, and a redesigned two-piece spare wheel carrier. Some armor pieces (especially the air tank cover) are genuinely good and will be retained.

## Notes

- The car sat on wet grass for 7 months before work began, causing surface corrosion
- Aftermarket light guards scratched both lens glass and plastic — caused net damage
- The previous installer numbered all plugs, but since every plug is a different shape, none could be mis-inserted — revealing lack of understanding
- Robin (son) now drives his own Discovery 3 daily including a solo trip to the Pyrenees in snow
- Phillip's Discovery 3 was driven to southern France and Spain after their repair
- A TUV-certified rental trailer rated for 2.75 tons does not require wooden wheel chocks — that applies only to light DIY trailers
- Critical warning: buying a Discovery with an exchange engine is risky — verify the quality and source of the replacement before purchasing
