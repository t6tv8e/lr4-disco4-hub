## Summary

Fabian's Discovery 3 suffered catastrophic engine failure from spun main bearings, traced to a faulty remanufactured fuel pump that caused prolonged cranking cycles of 3-15 seconds at each startup. These repeated cranking events loaded the main bearings without full oil pressure, progressively wearing the bearing surfaces until they spun and cut off oil supply. The video documents the full engine teardown, revealing zero cylinder bore wear at 190,000 km (confirming the TDV6 is fundamentally long-lived when properly lubricated), explains bearing science and oil delivery mechanics, and details the painful 12-hour engine removal without a body lift. The rebuild is estimated at 3,200 EUR including line boring and a new crankshaft, versus 3,500 EUR for a used engine of questionable provenance.

## Notes

- Bearings are rated for approximately 500,000 start cycles under normal conditions; prolonged cranking rapidly consumes this budget
- The hydrodynamic oil film that protects bearings requires full oil pressure, which is absent during cranking
- A spun bearing closes the oil supply hole in the block, creating a self-reinforcing failure loop
- Injectors should be bench-tested at Bosch rather than replaced new at 300 EUR each
- Fuel rail pipes are metal-to-metal with no logical reason to replace despite Land Rover recommendation (suspected upselling)
- Vacuum pump gasket is UK-sourced only
- Stretch bolts (head bolts etc.) must be replaced, never reused
