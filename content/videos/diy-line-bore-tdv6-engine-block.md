## Summary

Christian from LR TIME demonstrates an advanced DIY line boring operation on Fabian's Discovery 3 TDV6 engine block, which suffered catastrophic main bearing seizure. Two inner main bearing races spun and destroyed both the crankshaft and the bearing bores, but critically, the front and rear outer mains remained intact — providing the reference surfaces needed for a home-shop repair. Christian builds a custom boring bar from a high-precision 40mm Bosch hydraulic cylinder rod (0.01mm TIR over 1000mm), with a cutting tool ground from a 12mm HSS end mill and a brass wedge mechanism allowing 0.01mm diameter adjustments. Aluminum guide bushings machined to bore diameter + 0.02mm provide the concentricity alignment. The key insight enabling this repair is that when bearings seize, the extreme heat bends the bearing caps inward, narrowing the bore below the original 75mm — so material is available to machine back to spec. The operation is performed at 190 RPM on a hobby milling machine turned horizontally. Both damaged mains were bored to 75mm minus 0.02mm with excellent concentricity. The crankshaft will be professionally ground to match the newly bored block, saving over EUR 1,500 in block replacement and GBP 1,000 in crankshaft replacement costs.

## Notes

- This is Fabian's Discovery 3 — the same car whose engine was torn down and inspected at 270,000 km in a separate LR TIME video
- Line boring is normally a machine-shop-only operation — this DIY approach is only feasible when intact outer mains exist as guide references
- The bearing cap machining removes the factory-stamped numbers — must re-stamp immediately before losing track
- A modified bolt tightening order is required after machining because the caps are now slightly shorter
- Prussian blue is unavailable in Germany — Sharpie used as substitute for marking bore surfaces during cutting
- Christian's father (65 years of experience) assisted with the final boring passes
