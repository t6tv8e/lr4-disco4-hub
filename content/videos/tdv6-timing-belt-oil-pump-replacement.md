## Summary

Christian and Vera tackle a two-day timing belt and oil pump replacement on a TDV6 engine, rated 8/10 difficulty by Land Rover. The TDV6 has two timing belts (camshaft and high-pressure fuel pump); only the front camshaft belt is replaced here at 120,000 km. During disassembly, the suspected oil pump leak is traced to the upper camshaft seal instead. Both front seals are replaced. The oil pump is swapped and primed using a clever dry-install method that avoids contaminating fresh sealant. A Gates aftermarket tensioner fails during installation (stripped Allen socket), leading to reuse of the OEM NSK-bearing tensioner. Key torque specs, part numbers, and the complete priming procedure are documented in detail.

## Notes

- The TDV6 has two timing belts: one for the camshaft, one for the high-pressure fuel pump. They do not need to be replaced simultaneously.
- Timing belt replacement interval is 160,000 km; belt was replaced at 120,000 km.
- Crankshaft pulley bolt (10.9 stretch bolt) can be reused up to 3 times; center-punch to track reuse.
- Crankshaft pulley bolt part number (14 mm): LR002454A. Large O-ring part number: LR008353. Coolant hose clips part number: 1316152.
- PTFE oil seal must be installed absolutely dry and seated 1 mm under flush; requires 4-hour cure before oil contact.
- Oil pump bolts torque: 10 Nm. Timing belt cover screws: 10 Nm. Timing belt tensioner: 26 Nm. Water pump: 12-25 Nm. Crankshaft pulley wheel: 25 Nm. Cooling fan pulley: 46 Nm.
- Gates tensioner is not recommended for this application due to undersized Allen key interface that strips before reaching torque.
- NTN bearing pulley wheels are recommended for their quality and longevity.
- Denso alternator at 240,000 km still had serviceable brushes and perfect bearings.
- Oil pressure at 2000 RPM under operating temp improved from 1.8 bar (before) to higher reading (after) with new pump.
- No metallic debris found in oil filter -- engine internals in good condition.
- Always apply anti-seize to the cooling fan hub nut.
- Check for plastic shroud rubbing against the coolant outlet when covers are removed; this can crack the coolant outlet over time.
