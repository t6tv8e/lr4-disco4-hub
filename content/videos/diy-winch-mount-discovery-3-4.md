## Summary

Christian and Vera demonstrate the full process of designing, fabricating, and installing a custom hidden winch mount bracket on a Discovery 3. The bracket is made from quarter-inch steel plate with 2-inch thick-wall tubing, designed as laser-cut puzzle pieces that can be assembled without complex bending equipment. A key design advantage is mounting the winch controller next to the winch using the original cable, eliminating the need to relocate the controller under the hood and enabling the use of a low-cost winch. The bracket includes a reinforcement gusset mounting to the front tow loop and a relocation bracket for the air suspension valve pack. The original bumper is cut in a defined area and bolted back in place, keeping the exterior largely stock. The winch clutch actuator is modified with a hex bolt for Allen wrench access from above. Total material cost is well under a few hundred euros: the winch cost 370 EUR (Chinese 12,000 lb unit with steel cable), laser-cut pieces cost 142 EUR (next-day), and the project took approximately 3 days including filming.

## Notes

- DXF files for laser cutting available by emailing Vera; donations accepted but not required
- Laser cutting cost: approximately 100-120 USD anywhere; 142 EUR with next-day service
- Winch: 370 EUR Chinese 12,000 lb unit with steel cable ("12,000 minus the Chinese factor")
- Recommended steel grade: higher than mild steel (formerly called ST50), quarter-inch plate
- All weld joints need 30-45 degree chamfer to at least two-thirds depth on both sides
- Laser holes in bracket prevent weld beads from interfering with bolt heads on cross member
- Correct tubing was 50mm (2-inch) thick-wall; 55mm tube was cut open and welded back to size as workaround
- Front bumper mounting holes can be drilled from M8 to M10 (10.5mm) or kept M8 with 10.9/12.8 grade bolts
- Front recovery loop is hidden under a sheet metal panel below the bumper
- Cooling fan has left-hand thread and requires a special tool for removal
- Block intake with mesh during installation to prevent dropped bolts entering engine
- German TUV requires nothing protruding from front; hinged magnetic licence plate planned to conceal winch opening
- Remaining work: 12mm steel cover plate (doubles as replacement recovery loop), final switch mounting
