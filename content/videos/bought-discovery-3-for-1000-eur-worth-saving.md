## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME acquire a surrendered 2005 Land Rover Discovery 3 S — the earliest and most robust TDV6 diesel variant — for approximately 1,000 EUR. The previous owner gave up after his workshop refused to work on it again following a failed electrical repair, and a failed TUV inspection (logged in Germany's national database) made the car unsaleable through normal channels.

The vehicle turned out to be exceptionally desirable: it is one of the very first diesel Discovery 3s with a technically EU3 engine (despite the EU4 sticker), meaning minimal emissions hardware and the original robust oil pump that survived to 160,000 km. It has a manual 6-speed ZF transmission with no leaks, a nearly new clutch, and the barebone S trim that makes it a future historic vehicle candidate.

Problems found during full disassembly included a leaking vacuum pump, seized rear brake caliper guide pins, heavily corroded suspension arms, rotted tank cover and heat shields, a mix of broken body mounts (some cracked from crooked installation at 120 EUR each), a parking brake bypassed with haywire cable work, and critically — a pinched wiring harness under a reinstalled brace that was likely the root cause of all the electrical issues the previous owner could never resolve.

The full body-off, engine-out restoration is estimated at 3,000-5,000 EUR in parts (including four new struts, new wheels, and suspension arms) plus approximately 15 hours of disassembly labor. The engine block and cylinder heads will not be opened unless the oil filter reveals metal chips. The completed vehicle is destined for their son Robin.

## Notes

- At 80-160 EUR/hour German workshop rates, repair costs that are trivial for DIY become economically irrational — rear suspension + brakes alone would be ~4,000 EUR at a shop
- A failed TUV inspection in Germany is permanently logged nationally — the car cannot be re-registered even with valid registration remaining
- The 2005 TDV6 with the original oil pump is described as the most robust variant — the host claims these are the only Land Rovers capable of 500,000 km
- Rust expands metal thickness by 8-10x — surface rust looks far worse than actual material loss underneath
- This is the first runner the LR TIME team has ever bought — all previous project cars had failed engines
- Tonga Green color, S trim without heated seats, dual-zone climate, or parking sensors
- Previous owner had spent significant money on new EGR valve, alternator, crossover pipe, brake hoses, and several body mounts — but could never resolve the electrical issues caused by the pinched cable
- The manual ZF 6-speed transmission was dry with no leaks — considered an excellent gearbox
- 15+ hours of skilled disassembly labor documented in this video
