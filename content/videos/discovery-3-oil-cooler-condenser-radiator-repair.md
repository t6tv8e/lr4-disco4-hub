## Summary

Christian performs a comprehensive multi-system repair on Fabian's Discovery 3 after it returned from a multi-month overland trip through Africa. The primary repair was an oil cooler that was leaking due to two bolts that had physically fallen out of the assembly into the engine valley — not a cracked cooler or failed gasket as initially assumed. The Nissens replacement oil cooler comes with both the main gasket and diesel cooler gasket, but the large gasket cannot be purchased separately despite what online searches suggest. An AC condenser was also replaced, with the caveat that Nissens-supplied O-rings do not fit the Discovery 3 and must be sourced separately. A cheap aftermarket coolant reservoir with an incompatible cap thread was replaced with a quality branded unit. Glow plugs were tested and found functional, belt drive components inspected, and the oil filter confirmed clean. The video provides extensive torque specifications and practical warnings about aftermarket part quality.

## Notes

- The AC system had only 345g of refrigerant remaining out of 550g required — it would have stalled soon
- The vehicle needed approximately 10 litres of coolant after the repairs
- The most time-consuming part was extracting the oil cooler — approximately 1 hour 10 minutes due to the evaporator and wiring harness obstructing access
- "What happens in the V stays in the V" — the engine valley accumulates debris over time
- Loctite 242 applied to oil cooler bolts specifically to prevent the bolt-falling-out failure mode from recurring
- An aluminium coolant crossover pipe replacement from China (16 EUR) was mentioned but feedback pending
