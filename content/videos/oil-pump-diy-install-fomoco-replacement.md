## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME replace a two-month-old FAI aftermarket oil pump on their Discovery 3 TDV6 with an OEM Ford Motor Company (FoMoCo) pump sourced through PSA/Stellantis at a significant cost saving over the Jaguar-branded equivalent. The FAI pump had caused measurably lower oil pressure and extended cold-start pressure build-up time to approximately 6 seconds with audible rattling — a dangerous condition for engine bearing health.

The video covers the full procedure including engine locking, timing belt removal, camshaft and crankshaft seal replacement (using custom precision tools for the 1mm under-flush specification), oil pump installation with high-temperature sealant, and a detailed post-installation priming method using a zip-tie valve trick to fill the pump galleries before the first start.

Results were immediate and dramatic: cold-start oil pressure build-up dropped from 6 seconds to approximately 2 seconds, idle oil pressure nearly doubled from 0.6 bar to above 1.0 bar, and all startup rattling was eliminated. The video provides strong evidence that cheap aftermarket oil pumps can cause serious, measurable harm to engine oil delivery.

## Notes

- The OEM PSA/Stellantis pump (part 101 G2) is identical to the Jaguar-branded pump but approximately 120 euros cheaper
- Also discovered during the job: a camshaft seal leak (repaired), a rear crankshaft seal leak (deferred to engine rebuild), a transfer case leak (deferred), and a loose MAP sensor bolt
- The serpentine belt showed cracks during inspection and was replaced as a precaution
- All torque values documented in detail throughout the video
