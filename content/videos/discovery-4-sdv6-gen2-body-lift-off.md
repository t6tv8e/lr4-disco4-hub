## Summary

LR TIME documents the complete body-off procedure on their Discovery 4 "Prince Charming" (180,000 km, SDV6 Gen2) to access the failed engine for rebuild. The video is a comprehensive reference for hidden connectors, correct preparation sequence, and common mistakes during body removal. Key findings include corroded ground points (a known cause of electrical gremlins), a dead AC system, and a mysterious factory noise dampener bag found in the body cavity. The team also corrects their previous brake disc installation direction after viewer feedback and covers a concurrent rear brake service with detailed torque specifications and parking brake adjustment procedure.

## Notes

- Parking brake release cable must be disconnected at transmission tunnel; requires slack management and cabin release first to prevent dangerous tension
- Tire pressure sensor connectors in wheel arches are expensive; disconnect carefully before removing liners
- A mysterious plastic bag found in body cavity is a factory noise/wind dampener, not evidence of previous repairs
- Chassis rear showed more corrosion than front but was manageable
- Vehicle had 180,000 km with neglected engine but surprisingly clean body and chassis
- New rebuild strategy: complete all work (brakes, fluids, filters, transmission service, corrosion treatment) before reassembly rather than install engine first
