## Summary

Mark from Lanoguard demonstrates a complete underbody rust treatment on his newly acquired 8-year-old Discovery 4 that had never been treated underneath. Working at a Land Rover specialist workshop (CR Allen & Sons in Peacehaven, Sussex) using their ramp, he walks through the full process.

The treatment follows a systematic approach: jet wash to clean, wire brush flaky rust areas, inspect for problem spots, apply warmed Lanoguard grease concentrate to high-risk areas (bolt holes, seams, belly pan), spray the entire underbody using the trigger spray with extension tube (which works upside-down), and finally inject cavities and drain holes with the pressure sprayer.

The D4's underbody was in reasonable condition for its age, with the rear and belly pan/fuel tank protector showing the most corrosion — noted as known rust-prone areas on Discoverys. The trigger spray with flexible extension tube is highlighted as the best DIY tool, offering airbrush-like precision that the larger 10L pro sprayer can't match.

## Notes

- Belly pan/fuel tank protector is the most rust-prone area on D4 underbodies
- The trigger spray with extension tube is the recommended DIY application method — works upside-down
- Warming the grease concentrate makes it much easier to apply
- Lanoguard is safe on aluminium, plastics, and rubber
- Mutton cloth from the accessory pack useful for both cleaning and wiping excess
- Channel has follow-up videos at 12 months and 3 years showing treatment longevity
- This is a branded/sponsored product demonstration but contains genuinely useful application technique information
