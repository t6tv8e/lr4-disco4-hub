## Summary

Christian and Vera replace a seized EGR valve and a worn water pump on their Discovery 3 with the 2.7 TDV6 engine. The video explains why EGR valves fail: they only activate under an extremely narrow set of operating conditions (specific temperature, no A/C, no headlights, etc.), so the valves stay closed almost permanently, accumulate heavy carbon deposits, and eventually seize. Pre-2007 vehicles without DPF will not even throw a check engine light for the failure. The replacement is genuinely difficult due to extremely limited bolt access behind coolant pipes and A/C lines. A small angled impulse ratchet proved essential. The water pump was replaced opportunistically while the serpentine belt was off, after bearing play and housing leakage were confirmed. A missing intake pipe bolt was also discovered, explaining a prior power loss issue.

## Notes

- EGR valves seize because the system only activates under very specific conditions (18-22C, 80C fuel, no A/C, no lights, no passengers). They stay closed almost always and accumulate carbon.
- Pre-2007 Discovery 3 without DPF will not throw a check engine light for EGR failure.
- Blocking EGR valves is illegal in most European countries.
- EGR valve cost: approximately 150 EUR per bank. If one fails, expect the other to follow, but the job is painful enough to justify waiting for confirmed failure.
- EGR valve-to-cooler retaining torque: 10 Nm (very light).
- Always disconnect the battery negative lead before removing connectors to prevent storing fault codes.
- Remove the 3rd fuse from the bottom before lifting the vehicle to prevent air suspension faults.
- A small angled ratchet tool (impulse shopping from Connex) was critical for accessing EGR bolts in tight spaces.
- Allen-head bolts can replace original fasteners for easier future access.
- Water pump replacement: 25 Nm for pulley bolts. Use silicone grease on O-ring.
- Coolant should be filled 30 mm above the full mark after reassembly.
- Post-repair: EGR valve position showed 90 degrees on both banks (previously 5% and 13%). Cleaning cycle audible on engine shutoff.
- The diesel auxiliary heater (northern hemisphere only) activates below 6C and is prone to clogging and fuel delivery issues.
