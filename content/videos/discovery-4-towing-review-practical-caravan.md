## Summary

Practical Caravan tests the Discovery 4 3.0 TDV6 as a tow car, towing a Sterling Elite Searcher (a large twin-axle caravan). Key findings:

- **Acceleration**: 30-60 mph in 9.9 seconds with caravan attached — "enough oomph to tow just about anything"
- **Stability**: Nothing upsets its composure at speed; remarkably responsive in emergency swerve testing despite 2.5-tonne kerb weight
- **Comfort**: One of the most relaxing long-distance tow cars available; high driving position with commanding view
- **Practicality**: 150 kg nose weight limit (very generous); twin 7-pin electrics standard; 2,558L with seats folded; genuine 7-seat capability
- **Fuel Economy**: 20.7 mpg towing (UK combined route)
- **Value**: Expensive to buy and run, but strong resale values provide some compensation

The D4 is declared the "undisputed king of heavyweight tow cars" — having won Practical Caravan's Tow Car Awards almost every year since 2007.

## Notes

- The D4 had won tow car awards almost every year since 2007 at time of review
- 150 kg nose weight is exceptionally high — many tow cars are limited to 75-100 kg
- Twin 7-pin electrics supplied as standard is unusual for the era
- Each row of seats sits higher than the row in front — stadium seating gives all passengers a good view
- Running costs described as "steep" but offset by strong residual values
