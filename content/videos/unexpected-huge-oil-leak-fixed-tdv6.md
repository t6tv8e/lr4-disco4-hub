## Summary

Fabian's 2009 Discovery 3, which had its engine fully rebuilt in 2021 using an aftermarket Fai oil pump, develops a significant oil leak just before a major overland expedition through Spain and Africa. The leak is traced to the failed Fai oil pump seal, which was visibly flat and degraded after only two years compared to an OEM seal still serviceable after 50,000 km. The full-day repair includes oil pump replacement, front crankshaft seal replacement with an Elring Teflon seal (installed completely dry, seated 1mm under flush), and timing belt reassembly. The video provides detailed torque specs and warns strongly against using cheap aftermarket parts in critical engine rebuilds.

## Notes

- Aftermarket Fai oil pump seals fail dramatically sooner than OEM -- the size difference between a failed Fai seal and a used OEM seal was visually stark
- Discovery 3 versus Discovery 4 practical difference: removing the underbody protection shield takes approximately 10 minutes on D3 versus approximately 3 hours on D4
- The engine had been fully rebuilt including line boring the block at home and fitting new pistons
- A non-OEM trigger wheel caused a documented penalty in a previous video
- Test drive completed successfully with no visible leaks on final inspection
