## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME fit General Grabber AT3 all-terrain tires (255/60 R18), 25mm wheel spacers, and Brembo front brake discs and pads on their Discovery 3. The bulk of the video focuses on the German TUV (vehicle inspection) requirements for making the wheel spacer and tire combination street legal — a process involving 13 specific regulatory obligations that must all be met simultaneously.

Key TUV requirements include: wheel arch extensions (15mm front, 5mm rear), a minimum of 7.5 thread revolutions of wheel nut engagement, the wheel arch must cover the tire within a 30-degree forward / 50-degree backward zone with overhang calculated as tire width times 1.04, and the air suspension must remain in place (coil conversion invalidates the approval). Notably, 265mm tires are not approved for the Discovery 3/4 stock rims — Land Rover's own type approval only covers up to 255mm.

After approximately two weeks of driving, the tires provide firmer steering feel than winter tires, equivalent wet performance, and are noisier only at highway speeds. The 255/60 R18 size with AT tread is described as an excellent compromise for European mixed-use driving.

## Notes

- The TUV assessment document on the spacers is necessary but not sufficient — spacers must be physically inspected by TUV and registered on the vehicle title
- 265mm tires are not TUV-approved for Discovery 3/4 stock rims regardless of what physically fits
- All-terrain tires rated M+S with snowflake symbol are legally compliant for German winter use
- Tires speed-rated to 210 km/h vs the vehicle's 180 km/h top speed — adequate headroom
- Brembo front brake replacement done simultaneously to avoid undermining the tire upgrade with warped discs
- The 25mm channel lock pliers discussion is a recurring light-hearted element
