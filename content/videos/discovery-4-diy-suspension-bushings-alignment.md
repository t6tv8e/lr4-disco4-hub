## Summary

LR TIME replaces worn lower front suspension arm bushings on a 2016 Discovery 4 EU6 and performs a complete DIY camber and toe alignment. The bushings were visibly torn and causing a clunking noise. Lemforder aftermarket bushings were used as replacements. The video demonstrates that pressing out old bushings is far more difficult than online advice suggests, requiring custom tools because standard arbor presses cannot accommodate the arm geometry. After bushing installation, camber is set using a bubble level gauge zeroed on a precision-plastered wall, targeting -0.5 degrees within the plus/minus 0.75 degree tolerance. Toe is measured using a PVC pipe and fishing rope string line system at axle height, with calculations performed by a custom Excel spreadsheet. Suspension arm bolts are torqued to 275 Nm and shock absorber bolts to 300 Nm, both tightened at the correct ride height of 466 mm. The repair took approximately 6.5 hours across two days. The test drive confirmed the clunking noise was eliminated and the steering wheel tracked straight. The repair did not resolve a separate air suspension algorithm fault that required further investigation.

## Notes

- Lemforder and Meyle are recommended bushing brands; avoid cheap complete aftermarket arms that may lack alignment adjustment range
- Custom pressing tools were necessary; online claims of easy press-out are misleading for this application
- Do not strike near the DPF when hammering out stuck components
- Camber gauge must be zeroed on a known vertical surface before use
- Discovery 4 front camber spec differs slightly left to right but both sides can be set within the same target
- Toe calculator spreadsheet available on LR TIME Patreon
- Tighten bolts at ride height to avoid pre-loading rubber bushings incorrectly
- Ball joint bolts are the exception: they do not need ride-height tightening
- Separate air suspension issue (GAP tool reports algorithm-based failure) was not resolved by this repair
