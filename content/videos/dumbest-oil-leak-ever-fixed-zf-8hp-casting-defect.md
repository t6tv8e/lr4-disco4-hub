## Summary

After a Land Rover dealership charged the previous owner 2,492 euro for a transmission oil pan replacement and cleaning that failed to address the actual root cause, Christian traces a persistent oil leak to a known factory casting defect in the ZF 8HP70 transmission housing. A 2019 Land Rover Technical Service Bulletin identifies approximately 200,000 affected transmissions across specific VIN ranges. The official Land Rover repair uses Defcon epoxy putty (200 euro) and a custom filler piece (230 euro), but testing shows the Defcon putty runs and drips before curing and only resists 160C. Instead, JB Weld (45 euro, temperature resistant to 290C) is used with a machined aluminum plug to seal the cavity. The repair is confirmed successful after 4-5 days with the area completely dry.

## Notes

- The dealership, as a licensed Land Rover dealer, had access to the 2019 TSB and should have cross-referenced the VIN -- they did not
- The previous owner likely sold the car at a loss and may have switched brands entirely after the failed repair
- JB Weld outperformed Land Rover's own recommended Defcon putty at roughly one-tenth the cost
- Any ZF 8HP70 owner with a transmission oil leak should check their VIN against the 2019 TSB before paying for a generic oil pan replacement
- Once enough transmission oil is lost, the 8HP stops shifting correctly, explaining jerky downshifts
