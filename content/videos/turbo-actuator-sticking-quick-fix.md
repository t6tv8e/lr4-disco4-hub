## Summary

SilverFoxBull demonstrates a quick roadside fix for the common "Engine System Fault" warning on Discovery 3 and 4 diesel models caused by a sticking turbo actuator. The actuator control arm seizes due to rust and corrosion, preventing proper turbo operation and triggering limp mode.

The fix requires only basic tools: WD-40 with a flexible nozzle, a hammer, 10mm socket, magnetic pickup tool, and the metal bar from the vehicle's jack kit. Access is through the left wheel arch — remove three 10mm bolts holding the arch liner to expose the turbo actuator (visible as a shiny ball-shaped component). Use the magnetic tool to grab and move the control arm while spraying WD-40, tapping gently to free it. Cycling the ignition on/off triggers the actuator's self-test, which helps work it loose.

## Notes

- This is one of the most common causes of "Engine System Fault" on D3/D4 diesels
- Same 2.7 TDV6 and 3.0 SDV6 turbo arrangement — fix applies to both
- Can be performed roadside with tools already in the vehicle
- If the actuator cannot be freed, replacement may be needed (~£80 for the part)
