## Summary

Christian and Vera methodically reassemble a Discovery 4 SDV6 Gen 2 3.0L diesel engine from the crankshaft up. The video documents the complete assembly process with torque specifications throughout. Bank-specific head gaskets are installed (4-tooth for Bank 2, 3-tooth for Bank 1) with a five-stage torque sequence reaching 90+90 degrees angle torque. The oil pump is inspected for chips, primed with 5W-40 oil, and its PTFE front seal is installed dry using a custom seating tool, requiring a 4-hour cure time.

A Chinese aftermarket trigger wheel installation tool fails in two dimensions, requiring manual correction to achieve the specified 3.66 mm depth. The timing belt installation includes the Gen 2-specific timed high-pressure fuel pump, aligned using two white marks on the belt matched to pulley wheel markings. All six glow plugs are tested before installation. Fuel injectors are installed with new 1.5 mm sealing rings and torqued in stages.

## Notes

- Part of the LR TIME SDV6 engine rebuild series (S4-Ep17)
- Camshaft end float spec: 0.05-0.15 mm; measured 0.1 mm
- Camshaft bearing cap torque: 1 Nm first pass, then 11 Nm final
- Hydraulic lifters selectively replaced based on wear feel — not wholesale replacement
- Valve cover sealant applied at corners (known leak points); bolts torqued to 10 Nm
- Oil sensor hold-down bracket installed preventively — not fitted at factory
- Crow-foot adapter must be positioned at ~90 degrees to torque wrench to avoid reading error
