## Summary

A Condor Prestige presenter reviews a 2014 facelift Discovery 4 SE Tech SDV6 as a buying guide. He systematically covers trim level differences (GS lacks a media screen and cruise control; SE Tech adds those plus reversing camera, heated seats, and xenons; HSE adds electric memory seats, armrest, three sunroofs, and standard Meridian sound system). The 2014 facelift is identified by LED filaments in the headlights and black rear light surrounds.

A key financial finding: post-2014 models with the Eco stop-start system pay £360/year road tax vs £615 for pre-2014 — saving £255 annually. The 8-speed ZF gearbox paired with the 255 bhp SDV6 is praised for smooth, efficient driving at approximately 29-30 MPG.

On reliability, the presenter takes a measured approach: "they're by no means bulletproof, but if you find one with a really good service history... you're in for a winner." He identifies matching branded tires as a telltale sign of a well-maintained example and lists front lower arms, aircon compressor, and discs/pads as common service items.

## Notes

- The presenter has filmed multiple Land Rover reviews including a GS model Discovery 4 — references comparing the two
- Condor Prestige appears to be a used car dealer — professional knowledge of market values and specifications
- The Eco stop-start button is on the dashboard — most owners disable it immediately
- No armrest on SE Tech is specifically called out as a miss compared to HSE
- The SDV6 shares its engine block with the Range Rover Sport L494 but at a lower power rating (255 vs 292/306 bhp)
