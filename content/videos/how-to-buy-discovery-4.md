## Summary

LR TIME documents the complete process of selling their previous Discovery 4 and buying a replacement 2015 HSE D4 with 108,000 km. The video serves as a practical buying guide, demonstrating how to use a diagnostic scan as a negotiation weapon (revealing windshield sensor and auxiliary battery faults), how to evaluate test drive findings (transmission shudder, non-functioning distance assist and eco mode), and how to leverage known but fixable issues to negotiate down from the 29,900 euro asking price. The most valuable takeaway is that the transmission shudder -- which the selling dealer blamed on suspension bushings -- was fixed instantly and for free by resetting the ZF 8HP adaptation values, something the Land Rover dealer failed to do after changing the transmission oil. The video also reveals that the previous owner paid 3,000 euros for a transmission oil leak repair that was never actually fixed, and documents post-purchase work including auxiliary battery replacement (with a broken connector from workshop damage), rear bumper repair, wiper blades, and DAB+ radio installation.

## Notes

- The vehicle was purchased from a used car dealership that had taken it in on trade and regretted it due to the difficult service interactions with Land Rover.
- 20-inch wheels on the D4 cause noticeably harsher ride quality compared to smaller wheel options.
- License plates mounted with Velcro for a cleaner appearance (no bolt holes or plastic frames).
- Varta batteries criticized for changing their date code system to be undecipherable, making it impossible to verify manufacturing date -- channel switched to Exide.
- The 2015 D4 has features the earlier model lacked: adaptive cruise control, auto high beam assist, cornering lights, electric folding steering column.
- Custom locking wheel nut socket fabricated by machining from a hot glue gun casting of the original key pattern.
