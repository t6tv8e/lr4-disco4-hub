## Summary

Alan from The Overland Legend provides a 2-year follow-up on his Discovery 3 at 317,000 km (the first report was at 9 years/~270,000 km). While this is a Discovery 3, the air suspension system, coolant system, and many maintenance aspects are shared with the Discovery 4.

Key issues covered: a false coolant level warning caused by a swollen float in the reservoir sensor (required full bottle replacement, then recurred a year later as a genuine slow leak), tire flat spots from not balancing/aligning alongside rotations, and an upgrade to 5W-40 oil for the high-mileage engine. He also documents the air suspension system behaviour and his attempts to understand how the suspension modes and height settings work.

## Notes

- The presenter is based in South Africa and regularly drives to Mozambique — real-world long-distance use case
