## Summary

Christian from LR TIME performs a ZF 8HP automatic transmission oil change on a Discovery 4 at 90,000 km, following the community-famous "Robbie's Guide" from disco3.co.uk alongside the official ZF service manual. This is an entertainingly chaotic but thoroughly informative tutorial.

### Critical Points

1. **Level the vehicle precisely** — the ZF 8HP fluid level is set by overflow, so any tilt means wrong quantity
2. **Always open the fill plug before the drain plug** — if the fill plug is seized, you'll have an empty gearbox with no way to refill
3. **Drain plug: 12 Nm; Fill plug: 30 Nm** — different torques for the two plugs
4. **Two-person job during the fill procedure** — one shifts gears while the other pumps fluid
5. **Temperature-dependent** — the fluid level check must be done at 30-50°C (per ZF) or ~30°C (per Robbie's Guide)

### Fill Procedure (The Tricky Part)

The ZF 8HP uses a specific fill-and-prime protocol: fill until overflow, then start the engine and shift through all gears while continuing to pump fluid. Rev to 2,000 RPM in neutral for 30 seconds to prime the torque converter and internal pump. Warm to operating temperature, cool back down, then do a final level check by draining to a "fine thread" from the fill port.

### Oil Pan Decision

At 90,000 km with clean-looking fluid, they decided NOT to change the oil pan/filter — the pan requires raising the transmission and removing a crossmember, and the oil was still in good condition. Deferred to 130,000 km or next transmission removal. The filter is integrated into the oil pan, so changing the filter means changing the entire pan assembly.

## Notes

- ZF recommends 80,000 km change interval; Land Rover says "lifetime" — follow ZF's advice
- The oil should be green when fresh; dark/black = severely overdue
- Original ZF LifeGuard Fluid 8 costs ~€20/litre (5L needed = ~€100)
- Cheaper alternatives available, saving ~€100 — your call on the risk
- Hand pump from Amazon (~€9) works fine for filling
- Remember to disable air suspension (fuse) before working underneath
- Reset adaptation values after oil change — transmission needs to re-learn shift points
- The 3.0 SDV6 has 600 Nm of torque — "if you want power, get the 3-litre machine"
