## Summary

This video documents the diagnosis and repair of a failed drive belt tensioner on a Discovery 4 SDV6 3.0L with approximately 200,000 miles. The vehicle was making a terrible noise from the front of the engine. Upon inspection, the idler bearing on the tensioner was completely destroyed -- worn flat and rubbing against the engine block, creating red dust everywhere and causing the belt to overhang its pulleys.

The repair involves removing the engine cover, air intake piping, the viscous fan (which requires a specialist tool and has a reverse/left-hand thread), and the fan cowling to access the tensioner. The tensioner is held by a single 15mm bolt and locates via two tabs. The video also covers removing and rerouting the serpentine belt using a 3/8-inch drive on the tensioner to release tension, and reassembly. The presenters note differences between the 3.0 TDV6 and 2.7 TDV6 belt setups.
