## Summary

A quick video showing how to replace the engine start/stop button on the Land Rover Discovery 4. The common issue is that the lettering on the button becomes smudgy and worn over time, particularly around the "engine" text where your finger presses repeatedly. The replacement is extremely simple and requires no tools beyond a plastic trim removal tool.

The button is held in place by spring clips and simply pops out from the dash surround. The electrical connector pulls straight off with no clip to release. The new button can only be installed one way, and it clicks into a locating groove at the top. No battery disconnection is required. The entire job takes less than a minute.
