## Summary

Christian and Fabian demonstrate how to replace the front differential pinion seal on a Discovery 3 in approximately 4 hours without removing the entire differential, driveshafts, prop shaft, and cross member -- contrary to Land Rover's official classification of this as a non-serviceable, differential-out operation. The critical engineering challenge is preserving the crush washer bearing preload: the pinion nut is not torqued to a specification but tightened until rotating the pinion requires 4-7 Nm of friction torque. Over-tightening compresses the crush washer further and causes differential failure within approximately 2 weeks. The solution is to meticulously mark the nut, flange, and pinion stem alignment before disassembly and use a depth gauge measurement to verify exact restoration. A custom 74mm press tool is machined to drive the new seal in squarely and act as a depth stop.

## Notes

- Land Rover's "non-serviceable" classification forces owners into an unnecessarily expensive full differential replacement
- The actual engineering risk (altered bearing preload) is fully manageable with careful marking and measurement
- Driveshaft imbalance is factory-matched to axle imbalance -- mark orientation before removal or expect vibration
- Differential was pressurized when opened, indicating a blocked vent hose (separate issue that can push oil past seals)
- Front differential oil is exactly 0.61 liters, filled by quantity not level
- Disable air suspension before starting work to prevent vehicle height changes
- New patch-lock nuts required for driveshaft bolts; old ones cannot be reused
