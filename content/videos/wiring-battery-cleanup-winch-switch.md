## Summary

LR TIME documents the installation of a custom waterproof winch on/off switch and a full battery wiring cleanup on their Discovery. The winch switch was fabricated from an aluminum block housing sealed with silicone glue (no suitable off-the-shelf waterproof housing existed) and mounted behind the front grille. When the cable turned out too short, a custom battery pole bolt was fabricated by drilling and tapping an M6 thread directly into the battery pole at 10 Nm torque.

The battery wiring cleanup addresses the common problem of messy aftermarket accessory wiring. Key lessons include using quality crimp terminals (Thomas and Betts / Stakon — cheap Chinese alternatives fail to hold), copper/brass washers for proper electrical contact, battery pole grease, and labeling all wires to avoid the confusion captured in the running joke: "red is black and plus is minus."

The video closes with two practical maintenance tips: the TDV6's cooling fan is belt-driven and stops immediately when the engine turns off (unlike electric fans that run post-shutdown), making proper cool-down after hard use important; and switching A/C to heat for 30 seconds before engine shutdown dries the air ducts and prevents mold/condensation buildup.

## Notes

- Custom waterproof switch housing was fabricated because no off-the-shelf option existed — sealed with silicone glue
- Battery pole drilling: M6 thread, 10 Nm torque, sharpen drill bit properly before use
- Quality crimp terminals (Thomas and Betts / Stakon) are essential — Chinese alternatives do not hold
- Soldering is generally superior to crimping but quality crimping is a valid alternative
- Label all wires with colored tie wraps to avoid polarity confusion
- Use shrink tubing (not just tie wraps) for securing and insulating connections
- TDV6 cooling fan is belt-driven — it stops the moment the engine turns off, unlike Toyota electric fans that continue running
- Switch A/C to heat for 30 seconds before shutdown to dry air ducts and prevent mold
- Winch remote: keep wireless remote stored safely, carry spare batteries
