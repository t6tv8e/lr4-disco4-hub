## Summary

This video demonstrates how to fit an aftermarket manual release mechanism for the upper tailgate on a Discovery 3/4. The product, made by Land Rover Passion (LRP), is a cast aluminum lever that provides a manual backup to electrically release the upper tailgate from inside the vehicle. The presenter explains the common problem: when the electric actuator fails, the upper tailgate cannot be opened, and the repair is inaccessible because the trim panel is held in place by a plastic strip that can only be removed when the tailgate is open — a catch-22 situation.

The installation involves removing the lower tailgate interior trim panel, disconnecting the gas strut stays, removing the subwoofer, cutting the subwoofer mounting bracket, drilling holes through the trim panel using a template, cutting a hole in the carpet for the release lever, routing the cable to the actuator mechanism, and reassembling everything.
