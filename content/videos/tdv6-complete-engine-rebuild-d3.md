## Summary

Christian and Vera complete the final episode of Fabian's Discovery 3 2.7L TDV6 engine rebuild. The video covers timing belt installation using a three-point locking method (crankshaft timing pin, flywheel lock, camshaft pins) with a socket retention trick to keep the belt seated during tensioning. A freshly rebuilt turbocharger is fitted along with the EGR valve assembly (original units retained to avoid unnecessary cost). High-pressure fuel line unions are torqued in two stages (15 Nm then 35 Nm with crow foot at 90 degrees), and fuel injectors are reinstalled after leak testing and balance checking.

Engine installation proved the most challenging phase, requiring four attempts and five hours. A custom centre-of-gravity rig was welded to the hoist to achieve the correct engine angle (~9 degrees). Every sensor, bracket, and connector was paint-marked for future removal reference. Oil galleries were pre-primed overnight through the pressure sensor port. The first start attempt failed due to no oil pressure, but after battery charging and additional priming, the second attempt achieved nearly 4 bar at idle. The engine was described as sounding "like a new machine."

## Notes

- Part of the LR TIME engine rebuild series for Fabian's Discovery 3
- Budget repair philosophy: no unnecessary parts replaced — original water pump, EGR valves, and fuel lines retained
- Grade 10.6 bolts used for critical fasteners (vs common 8.8 grade)
- Fuel pump is NOT timed on the Discovery 3 2.7L (unlike the 3.0L SDV6 where it IS timed)
- Sump installation requires sealant at two spots where oil pump meets engine block
- High-pressure fuel line unions reused after inspection despite manufacturer single-use designation
- Custom 74.2 mm crankshaft stop tool fabricated for timing
