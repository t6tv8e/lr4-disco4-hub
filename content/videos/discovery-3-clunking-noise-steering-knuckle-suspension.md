## Summary

Christian methodically diagnoses a clunking noise on the Discovery 3 at 295,000 km. Initial inspection with a dial indicator revealed 0.2 mm of play in both front steering knuckles, which were replaced with Moya aftermarket units. A worn upper control arm with 12 mm of lateral play was also identified. However, the actual root cause turned out to be a loose bolt on the upper suspension arm bracket — left untightened during a previous rebuild despite a custom wrench having been fabricated specifically for it. The steering rack was confirmed in perfect condition at 295,000 km, contrary to the common assumption that it would be the source of the noise. The video also covers a Xenon headlight bulb upgrade to Osram 6200K units with +150% brightness.

## Notes

- Key diagnostic lesson: always check bolt torque on previously worked areas before condemning and replacing parts
- Grease imprints on brackets are a valuable diagnostic clue for detecting movement/slippage
- The steering rack on a well-maintained Discovery 3 can last well beyond 295,000 km
- Sahr brand suspension bushings degraded within weeks of initial installation

- GAP tool used to deflate all corners for suspension inspection on the lift
