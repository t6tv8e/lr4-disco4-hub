## Summary

ShowCare presents a methodical 5-step pre-purchase inspection framework for the LR3/LR4, designed to help buyers build a "case file" before paying for a professional mechanic's inspection. Each step covers specific LR3/LR4 common issues: exterior (height sensors, air suspension compressor, door handle fading, headlight yellowing), engine (crankshaft bearing carrier leaks, oil condition checks, exhaust smoke analysis), interior (heater blend door motor, wear as mileage indicator), test drive, and mileage verification.

A key insight: "when people run out of honesty they become horrible — they decrease mileage without ever restoring life to the car." The video emphasises that interior wear patterns, headlight yellowing degree, and service record consistency are better mileage indicators than the odometer alone. The phone-camera technique for inspecting wheel arch components is practical advice for non-mechanics.

## Notes

- "I never understood why people used to tell me never to go near a Land Rover LR4 until I saw this" — referring to the excessive bolt torque on the wheel rims, suggesting a vehicle that has been heavily worked on
- Air suspension bags shown being raised and lowered — demonstrating the test that every buyer should perform
- The inspection framework is generic enough to apply to any used vehicle but highlights LR3/LR4-specific issues at each step
- Cold start inspection is specifically stressed — sellers who have the car warmed up before viewing are potentially hiding engine noise issues
- No dipstick on later models means oil level can only be checked via dashboard — important to know before buying
