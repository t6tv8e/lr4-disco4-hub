## Summary

LR TIME tours a Discovery 4 overland build created by a visitor from Hamburg. The build emphasises clever, low-cost solutions over expensive off-the-shelf products. The bike rack mounts to the spare wheel carrier rather than the trailer hitch, preserving full access to the rear slide-out system. Storage uses repurposed Mercedes-Benz industrial transport boxes with custom-fabricated kitchen setup including fold-down legs, windscreen, cooker, and silverware drawer. An Engel compressor fridge sits on dedicated slide rails separate from the main cargo drawer. Water comes from a 26-litre Front Runner tank with electric pump mounted in the spare wheel compartment. Power is supplied by an auxiliary battery managed by a Votronic charger with MPPT solar input from a foldable panel. Sleeping options include a James Baroud XXL hardshell rooftop tent (2.2 x 2.6 m) and a rear sleeping platform on homemade slide rails. The vehicle also has a winch, snorkel, and maximum-legal BFGoodrich off-road tyres for European roads.

## Notes

- All modifications prioritise simplicity and low cost; most built with basic workshop tools
- Bike rack base carrier sourced from Discovery Parts; extended with custom weldment and 20-euro Amazon hook
- Mercedes-Benz boxes originally used for internal factory part transport; available on eBay
- Kitchen fold-down legs salvaged from a 20-euro camping table; height adjustable
- Fridge space separated from drawer space by a divider wall
- Cargo net used to secure gear in the rear
- Spare tyre is an original-size tyre since aftermarket tyres would not fit underneath
- Build designed for a family of four
- Accessing the spare tyre winch requires removing the slide-out, fridge, and water tank cover
