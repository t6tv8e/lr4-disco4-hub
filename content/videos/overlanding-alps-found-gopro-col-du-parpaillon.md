## Summary

Christian and Vera drive from Frankfurt through Switzerland to the Western Alps for an overlanding trip. In Switzerland, the car throws a brake lamp switch fault (C1A96-62), causing limp mode and air suspension lowering — diagnosed as contact bounce with 200 ms offset between the two internal switch contacts, not an actual failure. On Col du Parpaillon at 2,200 metres, a clicking noise is identified as tripod joint (inner CV joint) play on both driveshafts, estimated to have approximately 1,000 km of life remaining as long as the boots are intact. Vera finds a still-powered GoPro on the pass during a trail run, later identified as belonging to a French gravel cyclist. The couple wild-camps in the valley and witnesses a helicopter rescue for a motorcycle accident on the mountain road.

## Notes

- Brake lamp switch fault C1A96-62: contact bounce, not a failed switch — clear with IID tool
- Tripod joint (inner CV) play on both sides: monitor boot integrity, approximately 1,000 km remaining
- Switzerland roaming: approximately 1 EUR/MB; turn off before border
- Swiss vignette: approximately 40 EUR
- French wild camping: technically illegal but tolerated when discreet
- Christian gets 10-15% better fuel economy than Vera driving the same Discovery
- Airing down performed before off-road pass sections
- Tunnel du Pont: single-lane mountain tunnel, check for oncoming headlights before entering
