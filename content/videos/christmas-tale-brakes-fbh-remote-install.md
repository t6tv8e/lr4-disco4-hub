## Summary

This LR TIME Christmas episode is a winter garage vlog covering multiple topics. The main Discovery 3 work includes replacing front brake discs (Brembo) and pads — measured at 27.7mm disc thickness (still in spec) and approximately 4mm pad thickness with significant edge buildup, both replaced proactively. A brake pad wear sensor switch is also replaced, with the reminder to do this every time pads are changed. The wrong brake pads were initially ordered, highlighting the importance of verifying part numbers.

A notable upgrade is the Webasto fuel-burning heater (FBH) remote control installation: instead of paying Land Rover's EUR 200+ for their module, a compatible Ford Mondeo receiver was sourced for EUR 115 with a EUR 9 antenna, saving over EUR 100. The specific receiver version must be verified for compatibility, and the feature must be enabled in the vehicle software after installation.

The video also includes practical buying advice: the Discovery 3 "death zone" is 160,000-200,000 km mileage, where major failures are most likely. Vehicles above 200,000 km can actually be safer purchases if maintenance is documented. An SKF pulley wheel purchase (for the Mercedes) revealed that SKF packaging may contain bearings "purchased" rather than manufactured by SKF — a warning applicable to any brand-name parts purchase.

## Notes

- The Webasto FBH remote hack using a Ford Mondeo receiver is a significant cost savings — but requires knowing the exact compatible version
- "Death zone" of 160,000-200,000 km provides a practical buying framework for used D3/D4 shoppers
- SKF's "manufactured or purchased by SKF" labelling means premium brand packaging may contain generic Chinese bearings
- Winter tyre purchase for the secondary vehicle: EUR 460 including TPMS sensors (used)
- Always bring a diagnostic tool when inspecting a used Discovery — never buy blind
