## Summary

Christian presents a detailed decision framework for which engine components to replace versus retain during a budget SDV6 rebuild at 64,000 km. Using the bathtub failure distribution from reliability engineering, he argues that at 64,000 km the engine has just exited the infant mortality phase and most components are in their most reliable period. Replacing them unnecessarily wastes money and actively introduces new failure risk through the wear-in process.

Key decisions: piston rings retained (perfect condition, new rings would introduce wear-in risk to honing grooves), timing chain retained (estimated 200,000 km remaining life), turbochargers retained, water pump retained (replaceable in 1.5 hours later), EGR valves retained. All seals and O-rings are replaced, along with head gaskets, flywheel bolts, and the crankshaft position sensor ring (OEM only).

The video includes a cautionary tale about aftermarket connecting rods from Brake Part UK: all three ordered were out of Ford specification, with one being 30 grams overweight due to an unmachined casting burr. Only one was marginally usable. OEM rods at 64,000 km measured perfectly within spec, reinforcing the preference for OEM components.
