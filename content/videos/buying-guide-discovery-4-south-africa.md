## Summary

A presenter from Cars.co.za shares his personal experience buying a Discovery 4 in South Africa. He reveals what he calls a "cheat code" — the XS model, a stripped-down off-road variant released by Land Rover South Africa in 2013. The XS removes the two biggest reliability concerns: air suspension (replaced with coil springs) and the DPF. It also omits sunroofs and heated seats, but retains air conditioning, power steering, leather seats, and Bluetooth. He paid R290,000 for his XS and considers it exceptional value. The video emphasises checking for signs of off-road abuse (wheel condition, bush-rash scratches) and notes that XS models are becoming rare and sought after, with only 3 of 287 listed Discos being XS spec at time of filming.

## Notes

- The XS is specific to the South African market — international buyers won't find this spec
- For non-XS buyers, air suspension can be converted to coil springs but it's described as complicated and expensive
- The presenter is not a mechanic — this is a personal buying experience rather than a technical inspection guide
