## Summary

LR TIME documents a 2-day repair to fix a factory assembly error on their Discovery 4 SDV6. The oil level sensor connector was never properly clicked in at the Land Rover plant, causing fault codes P250A (oil level sensor circuit) and oil temperature sensor circuit errors. The oil level display showed "not available" instead of a reading. Despite the root cause being a simple unclipped connector, the sensor's location inside the oil sump requires removing the half shafts, front differential, starter motor, and sump to access it -- a 6-7 hour disassembly just to reach the sensor. During removal the oil pickup pipe broke at a fragile spot, requiring a custom replacement machined from brass and copper. The new sensor clicked in properly and immediately showed a correct reading. Land Rover later released a retention bracket for this connector, confirming the issue was widespread. A dealer would charge approximately 1,500 pounds for this repair, almost entirely labour cost for what amounts to clicking in a connector.

## Notes

- Root cause was purely an assembly error: the Deutsch connector was never clicked into its housing at the factory
- Oil was found inside the disconnected connector housing
- Land Rover later released a dedicated bracket to hold the sensor connector, confirming this was a known issue
- The new connector clicks firmly and would never come loose on its own
- Oil pickup pipe has a fragile cast section; replacement machined from soft copper is more durable and flexible
- Pre-fill oil filter (both inner and outer sections) to prime the oil gallery before first start; tip credited to Broken Piston Garage on YouTube
- Front differential fill: 0.61 litres of 75W-90; torque to 29 Nm
- Sump gasket uses an L-ring with grey silicone sealant at four points (8 mm beads)
- Cycle ignition 2-3 times to bleed diesel system before first start
- Ensure battery terminal connections are tight to prevent intermittent electrical issues
- Service mode oil level display accessed by opening bonnet and door, entering service menu, selecting oil level, pressing cancel twice
