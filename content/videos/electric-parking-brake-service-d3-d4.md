## Summary

Christian and Vera walk through the complete removal, disassembly, inspection, and reinstallation of the electric parking brake (EPB) module on their Discovery 3. The critical first step is activating EPB service mode (ignition on, press brake, press EPB button down, remove key) then pulling fuse FL8 (30 amp) from the engine bay — the interior glove box fuse alone is not sufficient. Seized bolts are freed using WD-40 soaking and a 3/8-inch socket hammered onto rounded heads. A guide wire is run alongside the parking brake cable before removal to ensure identical routing on reinstall. The gearbox teardown reveals the most common finding: gears in good condition but old degraded grease. The 120 EUR replacement gear set purchased as a precaution was largely unnecessary — cleaning and regreasing was the actual fix. One cable requires exactly 5 turns on reinstall (hand-tighten only, per Britpart). Brake shoe adjustment follows a paint-mark method: tighten until the disc locks, mark one tooth, then back off exactly one revolution. Key torque specs are 115 Nm for caliper mounting bracket bolts, 35 Nm for caliper bolts, and 6 Nm for the adjuster wedge. A near-miss is caught where the emergency release cable bracket was rubbing against the driveshaft. The video emphasises that EPB modules are frequently avoided due to perceived complexity, but the actual failure is usually just degraded grease rather than worn gears.

## Notes

- Pull fuse FL8 (30 amp) from engine bay to fully disable EPB; the interior fuse is NOT sufficient
- Also pull the air suspension fuse as a precaution (standard practice)
- Leave battery connected unless welding
- Hammering a 3/8-inch socket onto rounded bolt heads is an effective technique for seized fasteners
- Run a guide wire alongside parking brake cables before removal to ensure correct routing on reinstall
- One-way clutch parts should be placed in a plastic bag during disassembly to prevent loss
- The tie-wrap holding the spring during installation MUST be cut before finalising — they forgot this on one side and had to partially disassemble
- After successful service, use the EPB daily to keep it in working order
- Apply anti-seize or high-temperature grease to all brake contact points
- Brake clip closed end must face the adjuster wheel
- Clean brake pads and disc with a clean cloth before final installation
