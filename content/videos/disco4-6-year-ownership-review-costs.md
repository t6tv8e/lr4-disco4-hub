## Summary

Dan from Tour Overland reviews his 6-year ownership experience with a 2010 Discovery 4 purchased in 2019 at approximately 100,000 miles with no warranty. The first three years were remarkably cheap at just £1,400 total (including £600 for Pirelli Scorpion All-Terrain tires), but years 4-6 saw costs escalate as the 12-14 year old vehicle hit known weak points. The biggest expenses were twin turbo replacement at £4,000 (requiring body-off-chassis work at ~150,000 miles), two inlet manifold replacements at ~£900 each (plastic manifolds crack from heat cycling), and an electronic parking brake module at £700. Total 6-year maintenance spend was approximately £11,000, averaging just under £2,000/year. One outlier year cost nearly £6,000 (turbos + manifold + misc). Fixed monthly costs including road tax (~£700/year), insurance (~£700/year), and averaged maintenance come to about £280/month excluding fuel. Fuel economy dropped from 33 MPG stock to 25-28 MPG after adding a Patriot aluminium roof rack, iKamper Sky Camp rooftop tent, and AT tires. The family of five used it for a two-month trip through France and Spain with no major issues. Dan considers it the best vehicle of the 25 he's owned, offering an unmatched combination of off-road capability, on-road comfort, family practicality, and luxury at a used-car price point. His advice: budget for one bad year that could cost £5,000+, replace both inlet manifolds at the same time, do simpler repairs yourself, and keep overlanding setups simple.

- Purchased to replace an Audi S4 Avant that couldn't fit three child car seats
