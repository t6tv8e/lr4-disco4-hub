## Summary

LR TIME presents five areas where the Discovery 3 outperforms the Discovery 4, challenging the assumption that the newer model is always better. First, reliability: the D4 uses the same core Lion V6 diesel engine but adds significantly more components (second turbocharger, DPF, more complex EGR valves) without addressing the fundamental reliability issues, making it statistically less reliable despite being newer and more expensive. Second, fuel consumption: the D4 SDV6 uses approximately one litre more per 100 km in real-world mixed driving. Third, launch behaviour: the ZF 8HP transmission is calibrated very softly, resulting in poor initial acceleration from standstill despite the engine's 600 Nm of torque. Fourth, looks: subjectively, the D3's older styling has more character and distinction than the D4's more generic modern appearance. Fifth, engine sound: the D4 has been engineered to be so quiet that the diesel engine loses its character, sounding like an unconvincing imitation of a petrol engine under load.

## Notes

- The D4 SDV6 engine is the same core Lion V6 as the D3 TDV6 with minor bore and stroke changes (2 mm more stroke)
- Bore and stroke changes were made to improve combustion efficiency and reduce emissions, not to improve reliability
- Oil pan was made a structural part of the engine to reduce harmonics and NVH
- Crankshaft failure reduction in D4 attributed to softer drivetrain absorbing shock loads, not to engineering improvements
- D4 SDV6 has 600 Nm torque vs D3 TDV6 at 450 Nm
- HP8 shifts so quietly it feels like a Mercedes S-Class, but this softness removes driving engagement
- Paddle shifters on HP8 are the only manual override; no centre console manual mode available
- Paddle shifters can be accidentally engaged when steering wheel is turned, causing unexpected downshifts
- D4 engine compartment is much more densely packaged, reducing accessibility for maintenance
- D3 with manual transmission has vastly superior launch feel
- D4 is significantly more expensive even on the used market; D3 offers equivalent reliability at lower cost
- V8 option was not discussed in depth; acknowledged as not significantly improved between D3 and D4
