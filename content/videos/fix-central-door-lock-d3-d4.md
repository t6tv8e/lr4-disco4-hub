## Summary

LR TIME repairs a faulty rear door lock on a Discovery 3 that had failed to respond to remote locking for two years, causing the alarm to trigger whenever the door was opened. The repair cost zero euros — the lock actuator mechanism was simply dirty and dry, requiring only brake cleaner to flush out old dried grease and PTFE-based re-lubrication. A dealer would charge approximately 600 EUR for this job, and a new OEM lock assembly costs about 230 EUR.

The video is equally valuable for its 5-step DIY preparation framework: define the problem, order the spare part as insurance (return if unused), watch YouTube tutorials noting their mistakes (Powerful UK recommended for the removal process), study the specific failure mode (Elisanta's 6-year-old video recommended for Discovery 3/4 lock failures), and consult the workshop manual (found via disco3.co.uk wiki, section 501-14). The manual revealed a critical dependency chain — to remove the rear door latch, you must first remove the window regulator, which requires removing the door handle.

The video also explains the Discovery 3/4 security features: single lock disables interior door handles (breaking a window does not allow opening from inside), and double lock activates full deadlock mode.

## Notes

- Door card removal: 7 screws (one hidden behind latch cover) plus approximately 12 clips
- Lower window to one-third before removing door card
- Access to lock mechanism is through the speaker hole after speaker removal
- Use PTFE-based real grease, not spray grease — solvents in spray grease evaporate, leaving minimal lasting lubrication
- Replacement actuator motors available from China for 0.80 EUR each (part 993647062316)
- Spring positioning inside the lock is critical — study orientation before disassembly
- Workshop manual available via disco3.co.uk wiki — links change frequently due to copyright
- Videos only show the final successful attempt — expect multiple tries in practice
