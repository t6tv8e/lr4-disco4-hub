## Summary

LR TIME begins the body-off disassembly of their replacement Discovery 4, documenting the process from spare wheel removal through wheel arch liners, battery area, and body mount separation. The video emphasizes meticulous workshop organization with a three-category labeling system and finds multiple diagnostic clues of prior amateur work including tie wraps replacing clips and missing copper grease. The most significant discovery is a heavily corroded hidden ground cable behind the front wheel arch, identified as a classic cause of three amigos warnings and unexplained electrical faults. The episode also covers headlight removal (toolless on Discovery 4), viscous fan removal (left-hand thread), and proper fastener documentation.

## Notes

- Headlight removal on Discovery 4 requires no tools, described as one of the model's best design features
- If any rivets are broken during wheel arch liner removal, make a list immediately or they will be missing at reassembly
- Breaking the wheel arch liner clip costs approximately 60 EUR
- Battery area sequence: remove airbox and battery wiring before the battery tray to avoid awkward lifting and potential back injury
- 10 body mounting screws total must be removed before body lift
- Steering rack required lowering the car again because it could not be loosened at full height
- Uncovered fuel tank during storm may have allowed rainwater entry, potentially triggering water-in-fuel error
