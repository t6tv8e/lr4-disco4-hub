## Summary

Christian replaces the clogged heater matrix on a Discovery 3 that was producing a 16-degree temperature split between driver (21 degrees C) and passenger (37 degrees C) sides. After first confirming that all four blend motors were functioning correctly using the built-in self-calibration procedure, the rear of the heater matrix was found completely blocked with an inspection camera. The repair requires removing roughly 25 percent of the interior including the center console, stereo, and HVAC housing. An aftermarket Nissens heater matrix at 67 EUR replaced the OEM unit, restoring equal 41-degree output on both sides. The video includes bonus maintenance during the access opportunity: AC condenser cleaning, drain pipe clearing, cabin filter replacement with a note about confusing OEM vs aftermarket directional markings, and Liqui-Moly AC foam cleaner application.

## Notes

- Difficulty rated 3-4 out of 5 wrenches; described as equivalent to 12/10 for a Toyota driver
- Multiple plastic clips broke during disassembly; some are expensive or only available from UK suppliers
- Carbonated water used as a pressure-test leak indicator at approximately 10 PSI
- Coolant vacuum fill tool eliminates air pocket and bleeding issues
- OEM pipes reused because Nissens pipes are slightly smaller (16.35mm vs 16.46mm OEM)
- Always diagnose blend motors before assuming heater matrix failure; uneven heat symptoms are identical
