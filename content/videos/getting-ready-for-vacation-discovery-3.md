## Summary

Christian outlines the modifications needed for the family's fifth summer vacation in the Discovery 3, marking the transition from a four-person ground tent to a rooftop tent setup. The rooftop tent for two adults consumes nearly all roof space, eliminating the 30-year-old ski box and creating a storage crisis already compounded by the compressor fridge occupying interior cargo space. The solution is a planned DIY aluminium roof box mounted in front of the tent with an angled front face for aerodynamics on a custom low-profile rack. He reviews the evolution of their setup: compressor fridge as the single biggest improvement, second spare tyre after a Croatia flat, and abandonment of mountain bikes that interfered with roof access and grounded out off-road.

## Notes

- Compressor fridge + Goal Zero + solar panel: biggest improvement across all vacation years
- Second spare tyre: essential after Croatia flat left them with no spare for remainder of trip
- Rooftop tent (1.4 m) for two adults; teenagers in ground tents
- Mountain bikes abandoned: roof access issues and hitch rack grounds out off-road
- Planned: DIY aluminium roof box with angled front, low-profile custom rack
- Recovery boards (MaxTrax-style) hard to mount cleanly on Discovery alongside tent and boxes
- Typical destinations: Croatia, Southern Alps
