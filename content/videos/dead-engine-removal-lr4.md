## Summary

LR TIME removes the engine from a 2016 Discovery 4 that has been sitting body-off on a chassis, with the primary goal of determining whether this is the original engine or a previously replaced unit. The flywheel barcode confirmed the engine was manufactured in November 2015, matching the vehicle's original spec and proving this is a first-failure engine. Key findings include a rear crankshaft seal leak at only 135,000 km (a known weak point), oil contamination in the intake suggesting a damaged intercooler, and evidence of prior non-factory transmission work. The episode also features a visit from Simon (UK Land Rover community figure) with his Defender, and the team shares practical tips on hoist setup, torque converter handling, and copper grease preventive maintenance.
