## Summary

This video demonstrates how to replace the automatic gear change link bush on the Land Rover Discovery 3/4 and Range Rover Sport L320. The presenters work underneath the car to access the gear linkage cable that connects to the automatic transmission. The bush inside the linkage arm seizes over time, causing the gear selector to become stiff or, in worse cases, preventing the car from being taken out of park or causing it to jump out of Drive.

The procedure involves removing two bolts (13mm socket and a nut), extracting the old arm, drifting out the worn plastic/metal insert from the arm, cleaning everything up, and reassembling. The presenters mark the cable position with paint before removal to ensure correct reinstallation. They recommend putting the car in off-road mode to raise the air suspension for better access, with an axle stand placed underneath as a safety precaution in case an airbag fails.
