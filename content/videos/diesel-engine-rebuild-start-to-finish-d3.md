## Summary

Christian and Vera perform a complete engine rebuild on a Discovery 3 2.7L TDV6, starting from a seized engine with spun main bearings. The engine block required DIY line boring using a custom-built precision boring bar (TIR 0.01 mm) on a hobby lathe, achieving a bore within 0.03 mm of original Ford factory specification. The crankshaft was polished using a rope-and-abrasive technique, with careful attention to a nick near the seal surface. Cylinder bores were honed to restore 35-45 degree crosshatch patterns.

Assembly includes detailed plastigauge testing on all main and connecting rod bearings, piston installation with careful ring orientation, and oil gallery flushing. The main bolt torque sequence follows 30 Nm, 60 Nm, 145 Nm, then angle tightening to yield. First start achieved nearly 4 bar oil pressure at idle, with the engine described as sounding "like a brand new car."

## Notes

- This video is a condensed overview of the full rebuild series
- Demonstrates that precision engine machining including line boring is achievable in a home shop
- Spun bearings are not just a bearing failure but a block failure requiring geometry restoration
- Oil cooler gasket (steel, ~140 EUR) should be replaced as the cooler is difficult to clean
- Camshaft end float spec: maximum 0.15 mm
- Custom crankshaft stop tool fabricated to dimension 74.2 mm
- Fuel tank must be lowered to access rear chassis bolt — standard on a Land Rover
