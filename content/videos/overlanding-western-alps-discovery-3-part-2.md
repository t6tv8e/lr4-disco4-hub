## Summary

Christian and Vera take their Discovery 3 through the unpaved mountain passes of the Western Alps along the Italy-France border, crisscrossing between countries on gravel roads that were originally WWI military supply routes. The key resource is the Denzel guidebook (approximately 50 EUR, German only), which has documented every legally accessible high point across the Alps for 27 years. Trail conditions include 3-hour off-pavement stretches in low gear, one-lane mountain roads with steep unprotected drops, and tunnels as wide as the vehicle. Despite being in the most densely populated mountain region in the world, the trails are remarkably empty — fewer than 15 vehicles on the most popular French pass on a Sunday morning. The couple strongly recommends the experience, noting that European overlanding is a far smaller niche than in the US or Australia.

## Notes

- Denzel guidebook: German only, approximately 50 EUR, covers Italy/France/Switzerland/Germany/Slovenia
- Width restrictions strictly enforced: 2 metres means 1.98 metres
- Airing down essential for comfort on gravel passes
- Col du Parpaillon: fewer than 15 vehicles on a Sunday over 3 hours
- WWI military roads and accessible forts add strong historical dimension
- Switzerland transit vignette: approximately 40 EUR
- Food, fuel, camping within 2-hour reach everywhere in the region
- Almost exclusively German overlanders encountered on trails
