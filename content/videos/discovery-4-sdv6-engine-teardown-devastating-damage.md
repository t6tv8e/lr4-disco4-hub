## Summary

LR TIME tears down a failed 3.0 SDV6 engine from their fifth Discovery 4 project vehicle ("Prince Charming"), which had 181,000 km and a history of extremely poor maintenance by the previous owner. The teardown reveals devastating damage caused by oil starvation: a melted/collapsed piston, a completely disintegrated chain guide, cylinder wall scoring, and a wasted engine block. The crankshaft and bearings were found to be in acceptable condition, but the block damage makes the engine unsalvageable. The video also reveals the engine had been replaced once before by Land Rover at only 30,000-40,000 km. Financial analysis shows that a Gen 2 replacement engine costs approximately 8,500 EUR in Germany, making the total project cost exceed the price of a running Discovery 4 on the open market.

## Notes

- This is LR TIME's 5th Discovery 4 and 3rd with a failed engine, confirming this as a recurring pattern with the model
- The engine had a non-original VIN date stamp of 2017, meaning Land Rover had already replaced the engine roughly 1-1.5 years after the vehicle was built
- Crankshaft bearings were all unharmed with only minor wear despite catastrophic piston failure
- Piston squirters (oil jets that cool pistons from below) are expensive, not available as spare parts, and contain check valves; a blocked or damaged squirter can independently cause piston failure
- Oil pump on Gen 2 is significantly more expensive than Gen 1
- Ford F-150 2018 V6 Diesel crankshaft is a compatible and cheaper alternative source from the US
- There are TDV6 2.7L engines with 600,000 km that never failed because owners maintained them properly
