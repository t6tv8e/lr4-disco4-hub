## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME discover that the clutch pedal bracket on their Discovery 3 has cracked through from metal fatigue and corrosion, leaving the clutch completely inoperative overnight with no prior warning. The crack occurred at the upper hinge point where the bracket connects the pedal to the master cylinder actuator — an area that was originally only spot welded from the factory.

Removal took approximately 15 minutes using a 15mm spanner, 16mm spanner, and a Torx bit with a universal joint. The repair involved welding the crack closed and adding a reinforcement plate for extra strength, then painting the part using residual welding heat to dry the paint quickly. The video also discusses trail-side emergency fixes for overlanders: filling the crack with epoxy metal and wire reinforcement, or drilling and bolting a plate over the damaged area. As a result, a power drill is now permanently carried in their vehicle.

The recommendation for all manual Discovery 3 owners: remove the lower dash cover and inspect the clutch pedal bracket for fatigue cracks or rust near the hinge point. A Land Rover dealer would replace the entire clutch and brake pedal assembly for an estimated EUR 1,600 including labour and brake system bleeding.

## Notes

- This failure mode is rare but documented — posts found on discovery3.co.uk from 2011 and 2014
- Only applies to manual transmission vehicles (a small subset of D3 owners)
- The brake pedal is not at risk of the same failure due to a different actuation design
- Photographs were sent to Land Rover Germany requesting a statement on whether this is a known issue and whether an improved part exists
- The brake light switch is fragile and can break if pushed in too far when re-arming — always keep a spare
