## Summary

After receiving the most embarrassing TUV inspection report on their Discovery 3, Christian and Vera perform emergency repairs in freezing, snowy conditions. The primary failure was both rear anti-roll bar links — one had completely snapped out of its ball joint and was dangling underneath the vehicle. These aftermarket links failed after approximately 80,000 km, reinforcing the lesson that OEM or quality-brand suspension parts are worth the investment. The video then covers a thorough oil change on the 260,000 km TDV6 diesel, with detailed instruction on inspecting the oil filter for bearing wear glitter. The inspection revealed a healthy engine with no signs of bearing deterioration. Christian provides extensive guidance on oil specification, strongly recommending 5W-40 over the factory 5W-30 for high-mileage TDV6 engines, and warns about the critical oil filter plug insertion step that prevents catastrophic engine damage during routine oil changes.
