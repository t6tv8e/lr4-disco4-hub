## Summary

Peter walks through diagnosing rear air suspension drop (both sides sagging simultaneously points to the valve block rather than individual air springs) and replacing the rear valve block O-rings. The job involves deflating the system, removing the valve block from its bracket on the driver's side, cleaning out dirt contamination with brake cleaner, and fitting new greased O-rings. The trickiest part is accessing the rear pipe fittings in a tight space, requiring patience and about an hour total for reinstallation.
