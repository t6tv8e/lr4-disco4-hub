## Summary

A comprehensive "mythbusters"-style exploration of Discovery 4 headlights covering the differences between halogen and xenon units, how to identify which type you have, upgrading from halogen to xenon, bulb replacement, the beam pattern deflection switch for continental driving, and the LED side light elements. The key finding is that while xenon headlights physically plug straight into a halogen-equipped car (same 16-pin connector), the dipped beam will cut out because the CCF detects the higher current draw. The xenons initially pull 9 amps versus 3.8 amps for halogens, and the car's electronics shut down the circuit. A dealer or specialist tool is needed to reprogram the CCF.
