## Summary

Christian and Vera begin 2026 with a winter overlanding trip through southern France, departing Frankfurt at 5 AM and heading to the Mediterranean coast. With all campgrounds closed in December, they rely on wild camping, their rooftop-mounted diesel heater, and a Goal Zero battery that sustains full-night heating with 40% charge remaining. Highlights include an empty visit to a 4,000-year-old hilltop fort near Les Baux-de-Provence (packed in summer, deserted in December), hiking the frozen Gorge du Verdon with its dramatically low water levels, and a flooded tunnel that proved impassable. Night temperatures of -3 to -5 degrees C test their cold-weather setup, with the diesel heater proving essential not just for warmth but for eliminating tent condensation. The trip demonstrates that southern France offers genuine winter overlanding with world-class scenery and near-total solitude, at the cost of closed infrastructure and freezing conditions.
