## Summary

Christian from LR TIME diagnoses and fixes a front air suspension corner sinking issue on his Discovery. Starting with an overnight height test (raise to off-road, pull fuse, leave overnight), he identifies the front driver side as the problem corner. After removing the front wheels and inner wheel arch liners, he performs a soapy water leak test on strut fittings and bellows — finding cracked bellows but no active air leaks. He then deflates the entire system using the GAP diagnostic tool and removes the front valve for a full rebuild.

The valve disassembly is shown in detail: stainless steel screws, three solenoid plungers (middle one different from outer two), and multiple small O-rings. He uses a modified iPhone SIM tool to extract O-rings without scratching the bores. New O-rings from a rebuild kit are installed, everything is cleaned, and the valve is reassembled. He also takes the opportunity to inspect wiring ground points and general underbody condition while the wheel arch liner is out.

## Notes

- This applies to both Discovery 3 and Discovery 4 — same air suspension valve design
- Christian describes the air suspension as "extremely robust" despite the cracked bellows — the ride is still excellent after many years
