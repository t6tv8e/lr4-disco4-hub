## Summary

Mario V. compares his LR3 (210,000 miles) with his recently purchased LR4 (174,000 miles, 2010 model, $4,000) from a US overlanding perspective. He upgraded primarily for the interior improvements, Apple CarPlay capability, and better aesthetics — not for any mechanical advantage. Both vehicles share similar off-road DNA and are used for overlanding and camping rather than hardcore off-roading (he has a CJ-7 with lockers for that).

His strongest advice: "Do not buy this if you aren't mechanically inclined or interested in learning... this is not the vehicle for you if you're just looking to hop into a $4,000 LR3 or LR4 and just start driving." He notes that problems tend to cluster around 120,000-150,000 miles, so finding one that's past that threshold in good condition is actually a positive sign. Paint oxidation is universal and requires either compounding/polishing (2 hours) or repainting.

## Notes

- His LR4 was bought for $4,000 with 174,000 miles — after lift, tires, paint correction, and modifications, it became a capable overlander
- Solar panel setup with Cascadia panels and secondary battery system — designed for overlanding self-sufficiency
- The LR3 is being sold to fund further LR4 modifications
- He specifically calls out paint oxidation as something to expect on every example of this era
- North American market: called LR4 (not Discovery 4), 5.0L V8 petrol engine standard
