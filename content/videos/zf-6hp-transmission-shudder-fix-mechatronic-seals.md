## Summary

LR TIME delivers a masterclass on fixing the dreaded ZF 6HP transmission shudder that plagues Discovery 3 and early Discovery 4 models. The key revelation: **most 'torque converter failures' on the ZF 6HP actually start with €32 worth of mechatronic seals.**

The mechatronic unit is the hydraulic brain of the gearbox — it controls all the clutch packs via pressurized oil channels. Five rubber seals separate these channels, and when they degrade (becoming flat, brittle, and no longer protruding from the casting), hydraulic pressure leaks between circuits. This causes clutch slip, which you feel as shudder. If ignored, the slipping clutches eventually burn up, and/or the torque converter lockup clutch fails — turning a €32 seal job into a €2,000+ rebuild.

### Critical Installation Notes

1. **Tightening order matters** — mechatronic bolts must be torqued in a specific crisscross pattern at only 8 Nm
2. **Clean the magnetic sensor** — metal shavings accumulate on the gear position sensor; if not cleaned, you'll get wrong gear readings
3. **Shifter fork alignment** — the fork must engage the pin on the mechatronic unit; if missed during reassembly, the transmission won't work
4. **Oil pan access** — the plastic pan can't clear the crossmember without lifting the engine/trans; aftermarket steel pans (~€300) solve this
5. **Old bolts WILL snap** — the pan bolts corrode; always use new ones (included in ZF kit); use hand tools, never power tools

### Fluid Notes
- Original ZF LifeGuard 6 is ~€20/litre (7L in kit)
- Land Rover's "lifetime fluid" claim is essentially marketing fiction
- ZF recommends 80,000 km intervals
- "Arduous use" (which Land Rover says requires fluid changes) includes: city driving, cold weather, short trips — i.e., most normal driving
