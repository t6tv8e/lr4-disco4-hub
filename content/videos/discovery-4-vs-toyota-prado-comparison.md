## Summary

MotorSuite (Kenya-based automotive channel) puts the 2014 Discovery 4 and Toyota Land Cruiser Prado head-to-head in both on-road and off-road testing.

### Discovery 4 Wins On:
- **Power delivery** — 3.0 TDV6 significantly more powerful and responsive than Prado's 2.8 diesel
- **Ride quality** — air suspension described as "nice, soft, fluffy" off-road; Prado's suspension more wobbly
- **Interior quality** — soft leather, better materials, higher quality feel throughout
- **Throttle response** — immediate response vs Prado's noticeable lag
- **Split tailgate** — more versatile loading options
- **Off-road confidence** — "the car seems so confident and sure of itself"

### Prado Wins On:
- **Fuel efficiency** — 8.5 km/L city vs D4's 7 km/L; roughly equal on highway (11 vs 10-11 km/L)
- **Ground clearance** — 205mm vs D4's 185mm (standard ride height)
- **Simplicity** — "nothing too technical, anyone can operate it"
- **Reliability reputation** — reviewers note "is Land Rover really known for reliability?"

### Overall Verdict
The reviewers clearly prefer driving the Discovery 4 — "I'm a fan of it" — citing its power, ride quality, and interior as significantly superior. However, they honestly acknowledge the elephant in the room: Land Rover's reliability reputation vs Toyota's bulletproof image.

## Notes

- Kenya-market comparison — both vehicles common in East Africa
- D4 xenon headlights illuminate up to 300m
- D4 on 19-inch alloys (Pirelli tires); Prado on 17-inch alloys
- The D4 tested was a special edition with black accents
- Both are genuine 7-seaters but D4's split tailgate is more practical for loading
- Prado's side-opening rear door provides good access but less versatility
