## Summary

Christian and Vera retrofit an oil pressure gauge and sensor into their Discovery 3 TDV6, using a custom-machined manifold block that allows both the original factory oil pressure warning switch and a new real-time analog gauge to operate simultaneously. The video documents the correct thread specification (quarter-inch 18 NPT, not M14x1.5 as widely misidentified), the routing of the signal wire through the firewall, gauge mounting in the dashboard, and wiring. Key oil pressure benchmarks are provided: nearly 3 bar on cold start, approximately 1 bar at warm idle (0.7 bar minimum), and 1.9 bar minimum at 3,500 RPM with hot oil. The cheap Chinese sensor showed noticeably slow response compared to a direct reference gauge. A pre-made manifold block is available from R-Spec Ltd (UK) for 33 GBP for those without machining capability.

## Notes

- The correct thread is quarter-inch 18 NPT (tapered), not M14x1.5 as commonly listed online
- Ground the sensor to engine ground and the gauge to chassis ground independently for better signal quality
- Avoid routing cables in front of airbags -- a critical safety point discovered during the install
- The gauge brightness at night may require a dimming resistor
- A higher-quality sensor is strongly recommended over cheap Chinese alternatives for accurate real-time readings
