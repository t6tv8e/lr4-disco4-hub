## Summary

The Lost Cause Ranch team performs a complete timing chain replacement on a donated 2011 LR4 5.0L V8 with 183,000 miles. The engine exhibited classic chain failure symptoms: knocking noise, timing codes, and restricted performance messages. Upon teardown, the passenger side chain had significant slop and the guide failure was clearly visible — the early aluminium chain guides (pre-2013) lack a steel button where the tensioner pushes, allowing the tensioner piston to wear through the guide material. Updated guides with steel inserts became factory standard around the 2013 model year.

The video details the full 16-hour procedure including the most dreaded step — removing all 8 direct fuel injectors with a curved slide hammer. The crank pulley bolt is reverse-threaded (12.9 grade marking), requiring careful attention. Several specialty tools are required including camshaft timing locks, crankshaft and flywheel locks, and a crank pulley puller. The team demonstrates an improvised puller method that avoids removing the radiator and AC condenser.

## Notes

- Lost Cause Ranch has done "numerous" 5.0L V8 timing chain jobs — this is a recurring failure on pre-2013 vehicles
- The vehicle was donated for charity — being refurbished to sell with proceeds split between charities and the previous owner
- They towed this LR4 from Tennessee to Iowa using another LR4 that also had previously needed timing chains
- If the chain actually breaks, piston-to-valve contact occurs — this means cylinder heads must come off and be rebuilt, dramatically increasing the repair cost
- Clean internals at 183K miles suggest a decent oil change history — visible sludge would indicate neglect
- The oil pump chain/guide/tensioner was available but may not need replacement — discussed case-by-case
