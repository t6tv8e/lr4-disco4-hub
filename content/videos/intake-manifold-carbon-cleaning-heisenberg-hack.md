## Summary

LR TIME cleans heavily fouled intake manifolds from a 17-year-old Discovery 3 TDV6 (270,000+ km) using the "Heisenberg Hack" — a chemical cleaning method attributed to a German chemist's forum post. The formula is simple: 500g sodium hydroxide (kitchen drain unblocker, about 3.29 EUR), 100g laundry detergent (powder preferred), and water in a plastic container. The key insight that makes or breaks the method is that active circulation through each intake port via a garden pump is essential — passive soaking alone does not work.

The video establishes an important distinction: intake manifold cracking is specific to the 3.0L sequential turbo engine on the Discovery 4, which develops enough boost pressure to crack them over time. The 2.7L TDV6 manifolds on the Discovery 3 do not crack, making cleaning rather than replacement the correct approach. OEM manifolds cost approximately 270 EUR each and are far superior to aftermarket alternatives.

Additional technical context includes the diesel butterfly valve function (partially closes to force EGR gases into the intake, not throttle control as in petrol engines) and a note on diesel runaway risk if crankcase ventilation fails and oil enters the intake.

## Notes

- Active circulation is the critical success factor — move the pump hose to a different port every 30 minutes, 4 hours total per manifold
- Correct mixing order: water first, then detergent, then sodium hydroxide powder (prevents clumping)
- Safety: sodium hydroxide can cause permanent blindness on eye contact; respirator and safety glasses mandatory
- Use plastic containers only — metal will react with sodium hydroxide
- Remove all electrical components (intake pressure sensor, butterfly valve actuator motor) before submerging
- Map bolt positions on a paper template before disassembly — mark long vs standard bolts and different O-ring sizes
- Pump capacitor may fail from heat during the 4-hour process (4 uF replacement)
- Dispose of spent solution at hazardous waste center — dissolved soot contaminates the solution despite the drain-safe base chemicals
- If blanking the EGR, also remove the butterfly valve — leaving it risks the ECU closing it and stalling the engine
