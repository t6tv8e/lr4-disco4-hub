## Summary

Barrett's Reviews takes his Discovery 4 SE Tech out for a casual Sunday drive, presenting it as a perfect real-world daily driver. The vehicle has 69,000 miles, full service history, and was purchased for £20,000 as a second or third owner. He covers the practical reality of ownership: the 7-seat layout (full-size, not 5+2), the air suspension's ability to iron out British potholes, the 28-30 MPG fuel economy, and the different height modes (access, normal, off-road).

The biggest practical issue was insurance: his first insurer refused to cover the vehicle entirely due to theft risk, and even after switching, premiums were £150/month despite 10 years no claims. Installing a ghost immobiliser and GPS tracker (£800 total) brought premiums down significantly. He also notes the annoying start-stop system that every owner immediately disables.

His buying advice: spend more for a good example with low mileage and full service history rather than buying a cheap one with problems. "They become unreliable because they haven't been looked after."
