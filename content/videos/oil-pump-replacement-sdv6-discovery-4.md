## Summary

LR TIME replaces the oil pump on a Discovery 4 with 177,000 km that was showing below-spec oil pressure. The video demonstrates that this job is dramatically more complex on the D4 compared to the D3 -- the fan shroud removal alone takes 3.5 hours versus 15 minutes on the D3, due to the secondary turbo and additional engine bay components. The original pump (casting 6600AB, date-stamped 2010, 15mm rotor) was confirmed as the factory-original unit that had never been replaced. The channel argues this is a widespread problem: D4 oil pumps almost never get replaced because the job is so difficult, and this likely contributes to the bearing failures these engines are known for at high mileage. After replacement, oil pressure improved to 0.75 bar at idle (spec 0.5-1.0) and 1.9 bar at 3500 RPM -- within spec but still reflecting engine wear. Key techniques include a custom flywheel locking tool through the starter motor hole, the "socket under belt" trick for timing belt installation (from Broken Piston Garage), and a careful oil pump priming procedure using 100ml of oil poured through the filter housing with the check valve held open by a magnet, followed by hand-cranking the engine backwards. Total job time was approximately 1.5 days.

## Notes

- Oil pump casting number guide: AC = weak early pump (replace immediately), AE = newer acceptable pump, 15mm rotor = latest/best version. 13mm rotor to 15mm rotor is approximately 10% more fluid delivery.
- A deep stash/endoscope camera (~25 euros on Amazon) can be used through the starter motor hole to read the pump casting number without any disassembly.
- Crankshaft pulley bolt torque: 375 Nm. LR TIME used a 3-stage approach: 150 Nm, then 300 Nm, then to approximately 370-400 Nm rather than the specified 90-degree angle torque method.
- The crankshaft pulley bolt should ideally be replaced (stretch bolt) but was reused.
- Front crankshaft seal must be installed completely dry (no lubrication) and seated 1mm under flush using a precision custom tool.
- The bypass valve connector was accidentally left disconnected during reassembly, causing a fault code -- reinforces the need to photograph everything before disassembly.
- Fan shroud removal on D4 requires removing intercooler hoses, vacuum chamber, emission components, and various brackets that do not exist on the D3.
- Belt tensioner bolt is a 10.9 grade (uprated from 8.8 on early D3 models) and should be Loctite'd.
- Martin's 2008 D3 with 246,000 km was checked via endoscope and found to have an AE pump -- acceptable for continued use but the 13mm rotor means ~10% less fluid delivery than the latest 15mm version.
