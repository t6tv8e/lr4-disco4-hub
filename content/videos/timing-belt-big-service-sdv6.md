## Summary

The LR TIME team performs a comprehensive timing belt replacement and pre-sale service on a 2015 Discovery 4 SDV6 with 124,000 km. Christian removes the starter motor (35 min), inspects the timing area finding normal belt dust but no oil contamination, and installs an ENA timing belt kit (€110 on sale). Key findings include an oil level sensor connector leaking oil into the wiring harness and a recommendation to always replace the plastic water connection housing during belt work as it cracks around 200,000 km. Belt tension measurement with a Trum meter showed tension can double depending on tensioner position — they recommend setting the notch at the lower end of the window. The video also covers a bad experience with an SKF idler pulley (6mm hex too small to torque properly) and proper O-ring selection for the water housing. Total parts cost for the entire pre-sale service was approximately €700.

## Notes

- LR TIME has multiple timing belt and oil pump replacement videos with step-by-step detail — this video is more of an overview/vlog of the pre-sale service
- The vehicle being sold had a history: bought with serious transmission issues, fixed, driven 12,000 km, and is now being prepped for sale
- Christian's "Queen" (another Discovery) has too many issues to sell — illustrating that even experienced mechanics keep problem vehicles
- Vacuum line routing is critical: disconnecting individual vacuum connections and reassembling incorrectly causes limp mode from turbo system errors
