## Summary

LR TIME purchases a 2015 Discovery 4 in pristine cosmetic condition with a suspected bearing failure, documented from initial dealer inspection through delivery and first diagnostic assessment. The video serves as a comprehensive buying guide, covering how to diagnose a bearing failure before purchase (pulling the oil filter revealed 10+ metal chips), reading fault codes as the first step, and a detailed pre-purchase inspection checklist. Extensive coverage of Discovery 4 trim levels and options identification helps buyers evaluate listings from photos. The key insight is that a mechanically failed Discovery 4 in good cosmetic condition can be a smart buy if the block is not destroyed and you have the skills to repair it yourself. In Germany, 2015 models with AdBlue retain significant value due to emissions zone compliance.

## Notes

- UK vs Germany pricing gap makes buying broken cars and repairing them a viable strategy; a 2015 D4 in this condition might sell for 500-2,800 GBP in the UK but commands far more in Germany
- Discovery 4 has no dipstick; oil level is read through the infotainment system maintenance menu
- Full brake job costs under 300 EUR and should not be a major negotiation point
- Trailer hitch is worth 450 EUR in Germany; confirm it is present before buying
- Power steering hose leak is the same issue seen on their other Discovery
- Transmission fluid change costs approximately 220 EUR DIY with oil at approximately 28 EUR per liter
- M+S tires without the 3-peak mountain snowflake symbol are no longer legal for winter conditions in Germany
