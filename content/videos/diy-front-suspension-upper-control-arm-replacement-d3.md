## Summary

Christian and Vera replace the aftermarket front upper control arms on their 300,000 km Discovery 3 with the original OEM Land Rover arms. The aftermarket arms were dimensionally incorrect, leaving the camber and caster adjustment maxed out with no room for correction. The OEM arms were cleaned, treated with Fertan rust converter, primed, and painted before installation. After fitting, a DIY camber alignment was performed at 466 mm ride height, achieving the target of -0.5 degrees with plenty of adjustment remaining -- something impossible with the aftermarket arms. The fix was validated across a 3,500 km Spain road trip, where paint marks on the bushings confirmed zero rotation or movement. Fuel consumption data from the trip showed the 2.7-ton vehicle averaging under 10 L/100 km under optimal conditions, with the roof tent adding significant aerodynamic drag (difference between 11 and 15 L/100 km on the Autobahn).

## Notes

- OEM arms have the Land Rover emblem; aftermarket had no branding and were the cheapest available
- Lemfoerder bushings felt slightly loose in the arm, so JB Weld was used to take up clearance
- Delphi ball joints were pressed in using a custom-made pressing tool
- Heat shield removal required to access final bolts -- adds complexity
- Anti-seize on all bolt threads for future serviceability
- Height sensor calibration after arm replacement eliminated a 10 mm side-to-side difference
- Post-trip: rear brake discs worn beyond service life, windshield chip in legal line-of-sight zone requires full replacement under German law
- Vacuum pump oil leak confirmed dry after two repair attempts
- Wiper blade slap at Autobahn speeds remains unresolved
- 3,500 km trip consumed 480 L diesel (13.71 L/100 km average overall)
