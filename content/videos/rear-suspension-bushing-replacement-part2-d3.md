## Summary

This is Part 2 of the rear suspension bushing replacement on the Discovery 3, picking up from where Part 1 left off. The video covers reassembly with detailed torque specifications: lower arm to knuckle bolt at 175 Nm, lower arm bolts at 275 Nm, shock absorber to lower arm at 275 Nm (team chose this over Land Rover's 300 Nm spec), and toe-in bolt at 175 Nm. A critical point is reinforced: bushings must be torqued only at the correct ride height to avoid pre-loading the rubber, while bearings can be torqued immediately since they roll freely.

Copper grease is applied to all nuts and bolts but kept off the underside of bolt heads and nut faces to avoid affecting torque readings. A carrier bearing replacement is also performed -- the old bearing was pressed out and the new one pressed in, but the bearing kit shipped without a retaining clip, requiring reuse of the original.

The video honestly documents a mistake: the bump stop was installed on the wrong end of the suspension arm, caught on camera with no fix possible before reassembly. The subsequent TUV re-inspection passes on the suspension work but raises a new issue -- a windscreen chip in the driver's line of sight that two previous inspectors had overlooked. The sticker was issued conditionally on the windscreen being repaired.

## Notes

- Copper grease on all hardware but not on bolt head or nut face surfaces (affects torque accuracy)
- All suspension bolts wire-wheeled clean before reassembly
- Carrier bearing kit may not include retaining clip -- keep the original
- With no air in the strut, pushing the suspension up carries minimal risk; with active air suspension, this could lift the car off jack stands
- A brake hose replacement (donated by subscriber Carl Hansen) deferred to warmer weather
- Previous episode had fixed detached stabiliser bar links (guaranteed TUV fail)
- Severe TUV faults can mask minor ones during inspection -- once major issues are fixed, minor items may be flagged
- Torque specs in this video apply to both Discovery 3 and Discovery 4
