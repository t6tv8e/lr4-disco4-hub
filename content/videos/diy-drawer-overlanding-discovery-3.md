## Summary

Christian and Vera present their full-extension slide-out drawer system built for overlanding in their Discovery 3. The full slide-out (versus a partial fridge slide) was chosen because the Discovery's raised rear ledge makes loading heavy items difficult, and the full extension makes the deep trunk compartment usable as a daily driver. The platform measures 106 cm by 105 cm, uses heavy-duty Amazon rails rated for approximately 200 kg (190 EUR), and bolts into the Discovery's existing M8 tie-down holes with no drilling required. The base is 10-12 mm plastic board (strong but heavy; laminated waterproof wood recommended for future builds) framed with 5 mm thick aluminium angle rails. The drawer is installed at a slight angle (approximately 12 mm) to avoid rubbing against the non-level rear hatch. For power, they use a Goal Zero Yeti 400 (non-lithium) charged via 230V AC routed under the seats. The Engel fridge (35-litre, under 400 EUR) was chosen for its oscillating compressor: quieter operation and lower inrush current than Dometic's rotary compressor, running at 33W on 12V versus 45W on 230V. Storage tricks include hidden under-seat compartments (concealed strap access), a two-tier trunk system holding a week of food for four, removable cubby lids, and a fold-flat steel bench doubling as cooking table and shelf.

## Notes

- Slide-out platform: 106 cm x 105 cm
- Heavy-duty Amazon slide rails: approximately 190 EUR, rated for approximately 200 kg
- Bolts into existing M8 tie-down holes; no drilling required
- Engel fridge: 35 litres, under 400 EUR on sale; oscillating compressor
- Engel power draw: 33W on 12V, 45W on 230V AC; 12V is more efficient
- Goal Zero Yeti 400 (non-lithium version) used for off-grid power
- Inrush current workaround: start fridge on 230V AC, switch to 12V once running
- Plastic board base is extremely heavy; laminated waterproof wood recommended instead
- 5 mm thick aluminium angle rails are critical; flimsy framing causes rail failure under load
- Small latch needed to prevent loaded drawer sliding out uncontrolled on inclines
- Camping table fits in gap between fridge and storage boxes when drawer is closed
- Under-seat compartments: pull concealed strap, fold up lower seat cushion
- Cubby lids can be removed entirely for easier access
- Lower trunk tier: approximately 12 cm deep, holds food for 4 people for one week
