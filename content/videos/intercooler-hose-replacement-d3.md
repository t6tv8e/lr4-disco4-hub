## Summary

LR TIME walks through replacing all three intercooler hoses on a Discovery 3 with silicone upgrades as preventive maintenance before an overland expedition. The job difficulty scales inversely with hose size: the large hose takes about 15 minutes, the medium hose about 1.5 hours (requires wheel arch liner removal), and the small hose approximately 4 hours due to extremely limited access.

The original 180,000 km rubber hoses were found very soft and oil-filled — typical of aging Land Rovers. While silicone hoses are not strictly necessary for performance, they provide two practical advantages: instant visual confirmation that hoses have been replaced (valuable when buying used) and significantly longer service life than rubber originals of unknown age.

The teardown revealed additional problems left by previous workshops: missing hose clamps throughout the engine bay, an incorrectly routed cable, and hose rubbing against adjacent components. The video emphasizes that intercooler leaks are the number one cause of black smoke — and unlike burst hoses which fail obviously, an intercooler can develop a minute crack and leak undetected for years.

## Notes

- Disable air suspension first via fuse F26 (third from bottom) before raising the vehicle
- Remove more plastic trim than seems necessary — provides visibility to check for hose rubbing
- Always plug the intake opening when working near it
- Apply copper paste on all heat shield bolts and studs to prevent future seizing
- Pre-spray hard-to-reach bolts with WD-40 before starting work
- Use a camera/phone to inspect hose clamp seating on blind sides of connections
- Never lubricate lug nuts — grease prevents correct torque readings
- "If it ain't broken, don't fix it" is the wrong philosophy for a Land Rover — preventive replacement is essential
