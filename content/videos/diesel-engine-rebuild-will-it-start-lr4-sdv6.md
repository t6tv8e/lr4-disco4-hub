## Summary

LR TIME completes the full reassembly and reinstallation of a rebuilt SDV6 engine into their Discovery 4, documenting every step from turbo mounting through wiring harness, engine-to-transmission mating, body-on, and first start. The body-off approach proves its value dramatically: engine-to-transmission mating takes 4.5 minutes versus 4.5 hours on a previous body-on build. The first start attempt fails due to reversed polarity blowing the main fuse, but the second attempt succeeds with clean oil pressure and no fault codes. The test drive to 1,800 feet elevation confirms no leaks, stable coolant, and healthy performance. Key technical details include the pneumatic hose gasket centering trick, the unobtainable Gen 2 EGR gasket, and consistent Land Rover torque specifications throughout.

## Notes

- The SDV6 is described as a beautiful, simple, and pleasurable engine to work on, comparable in simplicity to working on a Toyota engine
- Timing belt can be fully disassembled in 10 minutes, described as super simple and super DIY
- Body-off dramatically reduces labor time for engine-transmission mating (4.5 minutes vs 4.5 hours)
- Coolant crossover pipe spring clip design praised as Nobel Prize-worthy engineering
- AC receiver/dryer should be replaced as standard practice whenever AC system has been open for extended period
- Third Land Rover Discovery in the family fleet; license plate includes 3 as a reference
