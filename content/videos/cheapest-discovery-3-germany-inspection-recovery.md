## Summary

Christian and Vera from LR TIME purchase the cheapest Land Rover Discovery 3 in Germany — a 2007-2008 model with 152,700 km for 2,000 EUR, intended for their son Phillip. The car was an auction repossession that had been sitting for approximately four years and was retrieved from an ostrich farm in Eastern Germany using their Discovery 4 as a tow vehicle.

Rapid triage diagnostics revealed a catastrophically failed engine: the oil sump contained large metal chips, two main bearing races were burned up, bearing shells were destroyed, an oil squirter was missing, and the crankshaft was cracked. A previous rebuild attempt had already installed an aftermarket oil pump with a weak housing and started timing belt work, but the repair failed. The "spare engine" found in the trunk turned out to be the original broken engine with a snapped crankshaft.

Despite the engine disaster, the purchase turned out to be justified: the car has a rare factory rear locking differential (estimated at approximately 1% of German Discovery 3s, worth roughly 2,000 EUR alone), the air suspension was fixed on-site by swapping a relay from the unused headlight washing system slot, the parking brake was restored by swapping a missing 30-amp fuse, the body was in excellent condition with cloth seats, and the transfer case and driveshafts showed no leaks.

The episode also documents SDV6 oil dilution on the Discovery 4 tow vehicle — oil level at maximum after only 5,000-6,000 km due to diesel fuel accumulating in the sump, a known SDV6 weakness.

## Notes

- The car had a 24,000 km oil change interval from the Land Rover dealer — described as dangerously long
- Clean oil after a bearing failure is a diagnostic clue that someone recently refilled fresh oil during a failed rebuild
- A missing relay is a simple but easily overlooked cause of air suspension failure — check all relay slots
- Factory rear locking differentials on Discovery 3s are extremely rare in Germany (~1%) and significantly increase the vehicle's value
- The hosts argue that surviving Discovery 3s are now culturally cooler than Discovery 4s — they are owned by mechanically capable people rather than uninformed mall crawlers
- Clogged fuel filter suspected as additional cause of fuel delivery issues
- Rodent infestation found throughout cabin, glove box, and air filter area after 4 years of sitting
- Bonnet latch broken — hood will not close properly
- Trailer loading tip: load the car further forward on the trailer than you think to achieve even tire compression
