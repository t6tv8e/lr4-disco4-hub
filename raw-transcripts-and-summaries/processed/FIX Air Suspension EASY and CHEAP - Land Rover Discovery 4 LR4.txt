## Land Rover Discovery 4: Rear Valve Block O-Ring Replacement Guide

*A DIY walkthrough for diagnosing and fixing rear air suspension drop on a Discovery 4 by replacing valve block O-rings.*

---

### 🔍 Diagnosis & Problem Identification

- **Both rear sides dropping = valve block fault**: If only one side drops, suspect a single-side suspension issue; if **both sides** lose air, the valve block is the likely culprit.
- **Valve block location**: Situated at the rear of the vehicle on the **driver's side (left side)**, identifiable by its electrical connector at the front and air pipes at the rear.
- **Root cause**: Dirt and debris buildup on O-rings causes them to fail and leak air — visible contamination was found during disassembly.

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Parts Required

- **IID Tool** (diagnostic tool) to safely deflate the air suspension system; without it, slowly release air by loosening the valve fittings manually.
- **12mm wrench** to remove air pipes.
- **T20 Torx screwdriver** to remove valve block screws.
- **Flat-head screwdriver** to release the electrical connector pin.
- **Brake cleaner** for cleaning the valve block internals.
- **O-ring kit** (linked in description): an affordable set covering **front, middle, and rear valve blocks**, plus pressure switch O-rings — one kit handles all blocks.
- **Grease** (included with O-ring kit) for lubricating new O-rings on installation.
- **Tape** to cover open air pipes and prevent dirt ingress during the job.

---

### 🔧 Step-by-Step Repair Process

- **Step 1 – Deflate**: Use the IID Tool or manually release air from the suspension before starting.
- **Step 2 – Remove electrical connector**: Push the locking pin and pull the connector free (may require a flat screwdriver due to dirt/tight fit).
- **Step 3 – Slide out valve block**: Push the block upward to release it from its mount.
- **Step 4 – Disconnect air pipes**: Use a **12mm wrench**; there are **three pipes** (one on the bottom, two at the rear). The rear pipes are the hardest to access — budget **~15 minutes** for this step alone. **Start with the green (top) pipe**, then the lower rear pipe.
- **Step 5 – Tape open pipes**: Immediately cover disconnected pipes to keep out debris.
- **Step 6 – Clean the block**: Use brake cleaner to remove dirt buildup from inside the block.
- **Step 7 – Remove T20 screws**: Unscrew the **T20 Torx screws** to open the valve block; watch for small internal **springs**.
- **Step 8 – Replace O-rings**: Work **top to bottom**; each valve has multiple O-rings including a small inner one. Old O-rings are visibly **thinner and worn** compared to new ones.
- **Step 9 – Grease and reassemble**: Apply grease to all new O-rings and metal contact surfaces (not too much). Reassemble block internals.
- **Step 10 – Reinstall valve block**: Reconnect pipes in **reverse order** — lower rear first, upper rear second, bottom last. Total reinstallation time: ~**50 minutes**.
- **Step 11 – Recalibrate**: After powering the system back up, **always calibrate the air suspension** using the IID Tool (referenced in a separate video).

---

### ⏱️ Time & Difficulty

- **Overall difficulty**: Moderate — the job itself is straightforward, but **accessing the rear pipes is the main challenge**.
- **Most time-consuming step**: Removing and reinstalling the rear air pipe fittings (~15 min removal, ~50 min full reinstallation).
- **Patience required**: Risk of breaking brittle fittings if rushed.

---

### 📋 Key Tips & Warnings

- **Don't overstretch the valve block** when maneuvering it to access rear pipes.
- **Lay out removed parts in order** to know exactly where each O-ring and component goes back.
- **Calibrate suspension** after every air suspension service — skipping this step can cause incorrect ride height readings.
- Result after repair: System pumped back to **off-road height** and held — a positive early indicator the O-rings were the source of the leak.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Replacing valve block O-rings is a **cost-effective first step** before committing to a full valve block replacement on a Discovery 4 with sagging air suspension. The O-ring kit costs only a few dollars, but the job demands patience — particularly when accessing the rear pipe fittings in a tight space. Proper cleaning, greasing, and post-job calibration are essential for a lasting fix.
