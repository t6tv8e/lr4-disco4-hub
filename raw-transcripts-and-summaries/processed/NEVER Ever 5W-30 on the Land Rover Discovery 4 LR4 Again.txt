## DIY Oil Change on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A step-by-step walkthrough of changing the engine oil on a Land Rover Discovery 4, including oil selection rationale, key tools, and Discovery-specific quirks.*

---

### 🛢️ Oil Selection & Why It Matters

- **Switching from C2 to C3 spec oil**: The presenter moved from **Fuchs 5W-30 (C2)** to **Castrol 5W-40 (C3)** based on community recommendations, as C3 has a **higher viscosity**, which is considered better protection for the Discovery 4 engine.
- **Known engine fragility**: The Discovery 4 engine has documented issues including **crankshaft failures**, making quality oil and frequent changes especially important.
- **Recommended change interval**: Every **10,000 km** to maximize engine longevity.

---

### 🔧 Tools & Materials Needed

- **Oil filter**: MAN filter (~**€15 on Amazon**), requires a **32mm socket** to remove the housing.
- **New O-ring**: Included with the filter — must be replaced; apply a little oil before reassembly.
- **Custom drain canister**: A DIY cut canister is strongly recommended because the drain plug sits only **~8–10 cm from the chassis frame**, causing oil to splash everywhere without one.
- **Oil quantity**: Approximately **5–5.5 liters**; start with **5 liters**, then verify level before adding more.

---

### 🚗 Discovery 4-Specific Quirks

- **Air suspension must be locked** before lifting: Either use a specialist module (e.g., Madtech 4x4) or **remove the 60-amp air suspension fuse** from the engine bay fuse box to prevent the suspension from dropping while the car is on stands.
- **Skid plate removal required**: The sump drain plug is only accessible after removing the underbody skid plate, which is held by **8 screws**.
- **Unusual oil level check procedure**: Unlike most cars, the Discovery 4 requires you to **run the engine for ~10 minutes, then wait another 10 minutes**, and check the level via the **onboard service menu** (not a traditional dipstick).

---

### ✅ Step-by-Step Process Summary

1. Lift car, put air suspension in service mode (remove 60A fuse if no module).
2. Place axle stands, remove the underbody skid plate (8 screws).
3. Position DIY splash canister under drain plug; remove plug and drain oil.
4. Remove oil filter housing (32mm socket); replace filter and **O-ring** (lightly oiled).
5. Reinstall drain plug; add **5 liters of new oil**.
6. Run engine ~10 min, wait 10 min, check level via service menu; top up if needed.
7. Reinstall skid plate and reinsert the 60A air suspension fuse.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This video matters because the Discovery 4 has **known engine reliability issues**, making proper, frequent oil maintenance critical. The car's design introduces non-obvious complications — a poorly positioned drain plug, a mandatory air suspension lockout procedure, and a digital-only oil level check — that can catch DIYers off guard. Switching to a **higher-viscosity C3-spec oil (5W-40)** and changing it every **10,000 km** is presented as a practical strategy to extend engine life.
