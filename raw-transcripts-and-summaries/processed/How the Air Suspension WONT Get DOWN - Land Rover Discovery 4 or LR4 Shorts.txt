## How to Prevent Air Suspension from Dropping on a Parked Car

*Removing a single fuse keeps an air-suspended vehicle at its set ride height when parked.*

---

### 🔧 The Fix

- **Root cause**: Air suspension systems can deflate/drop while the car is parked; removing the relevant fuse disables the compressor/leveling system and holds the current height.
- **Step 1 – Access**: Open the hood and remove the cover for the battery and fuse box.
- **Step 2 – Remove the fuse**: Pull the **60 amp fuse** responsible for the air suspension system.
- **Result**: The car remains locked at its **set ride height** rather than sagging when stationary.

---

### ⚠️ Things to Consider

- **Trade-off**: With the fuse removed, the air suspension system is disabled — automatic leveling and adjustments will not function until the fuse is reinstalled.
- **Reversible**: This is a simple, non-permanent workaround requiring no tools beyond fuse removal.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This is a quick, zero-cost workaround for owners frustrated by air suspension sag when parked. By pulling the **60 amp fuse**, the system is frozen at the desired height — useful for aesthetics or preventing uneven wear — but should be understood as a **temporary or situational fix**, not a permanent solution to underlying suspension issues.
