## How to Change the Differential Oil on a Range Rover Evoque

*A straightforward DIY guide covering the tools, fluids, and steps needed to drain and refill the rear differential on a Range Rover Evoque.*

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Materials Required

- **Oil type**: Castrol TransMax **80W-90**, as specified in the Land Rover workshop manual — approximately **€15 on Amazon**
- **Oil quantity**: **0.7 liters** total fill
- **Allen key size**: **10mm** (hex/Allen key or socket set)
- **Torque wrench**: Required — both plugs torque to **34 Newton meters**
- **Oil pump**: Recommended for filling, as the fill port is awkwardly positioned and the bottle's built-in funnel may not reach
- **Drain plug hardware**: Two bolts with **copper washers** — Land Rover recommends replacing these; reuse only if in good condition

---

### 🔩 Procedure Overview

- **Two plugs to remove**: The **fill plug** (left, upper) and the **drain plug** (left, lower) — both use a **10mm Allen key**
- **Remove the fill plug first** before draining, to ensure the fill port isn't seized (standard best practice to avoid trapping oil)
- **Drain completely**, then reinstall the drain plug at **34 Nm** before refilling
- **Fill until oil spills out** of the fill hole — this is the workshop manual's confirmed method to know the level is correct (~0.7 L)
- **Reinstall fill plug** at **34 Nm**

---

### ⚠️ Why This Service Matters

- **Magnetic drain plug captures metal particles**: The plug is magnetic and visibly accumulates fine metal debris from gear wear — cleaning this plug reveals how much contamination builds up over time
- **Degraded oil loses its protective properties**: Even if oil color looks acceptable, additives break down over time, reducing protection for the differential gears
- **Often overlooked**: Many owners service engine oil but neglect the differential — this video highlights it should be done **more frequently than most assume**

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Changing the differential oil on a Range Rover Evoque is a **beginner-friendly, low-cost job** (under €20 in materials) that takes under 30 minutes. The key details are using **Castrol 80W-90**, torquing both plugs to **34 Nm**, and filling until oil flows out of the fill port. The amount of metal debris on the magnetic drain plug alone makes a strong case for performing this service regularly.
