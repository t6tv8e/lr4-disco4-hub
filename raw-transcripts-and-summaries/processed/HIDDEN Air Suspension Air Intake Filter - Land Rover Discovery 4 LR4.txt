## Land Rover Discovery 4: Changing the Air Filter, Pollen Filter & Hidden Air Suspension Filter

*TLDR: A practical walkthrough showing how to service three filters on a Land Rover Discovery 4, including a little-known air suspension compressor filter hidden in the trunk.*

---

### 🔧 Engine Bay Air Filter

- **Location & Access**: The air filter sits under a cover near the battery and fuse box, secured by **7 screws**. Only a screwdriver is needed.
- **No full disassembly required**: Despite conventional advice to remove airflow sensors, the filter can be extracted by lifting the cover only slightly, avoiding the need to dismantle those components.
- **Cleaning vs. replacing**: If the filter isn't heavily soiled (especially with a snorkel/locker fitted), blowing it out with a **compressor** is sufficient for mid-season maintenance; full replacement can wait until end of season.
- **Reinstallation tip**: Lift the cover carefully when sliding the filter back in to avoid damage; ensure all screws are replaced.

---

### 🌿 Cabin Pollen Filter

- **Location**: Passenger side, behind the **lower glove box**.
- **Access method**: Push the two retaining arms inward on both sides of the glove box so it drops down, giving clear access to the filter housing — **no tools required**, fingers only.
- **Removal**: Push the housing clip inward, slide the cover off, push the filter downward slightly, then pull it out.
- **Critical installation detail**: The filter has **directional airflow arrows that must point downward** — incorrect orientation reduces effectiveness.
- **Difficulty level**: One of the easiest pollen filter changes of any car — the clip audibly **snaps** when correctly seated.
- **Filter used**: **Mann Filter** brand recommended.

---

### 💨 Hidden Air Suspension Compressor Filter (The Surprise)

- **Most drivers don't know this exists**: There is a **third filter** specifically for the air suspension compressor, located in the **left side of the trunk** behind a removable panel.
- **What it is**: A **foam/sponge element** housed inside a small plastic cylinder connected to the air suspension tubing.
- **Cleaning method**: Remove the cap by pulling it off, extract the sponge, wash with **warm water and soap**, allow to fully dry, then reinsert and snap the cap back on.
- **Why it matters**: A clogged compressor intake filter can strain the air suspension compressor — a costly component to replace on the Discovery 4.
- **Credit**: This filter's existence was highlighted by the YouTube channel **Discovery Adventures**.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Most Discovery 4 owners service only the obvious filters, but the **hidden air suspension compressor sponge filter** in the trunk is arguably the most important find here — neglecting it can accelerate wear on an expensive air suspension system. All three filters are DIY-friendly, requiring minimal tools and no specialist knowledge, making this a worthwhile routine maintenance task.
