## Replacing Steering Wheel Controls on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*TLDR: A step-by-step walkthrough of replacing a faulty left-side steering wheel control panel on a Land Rover Discovery 4, including airbag removal and reinstallation.*

---

### 🛠️ Why This Repair Was Needed

- **Cosmetic degradation**: The original control panel was visibly worn and discolored.
- **Functional failure**: The volume control was unreliable and unresponsive while driving.
- **Low-cost fix**: A replacement unit was sourced from **eBay for ~$8**, matching the original color and button layout.

---

### 🧰 Tools & Parts Required

- **Airbag removal tool** (specific to Land Rovers up to ~2016): costs **~$6–7 on Amazon or eBay** — required to release the spring clips holding the airbag.
- **T20 Torx screwdriver**: used to remove the **two screws** securing the control panel.
- **Replacement steering wheel control panel**: same button layout as the original; includes a blank plate where adaptive cruise control buttons would go if the vehicle had that feature.

---

### ⚡ Safety First: Battery Disconnection

- **Disconnect the battery before starting** and wait **at least 10 minutes** before touching the airbag — this bleeds residual voltage and prevents accidental airbag deployment.
- **Reconnect the battery briefly** mid-job only if needed to reposition the steering wheel for better screw access, then disconnect again before continuing.

---

### 🔄 Step-by-Step Process

- **Remove the airbag**: Insert the airbag tool into the side of the steering wheel to catch and release the retaining cable/clip; repeat on both sides. The airbag will pop forward slightly once released.
- **Disconnect airbag connectors**: There are **two or three connectors** (including a ground/mass cable) behind the airbag — press the clip tabs to release each one.
- **Remove the control panel**: Unscrew the **T20 screw on the bottom** (and one on top); pull the panel out — it is also held by clips. Press the connector clip and unplug it.
- **Install the new panel**: Connect the **shorter/bottom cable first** (the hardest step — cable is very short). Use the airbag tool to push the connector forward **2–3 cm** to gain enough slack to plug it in. Then connect the remaining cable.
- **Airbag wiring color code**: **Black connector → Green socket; Brown connector → Pink socket** — labeled to prevent incorrect installation.
- **Reinstall the airbag**: Align the connectors, seat the airbag, and press firmly until you **hear a click** confirming it is locked in place.

---

### ✅ Result & Verification

- After reconnecting the battery and starting the car, the **control panel indicator lights turned green immediately**.
- Functions tested: **Mode button, Bluetooth audio, DVD, Radio** — all responded correctly.
- The repair applies equally to the **right-side steering wheel controls**, which use the same removal method.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This is a **beginner-friendly DIY repair** that costs under $15 in parts and tools, avoids a dealership visit, and restores both appearance and functionality to a worn steering wheel control panel. The only genuinely tricky step is routing and connecting the short bottom cable — using the airbag removal tool to gain extra cable slack is the key technique that makes it possible.
