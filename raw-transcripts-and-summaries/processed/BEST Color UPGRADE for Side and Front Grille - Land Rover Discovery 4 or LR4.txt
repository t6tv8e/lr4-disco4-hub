## Painting & Recoloring a Land Rover Discovery 4 Grille and Side Inlet

*TLDR: A DIY guide to removing, sanding, painting, and reinstalling the front grille and side inlet on a Land Rover Discovery 4 to match the color of the door handles using Duplicolor metallic paint.*

---

### 🎯 Project Goal

- **Color change objective**: Repaint the front grille and side inlet from **silver/stock color** to a **steel gray metallic** finish matching the existing door handles and trim for a unified look.
- **Paint used**: **Duplicolor steel gray metallic** spray paint, followed by a **clear/transparent topcoat** for protection and finish.

---

### 🔧 Removing the Side Inlet

- **Removal tool**: A **plastic interior trim removal tool** (pry tool) is recommended over the rope-pull method some use; start from the bottom edge and pop the clips free.
- **Clip-based fitment**: The side inlet is held by **plastic clips** with an inner cover — no screws required, making removal straightforward.

---

### 🔧 Removing the Front Grille

- **Four clips on top**: Push down the **four clips along the top edge**, then pull the grille outward — described as very simple and quick.
- **Disassembly tip**: The silver mesh insert and the black outer frame are **two separate pieces held by four screws**, allowing them to be painted individually for a cleaner result.
- **Non-original grille**: The grille on this vehicle was not original, which made separation easier — original grilles may be more difficult to split.

---

### 🪣 Surface Preparation

- **Sanding grits used**: **240-grit** dry sanding paper first, followed by **600-grit** wet sanding paper (used with water for a smoother finish).
- **Cleaning step**: After sanding, parts are washed and wiped down with **brake disc cleaner** to remove all dust and grease before painting.

---

### 🎨 Painting Process

- **Coat sequence**:
  1. **First coat** — light base layer, allowed to dry
  2. **Second coat** — full metallic color coat
  3. **Two clear/transparent coats** — applied after color coats fully dry for protection and gloss
- **Heat drying caution**: When using a heat gun or dryer to speed drying, **keep heat low** — excessive heat causes the paint to **bubble**, ruining the finish.

---

### 🔩 Reassembly

- **Gluing the grille insert**: The inner silver mesh is glued back onto the outer frame using **"All Fix" adhesive** (a strong multipurpose glue), rather than screws, after painting.
- **Reinstallation**: Both the side inlet and front grille simply **press/clip back into place** — no tools required for final fitment.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This project demonstrates that recoloring factory plastic trim on a Land Rover Discovery 4 is a **low-cost, beginner-friendly DIY job** requiring only basic tools, sandpaper, spray paint, and patience. The key to a quality result is **proper surface prep (sanding + cleaning)**, using a **primer-compatible metallic spray paint**, and finishing with a **clear coat** — all achievable in a home workshop setting.
