## How to Replace O-Rings in the Front Valve Block on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*TLDR: A step-by-step walkthrough of deflating the air suspension, removing the front valve block, replacing worn O-rings, and re-inflating the system on a Land Rover Discovery 4 using a GAP IID diagnostic tool.*

---

### 🔧 Why This Job Is Needed

- **Symptom**: The front air suspension struggles to rise and hold position, indicating leaking O-rings in the front valve block.
- **Fix**: Replacing the O-rings inside the valve block is a low-cost repair (parts sourced cheaply on eBay) that can restore proper suspension function.
- **Key observation**: Upon disassembly, one O-ring was visibly much thicker/deformed than the other, confirming degradation over time — making periodic O-ring replacement a worthwhile preventive measure.

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Parts Required

- **GAP IID diagnostic tool** (or equivalent Land Rover-compatible scan tool) for deflating and re-enabling the Electronic Air Suspension (EAS)
- **Replacement O-rings** (available on eBay)
- Standard screwdrivers and sockets
- Jack to lift the vehicle after deflation

---

### ⚠️ Critical Safety Warning

- **Never unscrew the valve block connections while the air suspension is still pressurized.** If disconnected under pressure, fittings can violently pop off.
- Always **fully deflate the system first** and confirm pressure is gone before loosening any connections.

---

### 📋 Step-by-Step Process

- **Step 1 – Deflate the suspension**: Using the GAP IID tool, navigate to *Service Test → RLM Suspension → Deflate Corners* to release air from all four corners (deflating the reservoir is not necessary for this job). Confirm "Operation Succeeded" before proceeding.
- **Step 2 – Jack up the car and remove the wheel**: Once the suspension is fully deflated, the car sits very low; raise it and remove the front wheel to access the valve block.
- **Step 3 – Disconnect and remove the valve block**: Unplug the electrical connector, then remove **three screws/connections** securing the valve block to its bracket. Note the air supply ports — two feed left and right air springs, one connects to the air tank.
- **Step 4 – Disassemble and replace O-rings**: Unscrew the long bolts on top of the valve block carefully (components can spring out). Swap in the new O-rings and reassemble.
- **Step 5 – Reinstall**: Reattach the valve block to its bracket — **green fitting goes to the lower port; top-to-top and bottom-to-bottom** orientation applies to the connections. Connect the lower (black) connector first for easier fitment, then reattach the electrical plug.
- **Step 6 – Reinflate via GAP IID tool**: Start the engine, navigate to *Service Test → EAS → Re-enable*, then raise the suspension to set it back to customer mode. Confirm the car rises to the correct height and holds position.

---

### ✅ Result

- After the repair, the vehicle successfully raised to **extended mode** and held its position with **no audible air leaks**, indicating a successful O-ring replacement.
- The creator planned to monitor the car over the following days to confirm the suspension maintains height long-term.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Air suspension valve block O-rings degrade silently over time and are a common, inexpensive root cause of front suspension drop on the Land Rover Discovery 4. This job is DIY-accessible if you own a compatible diagnostic tool to safely manage system pressure — skipping that step is the main risk. Periodic O-ring replacement can prevent more costly suspension failures down the line.
