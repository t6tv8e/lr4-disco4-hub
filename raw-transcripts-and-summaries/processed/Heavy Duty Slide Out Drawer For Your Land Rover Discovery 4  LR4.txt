## DIY Car Boot Drawer Build for a Daily Driver Camper Conversion

*A maker builds a slide-out drawer and future camping table for his car boot for ~€400, saving over €600 compared to commercial alternatives.*

---

### 🚗 Why a Drawer Instead of a Full Kitchen

- **Daily driver constraint**: The car carries kids and handles shopping, making a permanently installed kitchen impractical — a removable drawer is a flexible compromise.
- **Modular advantage**: The drawer preserves the under-boot storage space, which a fully integrated kitchen would eliminate.

---

### 🛠️ Build Overview & Materials

- **Base material**: Made from **screen printing plates**, chosen because they are water-resistant.
- **Drawer plate thickness**: **80mm**; table extension plate: **12mm**.
- **Structural frame**: **Aluminium profiles** measuring **10cm × 5cm**, **5mm thick**, and **1 meter long**, cut at **45° at the front** and **~25° at the rear** to accommodate seat clearance and the slight V-shape of the boot floor.
- **Mounting**: Uses the **four existing factory mounting points** in the boot, secured with **M8 screws** and ~**15mm washers** to compensate for the floor angle.
- **Rails**: Heavy-duty **locking drawer rails from Juncker**, rated to **200 kg capacity**. Rail pairs cost **€17 (small)** and **€20 (large)** — four pairs needed. The rails come with special profiles that make height and width adjustment straightforward.

---

### 💰 Cost & Savings

- **Total build cost**: ~**€400** (rails, aluminium profiles, plates, bolts).
- **Cheapest commercial equivalent found**: ~**€1,000+** for a Land Rover Discovery drawer system.
- **Savings**: Approximately **€600+** by building it yourself.

---

### 📐 Key Design Details

- The rails are mounted **slightly non-horizontal** (higher at front, lower at rear) to match the boot floor's gentle V-shape, allowing the drawer to slide smoothly over the gap.
- The drawer sits **elevated** to leave room for folding **aluminium table legs** underneath, enabling the 12mm plate to extend out as a **camping table** (legs to be added in a future build).
- If no camping table is needed, the aluminium profile can be flipped upside down to allow the drawer to sit **much lower**, maximizing boot depth.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This build demonstrates that a functional, high-capacity (**200 kg**) boot drawer system can be made for less than half the cost of commercial options using off-the-shelf aluminium profiles, water-resistant screen printing plates, and quality locking rails. The design is particularly smart for daily-driver camper conversions because it remains modular, preserves under-floor storage, and can double as a camping table — without permanently sacrificing the car's everyday usability.
