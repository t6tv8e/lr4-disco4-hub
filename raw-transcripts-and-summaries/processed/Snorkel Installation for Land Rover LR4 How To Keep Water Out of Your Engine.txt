## Installing an MCH/Terra Firma Snorkel on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A step-by-step walkthrough of fitting an MCH (Terra Firma) high air intake snorkel to a Land Rover Discovery 4, including tool requirements, fitment tips, and honest trade-off assessments.*

---

### 🔍 Why MCH/Terra Firma Over Safari Snorkel?

- **Easier installation**: The MCH/Terra Firma snorkel is significantly simpler to fit compared to the Safari snorkel used on the presenter's previous Discovery 4.
- **Better A-pillar integration**: The MCH unit sits flush and seamlessly on the A-pillar, which is expected to **reduce wind noise** compared to the Safari alternative.
- **Trade-off — sealing**: The Safari snorkel is easier to achieve a **near-100% sealed system** with; the Terra Firma unit is harder to fully seal, making it less ideal for serious water crossing use.

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Kit Required

- **Included in the box**: Air intake head, snorkel body, two mounting plates (one for Discovery 3, one for Discovery 4 — the **C2 plate** fits the Discovery 4), ceiling/seal strips, mounting plate for snorkel top, screws, and rivet nuts.
- **Tools needed**: Rivet nut tool (recommended over a standard nut-and-bolt approach), electric drill, **9 mm drill bit** (not 7 mm as stated in the instructions), **16 mm drill bit** for the main air entry hole, screwdriver, and a **4 mm Allen/hex key**.
- **Sealing material**: **Sikaflex** (flexible car adhesive sealant) used to seal rivet nuts and trim to prevent corrosion and water ingress.
- **Cost of rivet nut tool**: Approximately **€20–€30** — strongly recommended for this job.

---

### 🔧 Installation Process

- **Step 1 – Remove air inlet cover**: Pull the factory air inlet cover out by hand; it unclips without tools.
- **Step 2 – Attach seal and mounting plate**: Apply the ceiling/seal strip, orient the snorkel correctly, and position the C2 mounting plate against the A-pillar.
- **Step 3 – Mark and drill holes**: Use tape to hold the plate in position, mark drill points with a pencil, ensuring clearance for the door to open (very tight fit).
- **Step 4 – Remove A-pillar trim**: Unclip the trim after opening the hood; note that the previous owner had **glued the trim clips** — an unexpected complication requiring cutting and re-clipping.
- **Step 5 – Install rivet nuts**: Drill **9 mm holes**, apply Sikaflex into each hole before inserting the rivet nut to prevent corrosion, then compress with the rivet tool.
- **Step 6 – Drill 16 mm snorkel entry hole**: Locate and drill a **16 mm hole** at the precise marked position where the snorkel body passes through the A-pillar area; the snorkel body fits perfectly once done.
- **Step 7 – Reassemble and mount snorkel**: Reattach the A-pillar trim with sealant, then mount the snorkel using the long screws with Allen key fittings, including a **distance/protective washer** between the snorkel and A-pillar to prevent paint damage.
- **Step 8 – Fit intake head**: Remove the plastic funnel from the factory cover and fit the intake head — this improves airflow and is recommended before final assembly to avoid disassembly later.
- **Note**: Full Sikaflex sealing of the system will be completed **after a full paint restoration** in warmer weather.

---

### 💰 Cost & Overall Assessment

- **Purchase price**: Approximately **€250** for the complete MCH/Terra Firma snorkel kit.
- **Biggest installation challenge**: Getting the screws aligned and tightened at the lower mounting point — the rest of the install was straightforward.
- **Verdict**: Recommended for those who prioritise **simple installation and clean aesthetics**; not recommended if a **fully sealed intake system** is the priority (e.g., deep water wading).

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

The Terra Firma snorkel is a strong choice for Discovery 4 owners wanting a cleaner A-pillar look and easier DIY installation, but buyers must understand it sacrifices the tight sealing capability of the Safari snorkel. The key practical tips — using a rivet nut tool, upsizing to a 9 mm drill bit, applying Sikaflex before rivet nut insertion, and fitting the intake funnel before final assembly — can save significant time and rework.
