## How to Change Transmission Fluid on a Range Rover Evoque (2016+)

*A DIY walkthrough for draining and refilling automatic transmission fluid on a 9-speed Range Rover Evoque, including key tips to maximize fluid extraction.*

---

### 🔧 Tools & Materials Needed

- **10mm socket/wrench**: Used to remove the underbody protection panel (~8–9 bolts)
- **8mm wrench**: Used to loosen both the drain plug and the fill plug
- **Small hand pump**: ~250ml capacity used to refill transmission fluid (a larger pump would save time)
- **Drain tray**: To catch old fluid
- **Transmission fluid**: Must meet **Land Rover specification LR023288** (example brand: Eurolab, made in Germany)

---

### 🚗 Key Facts About This Job

- **Transmission type**: 9-speed automatic
- **Total fluid capacity**: ~**6.5 liters**
- **Realistic drain amount via this method**: ~**4–4.5 liters** (roughly **2–2.5 liters remain** in the transmission)
- **Refill amount in this job**: ~**4 liters**
- **Full fluid replacement**: Requires a **professional transmission flush** if you want all 6.5L changed

---

### 🛠️ Step-by-Step Process

- **Remove underbody protection** first by unbolting ~8–9 x 10mm bolts to access the transmission drain and fill plugs
- **Loosen the fill plug before the drain plug** — critical safety tip: if you drain first and can't open the fill plug, you're stranded with an empty transmission
- **Drain the fluid** by removing the drain plug and directing flow into a tray
- **Maximize drainage by raising the car higher**: Counter-intuitively, lifting the car *higher* (not lower/level) causes significantly more fluid to drain out; repeating the lift cycle multiple times extracts additional fluid
- **Inspect the drain plug seal** — replace if it looks worn before reinstalling
- **Refill through the fill hole** using a pump until fluid starts to overflow from the fill hole — this is the indicator that the transmission is full
- **After refilling**: start the engine, cycle through all gears, and verify smooth shifting

---

### ⚠️ Important Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Fluid condition check**: This Evoque's fluid appeared relatively healthy; a comparison vehicle (Discovery 4) had black, degraded fluid resembling used motor oil — a sign of neglect
- **Regular partial changes are acceptable**: Since ~2.5L always remains, doing this service repeatedly will gradually dilute old fluid with fresh fluid over time
- **Don't use just any ATF**: Fluid must carry the correct **Land Rover LR023288 spec** to be safe for this transmission

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This is a straightforward DIY service that saves money and extends transmission life on the Range Rover Evoque. The most valuable takeaway is the **counterintuitive lifting trick** — raising the car higher extracts significantly more old fluid than leaving it level. The only limitation of the DIY method is that ~2.5L of fluid always remains, so a professional flush is the only way to achieve a complete fluid change. Done regularly, however, this partial-change method is a practical and cost-effective maintenance strategy.
