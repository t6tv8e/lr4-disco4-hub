## Retrofit Apple CarPlay & Android Auto Module on Land Rover Discovery 4 – Full Walkthrough

*TLDR: A ~€290 retrofit CarPlay/Android Auto module for the Land Rover Discovery 4 works well overall, with a few limitations worth knowing before buying.*

---

### 📦 Product & Installation Overview

- **Cost**: ~**€290** (tax included in Austria), ordered via **AliExpress**, shipped from Poland — described as one of the cheapest options on the market.
- **Installation time**: **2–3 hours** for someone unfamiliar with the process, due to many screws and panel removal steps.
- **A pricier alternative** (e.g., "Nut Plus") costs **~€600–700** and offers additional features like steering wheel song-skipping support.

---

### 📱 Connectivity – CarPlay & Android Auto

- **Apple CarPlay**: Connects wirelessly via **2.4GHz or 5GHz Wi-Fi**; pair the device once via USB, then it connects automatically afterward.
- **Android Auto**: Also supports **wireless connection** (via Bluetooth/Wi-Fi) — contrary to what the product description states. USB is only needed for initial setup.
- **Activation**: Triggered by pressing either the **navigation button on the steering wheel** or the **nav button on the dash**.

---

### ⚙️ Key Setup Steps

- After installation, go to **Audio & Video → Music settings** and switch the audio source to **AUX** — otherwise no sound will come through.
- The **original microphone** built into the car can be used, or an aftermarket hands-free mic can be retrofitted.
- **Echo cancellation** is available in settings but was not tested by the reviewer.

---

### 🛠️ Module Settings & Features

- **Audio settings**: Adjustable main volume, navigation volume, in-call volume, microphone sensitivity, and a **built-in equalizer** with custom presets.
- **Camera support**: Can integrate **front and rear retrofit cameras**; the original factory camera is supported and enabled by default.
- **UI customization**: Choose driver position (left/right), wallpapers, and display language (English, German, Portuguese, etc.).
- **Firmware updates** are possible but the process was not yet explored by the reviewer.
- **AV input** is also available for additional external devices.

---

### ✅ What Works Well

- **Spotify and music playback** runs smoothly with no audio lag.
- **Phone calls** can be made from either the CarPlay interface or the original Land Rover menu — both work simultaneously.
- All **original Land Rover functions** (e.g., 4x4 terrain settings) remain fully operational alongside the module.
- Switching between CarPlay and the stock Land Rover screen is seamless via the nav button or the home menu button.

---

### ⚠️ Limitations & Drawbacks

- **Original navigation is replaced** — the factory nav (last updated ~**2016**) is lost, but Google Maps, Waze, or HERE Maps fill the gap.
- **Steering wheel controls do not work** with CarPlay for skipping tracks — a notable missing feature for music users.
- **Small screen update delay** between physical controls and the CarPlay display.
- **Touchscreen scrolling** requires pressing and swiping rather than a standard flick gesture — slightly less intuitive.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

For **~€290**, this module is a cost-effective way to add modern CarPlay and wireless Android Auto to an aging Land Rover Discovery 4 with an outdated infotainment system. The trade-off is losing steering wheel media controls and native navigation. If those features are essential, the **~€600–700 premium alternative** is worth considering — otherwise, this budget option delivers solid everyday functionality.
