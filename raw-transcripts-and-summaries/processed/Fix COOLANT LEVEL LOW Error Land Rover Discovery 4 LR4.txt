## How to Replace a Coolant Expansion Tank on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*TLDR: A DIY guide to diagnosing and replacing a faulty coolant expansion tank that triggers a false "coolant level low" warning, costing ~€40 in parts.*

---

### 🔍 Diagnosing the Problem

- **False low coolant warning**: The car displayed a "coolant level low" error, but the coolant level was consistently correct when physically checked — pointing to a sensor/tank issue rather than an actual coolant leak.
- **Two root causes inside the expansion tank**: Either the **sensor itself is faulty**, or the **small white float** inside the tank gets stuck in the down position, falsely triggering the low-level warning even when coolant is full.
- **Rule out coolant loss first**: If coolant level is actually dropping, the problem is a leak — not the expansion tank. This fix only applies when the coolant level remains stable.
- **Part cost**: Replacement expansion tank sourced from Amazon for approximately **€40** — considered affordable for a DIY repair.

---

### 🔧 Tools & Preparation

- **Tube clamps/clips**: Used to squeeze and pinch the coolant hoses before disconnecting them, minimizing coolant spillage.
- **DIY plugs**: Homemade plugs inserted into disconnected tubes to prevent coolant from draining freely during the swap.
- **Plastic sheeting**: Laid under the work area to catch inevitable coolant spills.
- **Access strategy**: Removing the **left headlight** (released via plastic locking tabs) significantly improves access to the tank's screws and lower hose — removing the grille is optional but can also help.

---

### 🛠️ Replacement Process

- **Step 1 – Create space**: Remove additional lights, the left headlight, and temporarily move aside the steering fluid reservoir and windscreen washer tank (clipped in, no tools needed).
- **Step 2 – Pinch hoses**: Clamp both coolant hoses before disconnecting to reduce spill.
- **Step 3 – Remove two mounting screws** holding the expansion tank in place.
- **Step 4 – Disconnect the sensor plug**: A **safety clip must be pulled down first** before the connector can be unplugged; the plug is hidden behind the tank.
- **Step 5 – Remove hoses**: Pull off the upper tube freely; the lower tube will cause some coolant spillage despite precautions — prepare accordingly.
- **Step 6 – Install new tank**: Align the tank with the screw points, reconnect the lower hose first, reattach the sensor plug (ensure safety clip re-engages), then refit all removed components.

---

### 🧪 Refilling the Coolant

- **Coolant mix**: **1:1 ratio** of coolant concentrate to water — product used was **Crystal Team G30**.
- **Top up to maximum** on the new tank, then start the engine; the level will visibly drop as coolant circulates through the system, requiring a second top-up.
- **Monitor the level** after the system is running to ensure it stabilises correctly.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

A stuck or failed float/sensor inside the coolant expansion tank is a common and inexpensive cause of false "coolant level low" warnings on the Land Rover Discovery 4. At ~€40 for the part and no specialist tools required, this is a practical DIY fix — provided the coolant level is actually stable and no real leak exists. The key challenges are managing coolant spill during hose removal and safely accessing the hidden sensor connector.
