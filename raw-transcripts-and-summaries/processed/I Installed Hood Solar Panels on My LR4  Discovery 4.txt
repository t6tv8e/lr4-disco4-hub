## Installing a Solar Panel on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A step-by-step walkthrough of mounting a 110W solar panel from Lanzan Solar onto a Land Rover Discovery 4, covering hood prep, decal application, panel mounting, and charge controller wiring.*

---

### 📦 What's in the Kit

- **Solar panel**: **110W** panel designed to fit the Discovery 4 hood profile
- **Charge controller**: **IP67-rated** (waterproof) MPPT/PWM converter with plug-and-play wiring, integrated fuse, and **Bluetooth app monitoring** for real-time power output
- **Decal (DK)**: Protective film applied under the panel to prevent paint damage and allow future removal
- **Squeegee tool**: Included with the decal kit for bubble-free application
- **Not included**: **3M double-sided tape** (needed to mount the panel) — costs approximately **€26–27** on Amazon

---

### 🪣 Hood Preparation & Decal Application

- **Clean first**: Use glass cleaner or isopropanol to degrease the hood before applying the decal
- **Installation conditions matter**: Minimum **10°C** ambient temperature required; avoid direct sunlight, especially on dark-colored vehicles
- **Soap + isopropanol trick**: Mix **5–6 drops of isopropanol** into soapy water — this helps displace water faster when squeegee-ing the decal flat
- **Application method**: Wet both the decal and hood thoroughly, position the decal, then work the squeegee **from the center outward** to eliminate bubbles
- **Heat helps**: A heat gun can assist in conforming stubborn edges of the decal
- **Final clean**: Wipe residue with isopropanol after application

---

### 🔧 Mounting the Solar Panel

- **Double-sided 3M tape** is applied to the back of the panel before placement
- **Two-person job**: Positioning and pressing the panel accurately requires a second person — strongly recommended
- **Painter's tape**: Used to mark the correct position before final adhesion
- **Sequence**: Place tape → position panel with painter's tape guides → peel backing → press firmly into place

---

### ⚡ Wiring the Charge Controller

- **Plug-and-play design**: Only two connections needed — **red (positive) to battery**, **black (negative) to vehicle chassis ground**
- **Placement**: Controller is zip-tied/double-taped near existing accessory wiring (alongside auxiliary light switches) for a clean install; will be permanently screwed into the plastic panel later
- **Cable routing**: Ensure cables do **not contact heat sources** (engine components) to avoid damage
- **App connectivity**: The controller pairs with a smartphone app to monitor solar production in real time (dedicated follow-up video planned)

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This install demonstrates a practical, reversible way to add solar charging to an overlanding or expedition vehicle without drilling or permanently damaging paintwork. The decal layer is the key protective detail that makes the setup non-destructive. The plug-and-play IP67 charge controller lowers the technical barrier significantly — the hardest part is the two-person panel placement.
