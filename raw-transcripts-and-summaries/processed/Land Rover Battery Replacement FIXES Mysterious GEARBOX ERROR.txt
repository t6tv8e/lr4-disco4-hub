## How to Replace the Main Battery on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*TLDR: A step-by-step guide to physically replacing the Discovery 4's main battery and using the IID diagnostic tool to register the new battery and relearn window positions.*

---

### 🔧 Why the Battery Was Replaced

- **Trigger**: Three gearbox errors appeared within two weeks, commonly linked to insufficient power supply from a weak battery.
- **Root cause**: The battery (a **Banner** brand unit) was already heavily depleted when the car was purchased — it had sat unused with no movement for an extended period.
- **Replacement unit**: A new battery from **Varta** was used.

---

### 🔩 Physical Replacement Process

- **Step 1**: Remove the **fuse box/battery cover** to access the battery terminals and bracket.
- **Step 2**: Disconnect the **minus (negative) cable first** to preserve the car's memory, secured with cable ties.
- **Step 3**: Disconnect the **plus (positive) cable** and remove the **venting hose** (simply pull it out).
- **Step 4**: Remove the **10mm bracket bolt** holding the battery in place, then lift the battery out — **back first, then front**.
- **Step 5**: On the new battery, ensure the **correct venting hole orientation** before installing (one hole is open, one is sealed).
- **Step 6**: Install in reverse — **back then front** — reconnect **negative first, then positive**, reattach the vent hose, and replace the cover.

---

### 💻 Post-Replacement: IID Tool Registration

- **Battery registration is mandatory**: The Discovery 4 must be told a new battery has been fitted via the **IID (iCarsoft) diagnostic tool**, otherwise the car continues managing charging based on the old battery's profile.
- **Navigation path**: `Service Test → PCM Body Control → Battery Replacement` → run the routine → wait **~9 seconds** → tool automatically **clears all fault codes**.
- **Window relearn via IID**: The tool also offers a **driver and passenger door glass learn routine** (front and rear) — it automatically cycles each window down and back up to relearn its positions.

---

### 🪟 Manual Window Relearn (Without the Tool)

- After a battery disconnect, the car **forgets window calibration** — windows will go down but not auto-close upward.
- **Manual fix**: Press window down → fully close manually → release → close again until you hear a **click** → window auto-function is restored.
- Must be repeated on **all four windows**.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Replacing a Discovery 4 battery is a straightforward DIY job, but **simply swapping the battery is not enough** — using a diagnostic tool to register the new battery and relearn window positions is essential to avoid lingering errors and restore full functionality. Skipping the registration step can leave fault codes active and cause continued electrical or gearbox-related warnings.
