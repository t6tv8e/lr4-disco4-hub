## How to Calibrate Air Suspension on a Land Rover Discovery 4 Using the GAP IID Tool

*TLDR: You can calibrate your Land Rover Discovery 4's air suspension at home in ~10 minutes using the GAP IID tool, saving at least €200 compared to dealership prices.*

---

### 💰 Cost Comparison: DIY vs. Dealership

- **Dealership pricing is steep**: Deleting an error code costs ~**€100**, air suspension calibration costs ~**€200 minimum**, and adding a new key costs ~**€450**.
- **GAP IID tool costs €550** but pays for itself after just two dealer visits' worth of work (e.g., one calibration + one key programming).
- **Aftermarket alternatives exist for other jobs too**: Tire pressure sensor setup quoted at **€400 by Land Rover** for sensors alone; aftermarket sensors cost only **€70**.

---

### 🛠️ What You Need Before Starting

- **Flat surface** to park the car (doesn't need to be perfectly level, but close).
- **Painter's tape and a measuring tape** to mark the center of each rim for consistent height measurements.
- **GAP IID Bluetooth OBD2 tool** plugged into the OBD2 port (located above the brake pedal).
- **Car at standard ride height**: If you have a lift module (e.g., XLifter or Mud Tech module), set it to default first.
- **Sufficient air pressure** in the suspension tank and adequate battery voltage — start the engine if either is low.
- **Windows open** for a stable Bluetooth connection between the tool and your phone.

---

### 📐 Measurement Setup

- Divide each rim in half (**rim diameter = 52 cm → half = 26 cm**) and apply a horizontal strip of painter's tape at the midpoint.
- Measure from the **center of the wheel (tape mark) to the lower edge of the wheel arch** on all four corners.
- **Target heights**:
  - **Front wheels**: **466 mm**
  - **Rear wheels**: **458 mm**

---

### 📱 Step-by-Step Calibration Process

1. **Connect** the GAP IID tool to the OBD2 port and pair it via Bluetooth using the free app (available on Google Play / iOS App Store).
2. In the app, navigate: **Menu → Calibration → RLM Suspension → Guided Calibration** (easier than manual — no need to know target heights in advance).
3. The tool walks you through **all four corners in sequence**: Front Left → Front Right → Rear Right → Rear Left.
4. For each corner, **measure the height**, then use the **up/down buttons** in the app to raise or lower that corner until it matches the target.
5. Once all four corners are set, press **Next → Apply** to save the settings.
6. Finally, **cycle the suspension** (lower it down) to complete the calibration — confirmed by a "Operation Succeeded" message.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

A task that costs **€200+ at a Land Rover dealership and takes a technician ~10 minutes** can be done at home with basic tools and the **€550 GAP IID tool**. The tool recoup its cost quickly for any Discovery 4 owner who performs more than a couple of these procedures, making it a worthwhile investment for DIY Land Rover maintenance.
