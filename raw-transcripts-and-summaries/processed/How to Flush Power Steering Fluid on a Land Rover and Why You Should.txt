## How to Flush the Power Steering System on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*TLDR: A step-by-step guide to flushing and replacing the power steering fluid and expansion tank on a Land Rover Discovery 4, a maintenance task recommended every 2 years or 48,000 km.*

---

### 🔧 Why This Maintenance Matters

- **Fluid degradation**: Like engine oil and transmission fluid, power steering fluid loses its lubricating properties over time and must be replaced.
- **Failure prevention**: Neglecting this service risks losing power steering while driving — a serious safety hazard.
- **Service interval**: Land Rover recommends this procedure every **2 years or 48,000 km**.

---

### 🛒 Parts and Tools Required

- **Power steering fluid**: Minimum **4 liters** needed to flush the system (the system itself only holds **0.89 liters** — the rest is for flushing). Used: *Liqui-Moly ATF Top Tec 1800* (Land Rover approved, ~**€60 for 5L**).
- **New expansion tank**: The integrated filter inside the tank cannot be replaced separately, so the whole tank must be swapped. Aftermarket quality units cost ~**€30–€40**.
- **8mm hose + clamp**: Used to redirect the return hose into a collection canister during flushing.
- **Empty 4-liter canister**: Marked at the 4-liter line to know when flushing is complete.
- **Suction pump**: Used to extract the old fluid from the expansion tank before disconnecting hoses.
- **Funnel** (large capacity preferred) and **paper towels**.

---

### 🔩 Preparation Steps

- **Remove the front panel and right headlight** to gain easier access to the lower hose on the expansion tank.
- **Disconnect the windscreen washer tube** (secured by two clamps) to create more working space.
- **Suction out old fluid** from the expansion tank first — the old fluid was visibly very dark/black, indicating degradation.
- **Redirect the return hose**: Disconnect it from the expansion tank and connect the clear 8mm hose in its place, routing it into the marked collection canister. Secure with a clamp or cable tie.

---

### 🔄 Expansion Tank Replacement

- Remove the old tank, lubricate the hose connections on the new tank with a little fluid to ease fitting.
- **Transfer the rubber cap/plug** from the old tank to the new one to prevent spills during filling.
- **Transfer the mounting bracket** from the old tank, ensuring correct orientation.
- Mount the new tank temporarily (without final tightening) to make fluid filling easier.

---

### 💧 Flushing Procedure

- **Two people strongly recommended**: one filling fluid at the expansion tank, one inside the car turning the steering wheel.
- Fill the expansion tank to the **MAX level** before starting.
- Start the engine and turn the steering wheel **lock-to-lock**, approximately **one full rotation every 5 seconds**.
- The person outside must **continuously monitor the fluid level** and top up to prevent the pump running dry — this is critical.
- ⚠️ **Key lesson learned**: If using a funnel with a strainer, the strainer can restrict flow too much, starving the system. **Remove the funnel strainer** to ensure adequate flow rate during flushing.
- Continue until **4 liters** have passed through the system (monitor the collection canister).

---

### ✅ Final Steps

- Reconnect the return hose back to the new expansion tank and secure clamps.
- Re-seat the expansion tank in its final mounted position.
- Top up fluid to between the **MIN and MAX marks** — do not overfill or underfill.
- Refit the windscreen washer tube, headlight, and front panel.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This is a straightforward but important maintenance job that most Land Rover Discovery 4 owners overlook. The total parts cost is roughly **€90–€100** (fluid + expansion tank), and the biggest practical risk is running the power steering pump dry during flushing — always keep the reservoir topped up and ensure your fill funnel isn't restricting flow. Doing this job proactively prevents costly pump failure and maintains safe steering feel.
