## How to Replace Rear Brake Hoses on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A step-by-step DIY guide to removing, replacing, and bleeding rear brake hoses on a Land Rover Discovery 4, prompted by a dangerous bulge discovered on an existing hose.*

---

### 🔍 Why This Job Was Needed

- **Bulging brake hose**: A viewer spotted a visible bump/bulge on the rear brake hose in a previous video — a sign of internal hose failure that can lead to a blowout and total brake loss.
- **Emergency temporary fix**: Before the proper repair, a hose clamp was applied over the bulge as a **hotfix to contain the damage** — explicitly noted as not recommended, only used out of necessity.

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Parts Required

- **Replacement brake hoses**: Delphi Technologies brand, approximately **€20 per hose**.
- **DOT 4 brake fluid**: Specifically required by Land Rover; do not substitute with other ratings.
- **Brake bleeding bottle**: Inexpensive one-man bleeding kit with a rubber hose attachment.
- **Spanners**: **11mm** for the upper brake line fitting, **8mm** for the lower fitting.
- **Small plastic cup/paper towels**: To collect brake fluid spillage during removal.

---

### 🔧 Hose Removal Process

- **Clean the area first**: Dirt around brake fittings must be removed before opening the system to prevent contamination.
- **Upper fitting (11mm)**: Unscrew the brake line fitting and collect escaping fluid; a small amount is expected.
- **Retaining clip/holder**: Each hose is held by a metal clip/holder at both the top and bottom — this must be **pulled/pushed out** before the hose can be freed.
- **Lower fitting (8mm)**: Same process applies — unscrew fitting, remove retaining clip, extract hose.
- **Inspect fluid condition**: Clear, honey-colored fluid = good condition, no flush needed yet. **Dark brown fluid = flush the entire system immediately**.

---

### ✅ Hose Installation

- **Orientation matters**: The end fittings must be inserted in the **correct rotational position** or the retaining holder will not seat properly.
- **Secure the holder**: Confirm the retaining clip fully seats back into place before tightening the fitting screws.
- **Repeat on both sides**: The same procedure applies to the opposite rear wheel.

---

### 💧 Bleeding & Flushing the Brake System

- **Fill the reservoir first**: The brake fluid reservoir is located on the **driver's side under a cover**; top it up before bleeding begins.
- **Pre-fill the bleeding bottle**: Add a small amount of brake fluid into the bleeding bottle so the system doesn't suck air back in during the process.
- **Bleeding order**: Start at the **wheel furthest from the reservoir** and work toward the nearest — standard bleeding sequence for maximum air purging.
- **Bleeding point**: An 11mm bleeder nipple is located at each caliper; attach the rubber hose from the bleeding bottle before opening it.
- **All four corners were bled**: Even though only rear hoses were replaced, all four brake points were bled to ensure no air remained.
- **Full fluid flush completed**: Since the fluid was **over 3 years old** (Land Rover recommends replacement every 3 years), the entire system was flushed — approximately **750ml of old fluid** was expelled and replaced with fresh DOT 4.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

A bulging brake hose is a **critical safety failure** that must be addressed immediately — it signals imminent hose rupture and potential brake failure. This job requires only basic tools and ~€20 in parts per hose, making it an accessible DIY repair. Combining the hose replacement with a **full brake fluid flush** (especially if fluid is over 3 years old) maximizes the value of the job since the system is already open and being bled anyway.
