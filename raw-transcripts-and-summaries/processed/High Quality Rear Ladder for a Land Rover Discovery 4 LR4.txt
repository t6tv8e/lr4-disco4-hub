## Replacing a Rear Ladder on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A DIY walkthrough of swapping out an incompatible rear ladder for a compact Polish-made alternative that clears the Rhino Rack batwing awning.*

---

### 🔧 The Problem Being Solved

- **Root cause**: The original rear ladder conflicted with a **Rhino Rack batwing awning** — when the trunk or awning was opened, the ladder physically crashed into it.
- **Solution**: Replace the tall ladder with a shorter, more compact unit mounted higher and closer to the trunk window, clearing the awning entirely.

---

### 🪜 The New Ladder: MP4x4 (Polish Brand)

- **Source**: A Polish off-road accessories shop (**MP4x4**); not a sponsored purchase — bought independently.
- **Design**: Compact and mounts over the door handle area, sitting close to the rear window.
- **Price**: ~**€160**, compared to **~€470** for the equivalent **Front Runner** ladder.
- **Hardware quality**: Comes with **stainless steel bolts/screws**, unlike Front Runner which supplies black (non-stainless) hardware that rusts.
- **Build quality verdict**: Robust and well-made overall; minor criticism is the **foam quality on the back plate**, but otherwise considered excellent value.

---

### 🛠️ Installation Process

- **Tools needed**: **T30 Torx bit**, **10mm socket**, drill (as contingency), WD-40.
- **Step 1**: Remove **4 T30 screws** securing the old ladder handle/light bracket — severely rusted screws required WD-40 soaking (~30 min) and ultimately **drilling one out**.
- **Step 2**: Remove the **plastic trim cover** and locate a hidden additional small screw.
- **Step 3**: Disconnect **3 × 10mm bolts** and unplug the **camera cable connector** before fully removing the old assembly.
- **Step 4**: Apply **foam adhesive tape** to the new ladder's contact points to protect the paintwork from metal-on-paint scratching.
- **Step 5**: Route cables back through, reinstall the **3 × 10mm bolts**, fit the new ladder bracket, and secure with the supplied stainless screws.
- **Step 6**: Refit plastic trim carefully, aligning plastic clips on both left and right sides, and replace the trim screw.

---

### ⚖️ Old vs. New Ladder Comparison

- **Old ladder advantage**: Extended lower, making it slightly easier to step onto initially.
- **New ladder advantage**: Compact size avoids awning conflict; stainless hardware resists rust; better long-term fitment.
- **Planned improvement**: Adding **grip tape (sandpaper tape)** to the new ladder steps, replicating a grip strip already on the old ladder.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

For Discovery 4 owners running a rooftop awning system, a full-size rear ladder is a genuine functional conflict. The **MP4x4 compact ladder at €160** offers a practical, well-built fix at **less than 35% of the Front Runner price**, with superior stainless hardware — making it a strong value alternative for budget-conscious overlanders.
