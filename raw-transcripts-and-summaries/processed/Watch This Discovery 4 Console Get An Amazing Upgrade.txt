## DIY Center Console Cooler Upgrade for Land Rover Discovery 4

*A straightforward plug-and-play guide to replacing the stock center console storage box with a factory-style cooler/fridge unit in a Land Rover Discovery 4.*

---

### 🔧 What the Upgrade Is

- **Factory cooler retrofit**: Replaces the standard center console storage box with an OEM-style cooling unit — a feature the creator wished they had from the start.
- **Plug-and-play wiring**: The **Discovery 4** (and likely **Range Rover Sport**) already has a pre-run cable beneath the center console specifically for this cooler, meaning **no custom wiring is needed**.
- **Unanswered question**: The creator recalls seeing a version that can both **heat and cool** but couldn't find it — asks viewers to share links if they know of it.

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Parts Required

- **T40 Torx** for rear bolts on the armrest
- **T20 Torx** for front screws on the inner box
- **Replacement center console cooler unit** (OEM fitment)
- No specialist tools beyond standard Torx bits

---

### 📋 Step-by-Step Installation

- **Step 1 – Remove cup holder**: Lift and pull out the cup holder insert.
- **Step 2 – Remove water protection plate**: Unclip and set aside (note: pre-owned vehicles may have this already modified).
- **Step 3 – Unscrew rear armrest bolts**: Use **T40 Torx**; moving the passenger seat forward improves access.
- **Step 4 – Unclip and remove armrest cover**: Pry up clips on both sides and pull the cover straight out.
- **Step 5 – Remove armrest assembly**: Two front screws, then disconnect the **gray** and **green** connectors underneath before lifting free.
- **Step 6 – Extract original storage box**: Pull upward — resistance comes from plastic clips on the bottom, not additional screws.
- **Step 7 – Install new cooler**: Seat the alignment pins (front and rear) into place, then plug in the pre-existing cable.
- **Step 8 – Test before reassembly**: The cooler **requires the engine running** (ignition alone is insufficient) — the fan activates and the indicator light turns **green** within a few seconds of switching on.
- **Step 9 – Reassemble** in reverse order.

---

### ✅ Results & Observations

- The cooler **worked immediately** after engine start — green light confirmed active cooling.
- One clip on the creator's unit was already broken, slightly affecting the fit, but functionality was unaffected.
- Creator planned to verify cooling performance by chilling drinks and sharing results.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This is a **low-effort, high-reward OEM upgrade** for Discovery 4 and Range Rover Sport owners — no rewiring, no cutting, just Torx screws and a pre-existing connector. For anyone who wants in-cabin drink cooling without aftermarket solutions, this factory fitment swap is one of the cleanest options available.
