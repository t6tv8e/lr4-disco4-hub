## Rock Slider Replacement on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A DIY walkthrough of replacing worn, rusty rock sliders on a Land Rover Discovery 4 with new MP4x4 units, including prep, mounting, and finishing tips.*

---

### 🔧 Why the Replacement Was Needed

- **Aggressive tires causing damage**: BFGoodrich off-road tires were throwing stones and repeatedly destroying the original rock sliders, forcing an annual rust restoration — a cycle the owner wanted to end.
- **Switching tire setup**: The owner is reverting to **19-inch rims with Goodyear Wrangler tires** to reduce the stone-throwing problem, and plans to repaint the rims to match the door handle color.
- **Additional damage**: The Discovery 4 was also involved in a minor parking collision that week, with workshop repairs scheduled within **two weeks**.

---

### 📦 What Comes With the MP4x4 Rock Sliders

- **Sponsored by MP4x4**: The new sliders are branded with the MP4x4 logo and come as a complete kit.
- **Hardware included**: A full package of bolts and screws is provided — **two types**: round-head bolts for the top mounting points and standard bolts for the bottom.
- **Notable design feature**: The new sliders include a **rear cap/cover** that prevents dirt ingress — a feature absent on the old sliders and highlighted as a significant quality improvement.

---

### 🛠️ Installation Process

- **Removing old sliders**: Straightforward unbolting; the area beneath revealed rust and significant dirt buildup.
- **If starting from scratch (no prior sliders)**: A plastic cover sits in place of the rock slider — it is clipped on and must be removed first to expose the mounting holes underneath.
- **Cleaning before final install**: After dry-fitting, the owner recommends jet-washing the underbody to remove dirt and treating any rust before permanent mounting.
- **Bolt prep**: Adding **grease to the bolts** before final installation is recommended for easier fitment and corrosion resistance.
- **Dry-fit first**: The sliders were mounted once to check fit, then removed for a **paint/rust treatment job** on the bodywork before final installation.
- **Ease of installation**: Described as straightforward with no special tools mentioned beyond basic hand tools.

---

### ✅ End Result

- **Wide stepping surface**: The new sliders are notably wide, making it easy to step up and enter the vehicle.
- **Improved protection and aesthetics**: The finished install is visually clean and functionally more robust than the previous setup.
- **Quality assessment**: Solid construction, good hardware inclusion, and the dirt-sealing rear cap make these a clear upgrade over the originals.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This video matters for **Land Rover Discovery 4 owners** running off-road builds — it demonstrates that aggressive tire choices can have a cascading maintenance cost (frequent rust restoration), and that switching tires alongside upgrading to better-quality, sealed rock sliders is a practical long-term solution. The key practical tip: **always jet-wash and treat rust before final mounting**, and **dry-fit first** to confirm alignment before committing.
