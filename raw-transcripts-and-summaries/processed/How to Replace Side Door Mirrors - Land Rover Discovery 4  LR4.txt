## DIY Land Rover Exterior Mirror Replacement & Fault Diagnosis with GAP IID Tool

*A Land Rover owner demonstrates how to diagnose short-circuit faults in heated exterior mirrors using the GAP IID diagnostic tool and replace both mirrors for ~€60 instead of paying a dealer ~€500.*

---

### 🔧 The Problem

- **Fault**: Both exterior mirrors (driver and passenger side) had a **short circuit** in the heater output circuit, causing the heated mirrors to stop working in winter.
- **Diagnostic codes**: "DDM Driver Door – Mirror Heater Output General Electric Failure – Circuit Short Battery or Open" on both sides.
- **Detection tool**: The **GAP IID Tool** (available at gap-diagnostics.com), connected via the **OBD2 port** under the steering wheel and operated through a smartphone app (iOS & Android).

---

### 💶 Cost Comparison

- **Dealer cost**: ~**€200–€250 per mirror** = **€500 total** at a Land Rover dealership.
- **DIY cost**: ~**€25–€30 per mirror** (aftermarket, purchased on eBay) = **~€60 total**.
- **Savings**: Approximately **€440** by doing it yourself.
- **GAP IID Tool cost**: ~**€550**, but it pays for itself quickly by enabling multiple DIY repairs and diagnostics.

---

### 🛠️ How to Replace the Mirror (Step-by-Step)

- **Only tool needed**: A flathead screwdriver — no special equipment required.
- **Step 1**: Turn on the ignition and use the mirror adjustment controls to angle the mirror fully outward so the edge protrudes, making the interior clips accessible.
- **Step 2**: Insert a screwdriver carefully behind the mirror glass to **press the retaining clips** (there are two) inward — do this gently to avoid damage.
- **Step 3**: The mirror glass **pops out easily** once both clips are released.
- **Step 4**: **Unplug the electrical connectors** (cables may be tight — patience required).
- **Step 5**: Plug connectors into the new mirror, align the **pins with the pin holes on all four sides**, then push the mirror firmly back into place — avoid excessive force.
- **Repeat** for the other side.

### ✅ Verification

- After replacing both mirrors, re-scanning with the GAP IID Tool showed **zero fault codes** — confirming the repair was successful.
