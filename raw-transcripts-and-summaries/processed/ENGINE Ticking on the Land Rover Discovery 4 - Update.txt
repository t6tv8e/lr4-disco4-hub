## Land Rover Discovery 4 Build Update: Exterior Mods, Interior Restoration & Engine Diagnosis

*TLDR: Peter walks through all completed and planned upgrades on his Land Rover Discovery 4, from off-road lighting and underbody protection to interior restoration, while sharing a promising diagnosis on the mysterious engine ticking noise.*

---

### 🔦 Exterior Upgrades Completed

- **Off-road Xenon lights**: Added to the front, plus new **LED fog lights with E-mark approval**, meaning they are street-legal and adjustable.
- **Underbody protection plate**: A **5mm two-part steel plate from MP 4x4** installed under the front section — heavy enough to require a lifting aid, secured with ~6 bolts.
- **Roof rack (MP 4x4)**: Tested up to **120+ km/h on the motorway** — noticeably quieter than a Front Runner rack. Peter emailed MP 4x4 about missing mounting nuts; the company responded by **adding M6 and M8 nut sets to all future packages** — a direct customer-driven product improvement.
- **Rock sliders (MP 4x4)**: Feature a **closed end plate to prevent dirt ingress** and drainage holes along the full length. Peter added grip tape himself. Previously used the same sliders on another Discovery 4 with good results.
- **Mud flaps**: Aftermarket plastic versions fitted for ~**€30**, preferred over rubber for aesthetics.
- **Rear ladder (MP 4x4)**: Improved version — company added **adhesive foam padding** (scratch protection) and grip tape after Peter's feedback on an earlier video.

---

### 🪑 Interior Restoration

- **Door card**: Repaired a burned/damaged panel; a rusted piece will be swapped out with a spare using just a screwdriver.
- **Seat cushions**: Replaced on both **driver and passenger sides** — no more holes, looks significantly better.
- **Center console**: Installed an **X-Lifter** (dedicated video in production).
- **Button panel & steering wheel**: A replacement panel with buttons has been sourced; airbag must be removed first. Steering wheel leather restoration also planned.
- **LED conversion**: **Every interior and exterior bulb** replaced with LEDs — doors, number plates, and cabin lighting.
- **Climate system cleaning**: Used a **foam cleaning spray** injected into every vent, then ran heat for 20 minutes — fully eliminated dog/cigarette odors. Product linked in description.
- **Interior roof**: Cleaned with a **Sonax product (~€8)** that removed deep-set dirt and black scuff marks effectively.

---

### ⚙️ Mechanical Work & Engine Diagnosis

- **Gearbox service**: Hard, jarring shifts under full throttle were resolved with a **full gearbox service** — car now shifts smoothly. Not cheap, but described as well worth it.
- **Engine ticking noise — new diagnosis**: Workshop (with prior experience of this issue) is **~90% confident the cause is a leaking copper sealing ring between the injector and the engine block**, rather than a worn timing chain.
- **Why the chain is likely ruled out**: A worn cam chain typically **rattles from cold start**; this ticking only appears **after the engine warms up**, pointing away from chain wear.
- **Other possibilities still open**: A faulty **injector** itself, or potentially a **fan issue** (suggested by viewers). Workshop will do a follow-up inspection to confirm.
- **Hydrolock concern from previous video**: Peter no longer believes hydrolock is the root cause.

---

### 🔧 Upcoming Plans

- **Paint correction/exterior repaint**: Deferred until warmer weather — acknowledged as the car's most visible remaining issue.
- **Automatic wiper fault**: Rain sensor not functioning — likely due to a **non-original windscreen misaligned with the interior mirror sensor pad**. A **replacement gel pad** has been ordered. Peter will not replace the entire windscreen for this feature if the pad fix fails.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This update is valuable for any Land Rover Discovery 4 owner or overlanding enthusiast because it combines **practical budget restoration tips** (seat covers, LED swaps, foam climate cleaning) with **real-world diagnosis of common D4 issues** like gearbox harshness and injector/timing noise. The MP 4x4 product feedback loop — where customer emails directly improve future product packaging — is also a noteworthy example of a responsive aftermarket supplier worth knowing about.
