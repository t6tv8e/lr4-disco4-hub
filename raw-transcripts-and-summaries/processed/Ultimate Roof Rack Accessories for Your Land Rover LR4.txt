## MP 4x4 Roof Rack: How to Mount Accessories Using Sliding Nuts & Brackets

*TLDR: Peter demonstrates how surprisingly easy it is to mount accessories on an MP 4x4 roof rack using spring-loaded sliding nuts, and installs sandboard brackets, mounting plates, and awning brackets.*

---

### 🔧 The Sliding Nut System (Key Feature)

- **Spring-loaded sliding nuts**: Each nut contains a ball and spring, allowing it to be pressed sideways into the roof rack rail, rotated, and locked in place — no tools required for insertion or removal.
- **Available sizes**: Nuts come in **M6 and M8** thread sizes, compatible with standard accessories.
- **Correction from prior video**: Peter previously stated mounting things was difficult — this system proves otherwise, and he credits MP 4x4 for sending the nuts and brackets.

---

### 📦 Accessories Unboxed

- **Sandboard mounting brackets**: Dedicated brackets designed to hold recovery boards on top of the roof rack.
- **Heavy car lifter brackets**: For mounting a Hi-Lift or similar jack securely to the rack.
- **Awning brackets**: Front and rear brackets enabling side-mounted awning installation; Peter plans to buy an awning after poor experience with a **Rhino Rack** unit whose plastic parts broke easily.
- **Large mounting plates**: Versatile flat plates that allow almost any gear (e.g., jerrycans/military canisters) to be strapped or bolted down.

---

### 🏗️ Installation Process

- **Sandboard brackets**: Two sliding nuts placed at the front and two at the rear; brackets are bolted down with **M8 bolts**, which Peter considers more than sufficient. Brackets were moved slightly rearward to prevent sandboards from protruding over the windshield and causing wind noise.
- **Mounting plates**: Positioned toward the front of the rack to hold two restored military-style fuel canisters, secured with straps threaded front-to-back.
- **Awning brackets**: Installed front and rear on the side rail using the same sliding nut method; described as very stable once tightened.

---

### 📐 Roof Rack Specs (MP 4x4 Aluminium Version)

- **Weight**: **20 kg**
- **Load capacity per bar (static)**: **75 kg at center** per bar; up to **400 kg static** total
- **Dynamic load limit (Land Rover Discovery 4)**: **100 kg while moving** (vehicle manufacturer restriction)
- **Height added**: **~11.5 cm** at rear, **~16.5 cm** at front
- **Width**: **123.5 cm**
- **Length** (including front windshield angle): **228 cm**
- **Construction**: Powder-coated aluminium with **stainless steel hardware only** — no black steel screws that rust (noted as an advantage over Front Runner racks)

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

The MP 4x4 roof rack's **spring-loaded sliding nut system** makes it genuinely tool-free and flexible to mount accessories anywhere along the rails — a significant practical advantage. Combined with all-aluminium construction, powder coating, and stainless steel fasteners, Peter argues it offers **strong value for money** compared to competitors. For overlanders looking to mount sandboards, awnings, jerrycans, or jacks, this system provides modular versatility without compromising structural integrity.
