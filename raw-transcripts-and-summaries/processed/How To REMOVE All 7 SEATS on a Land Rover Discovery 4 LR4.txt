## Removing All Seats from a Land Rover Discovery 4 for Interior Deep Clean

*A practical walkthrough of removing all 7 seats from a Discovery 4 to access and clean carpets affected by cigarette smoke odor.*

---

### 🎯 Why This Job Was Done

- **Motivation**: The previous owner was a smoker, leaving a persistent smell in the cabin; removing all seats was necessary to fully clean and dry the carpet.
- **Timing note**: Winter is not ideal for this job since the interior must be washed and then dried with a heater, but the smell made it urgent.

---

### 🪑 Front Seats (Row 1 – Driver & Passenger)

- **Cover removal first**: Each seat has multiple plastic covers held by strong metal clips — one on the side, one on the front, and one on the back. Clips can crack in cold weather, so extra care is needed.
- **Bolt pattern**: **4 bolts total** — 2 at the front of the seat rail, 2 at the rear. A **T50 Torx** bit is needed for the seatbelt anchor.
- **Electrical connectors**: Hidden underneath the seat; the seat must be lifted (using a piece of wood, for example) to access and unplug them before removal.
- **Extraction tip**: Slide the seat forward or backward to ease maneuvering it out of the door opening — it is a tight fit.

---

### 🪑 Second Row Seats (Row 2 – 3 Seats)

- **Each seat has 4 bolts**: 2 in the front, 2 in the rear.
- **Outer seats first**: The center seat's rear bolts are located on the outer seats' mounting area, so the left and right seats must come out before the middle one.
- **Heated seat connectors**: Outer second-row seats are heated and have electrical connectors underneath that must be unplugged; the **center seat is not heated** and has no connector.
- **Damper assist**: A built-in damper helps lift the seat bottom — fold the seatback fully down before removing to gain access to rear bolts.
- **Bullet/locating pins**: Small alignment pins on the floor locate each seat precisely — lifting seats **straight up** is critical to avoid snapping these pins.

---

### 🪑 Third Row Seats (Row 3 – 2 Seats)

- **Mounting covers**: Plastic covers on the left and right sides must be removed first to expose the mounting screws.
- **Bolt locations**: Screws are found under covers at the front and rear of each seat mounting point — **2 screws per mounting side**.
- **Button/switch panel**: The seat-folding function button panel is mounted to the car body itself, not the seat cover, so the cover can be removed without damaging the switch mechanism.
- **Fragile covers**: Third-row seat covers break very easily — slow, careful removal is advised.
- **Future mod consideration**: The host is considering adding a **slide-out drawer** in the boot area while retaining the 7-seat function, but notes the challenge of working around the seat mounting rails.

---

### 🔧 Key Tools & Tips

- **T50 Torx bit** for seatbelt anchor bolts
- Use a **piece of wood** to prop seats up when accessing hidden connectors
- **Warm conditions preferred** — clips are brittle in cold weather and crack easily
- Always lift seats **straight up** off locating/bullet pins rather than angling them out
- Fold seatbacks **fully flat** on row 2 and 3 seats to access rear bolts more easily

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This video is a practical reference for anyone needing to deep-clean a **Land Rover Discovery 4** interior or undertake seat removal for any reason. The process is more involved than typical vehicles due to multiple hidden clips, electrical connectors for heated seats, fragile alignment pins, and tight extraction angles — making a methodical, cover-first approach essential to avoid breaking components.
