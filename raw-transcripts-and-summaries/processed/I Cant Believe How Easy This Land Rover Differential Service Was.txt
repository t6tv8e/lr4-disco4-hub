## Land Rover Discovery 4: Front & Rear Differential Oil Change Guide

*A practical walkthrough of changing differential oils on a Land Rover Discovery 4, including fluid specs, torque values, and maintenance intervals.*

---

### 🔧 Why Change Differential Oil

- **Land Rover's official interval**: Every **10 years / 240,000 km (150,000 miles)** for the front and open rear differential.
- **Electronic Torque Managed (ETM) rear differential**: Requires more frequent changes — every **5 years / 120,000 km (75,000 miles)**.
- **Applies to all variants**: Both petrol and diesel Discovery 4 models follow the same schedule.
- **Real-world risk**: Skipping changes allows metallic particles to accumulate, accelerating wear and leading to expensive differential failure.

---

### 🛢️ Oil Selection & Quantities

- **Land Rover recommended oil**: **75W-90**
- **Peter's preferred alternative**: **75W-140**, chosen for its superior **temperature-viscosity behavior** (better kinematic viscosity across a wider temperature range), including cold climates — validated by 3–4 years of use on a previous Discovery 4 with no issues.
- **Cost difference**: 75W-140 is approximately **£3–£5 more expensive** per bottle (~£30/bottle).
- **Fluid quantities**:
  - Front differential: **~0.56 L**
  - Rear open differential: **~1.8 L**
  - Rear ETM differential: **~1.72 L**

---

### ⚙️ Procedure & Key Details

- **Critical rule**: Always remove the **filling plug first**, then the drain plug — if the drain plug is removed first and the filling plug is seized, you cannot refill the differential.
- **Front differential**: Filling plug uses an **8mm hex/Allen key**; located near two large bushings on the front axle.
- **Rear differential**: Located behind the spare wheel; both plugs noted as heavily rusted — replacement plugs are recommended.
- **Drain plug tool**: **38mm oil key** used on both front and rear.
- **Torque specs**:
  - 38mm drain plug: **28 Nm**
  - Hex filling plug: **54 Nm**
- **Filling method**: A small hand pump is necessary as a direct-pour bottle cannot reach the filling hole easily; expect the process to take time.

---

### 🔍 What the Drained Oil Reveals

- **Front differential drain plug**: Visibly coated in **metallic particles** — a clear sign of internal wear accumulation over time.
- **Rear differential oil**: Appeared relatively cleaner, though fine metal particles were still present in the drained fluid.
- **Rust particles** in the rear drain were attributed to the underbody, not the differential itself — distinguishing the two is important when assessing differential health.
- **Key insight**: The presence of metal particles supports changing oil **more frequently than Land Rover's maximum interval** of 240,000 km.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Differential oil changes are a low-cost, DIY-friendly maintenance task that directly prevents catastrophic (and expensive) differential failure. The metal debris found on the drain plugs demonstrates that even within Land Rover's recommended intervals, significant wear material accumulates — making a case for **proactive, more frequent changes**, especially if the vehicle is used in demanding conditions.
