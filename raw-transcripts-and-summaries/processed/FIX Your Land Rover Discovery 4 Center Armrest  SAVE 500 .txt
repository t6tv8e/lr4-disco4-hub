## Land Rover Discovery 4 Center Armrest Handle Replacement (Budget Fix)

*A DIY guide to replacing the broken center console armrest handle on a Land Rover Discovery 4 using a cheap AliExpress alternative instead of the expensive OEM part.*

---

### 💰 The Cost Problem & Solution

- **OEM Rip-off**: Land Rover only sells the **entire armrest assembly** — not just the handle — forcing owners to spend **€500–€600** for a single broken component.
- **AliExpress Alternative**: The replacement handle was sourced for approximately **€1**, making it over **500x cheaper** than the dealer option.
- **Visual Difference**: The aftermarket part has a **glossy chrome finish** vs. the original's **brushed aluminum look** — noticeable but acceptable at that price point. Painting with a **clear matte coat** can close the gap.

---

### 🔧 Tools Required

- **T40 Torx** — for removing the armrest from the center console
- **TX20 Torx** — for the screws on the handle cover
- **Flat-head screwdriver** — to pop out rubber bumpers and pry the cover
- **Pipe wrench** — to remove and reinstall the internal springs

---

### 🪛 Step-by-Step Removal Process

- **Prepare the workspace**: Slide both front seats rearward to gain access to the center console.
- **Remove the armrest**: Pull up the two cover caps, then lift the entire armrest assembly. It is secured with **T40 Torx bolts** underneath the caps.
- **Identify the damage**: The original handle suffered from **peeling foil** (sharp enough to cut skin) and a **broken mounting pin**.
- **Remove rubber bumpers**: Pry them out carefully with a screwdriver — note they have alignment pins and must be reseated correctly.
- **Remove TX20 screws** securing the handle cover, then carefully pry off the cover, being aware of an internal spring.
- **Release the clip**: A small pin/clip on the side of the handle assembly must be pressed out to free the handle holder — this is the trickiest step.

---

### 🔄 Handle Swap & Reassembly

- **Transfer the springs**: Remove springs from the old handle using a pipe wrench and install them into the new handle in the correct orientation — they must seat onto specific pins or the mechanism won't function.
- **Reattach the clip**: Slide the clip upward first, then press the round retaining section inward to lock it — this step requires patience if done for the first time.
- **Verify function before reassembly**: Test the handle mechanism (open/close action) before putting the cover back on.
- **Reinstall in reverse order**: Cover → rubber bumpers → armrest → T40 bolts → seat caps.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This repair matters because **dealer pricing on small cosmetic components can be exploitative**, and this video demonstrates that a functional, near-identical fix is achievable for under €1 with basic tools. The full OEM part number is linked in the video description for reference.
