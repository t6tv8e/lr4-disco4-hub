## Installing the X Lifter Module on a Land Rover Discovery 4

*A step-by-step walkthrough of installing the X Lifter air suspension control module on a Land Rover Discovery 4, covering what's in the box, installation process, calibration, and key features.*

---

### 📦 What's in the Box

- **Contents**: X Lifter ECU unit, control display unit, connecting cable (plugs between the new module and the original suspension ECU), stickers, and a manual.
- **App support**: A free **X Lifter app** (iOS & Android) mirrors all on-device settings and allows remote control and calibration.

---

### 🔧 Installation Process

- **Access point**: The original air suspension ECU is located on the **driver's side** behind two plastic trim panels — one held by metal clips, one by two screws and pins (note: a foot-light connector is attached to the lower panel).
- **Wiring**: Only **three connectors** need to be unplugged from the original ECU — the **far left** and **far right** only (the middle connector is left alone). The X Lifter cable plugs in between, acting as an interceptor module.
- **Power**: The control unit is powered via the **cigarette lighter/12V socket** — no hardwiring required for basic setup.
- **Time & difficulty**: The entire install is described as straightforward — remove two panels, swap three connectors, plug in power. No specialist tools needed.
- **Mounting**: X Lifter provides **3D-printable mount files** on their website for various interior locations (e.g., near the gear selector or steering wheel trim gap).

---

### ⚙️ Calibration & Setup

- **First boot**: The unit displays "Not Calibrated" — calibration requires a **flat, even surface with no bumps**.
- **Vehicle selection**: User selects car model (e.g., Discovery 4, 2009–2017) and **drive side** (LHD/RHD), then initiates calibration — completes automatically within seconds.
- **Service menu access**: Hold any button while plugging in power to enter the hidden **service menu**.

---

### 🚗 Key Features & Settings

- **Lift range**: **-30 mm to +75 mm** relative to standard ride height, giving a total adjustment range of **105 mm**.
- **7 preset lift modes**: Includes increments of **+25 mm, +50 mm, +55 mm**, and a **"Sky Rocket" mode at +75 mm**.
- **Aesthetic lift**: A subtle **+15 mm** preset for a mild visual and practical height gain.
- **Speed limiter override**: Unlike the factory system, the X Lifter allows driving in **off-road height above 40 km/h**.
- **Auto-leveling**: The car can automatically **level itself on uneven terrain** (e.g., campsites) — leveling angle tolerance is approximately **4–12°**.
- **Trailer assist**: Raises or lowers the **rear of the vehicle** independently to align with a trailer hitch — useful for solo hitching.
- **Recovery tools** (in service menu): Includes **ABS recovery** and **suspension lock** functions — helpful when field errors occur on the suspension or ABS sensors.
- **Compressor temperature monitor**: Real-time display of air compressor temp to avoid overheating.
- **Default lift setting**: A preferred ride height can be saved as the default startup position.
- **Firmware updates**: Supported directly through the device menu.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

The X Lifter is a **plug-and-play air suspension controller** that significantly expands the factory suspension capabilities of Land Rover Discovery/Range Rover Sport models — adding greater lift range, speed-limit override in raised modes, auto-leveling, and recovery tools. The install requires **no permanent modifications**, takes under an hour, and the interface is intuitive enough to configure without a manual. For overlanders or owners wanting more ride height for larger tires, it represents a practical, reversible upgrade.
