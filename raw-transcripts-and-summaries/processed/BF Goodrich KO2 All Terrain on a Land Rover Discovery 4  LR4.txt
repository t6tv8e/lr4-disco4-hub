## BFGoodrich All-Terrain Tires on a Land Rover Discovery 4: Long-Term Review

*A real-world owner reviews BFGoodrich all-terrain tires after 25,000 miles of mixed on-road and off-road use.*

---

### 🔍 Usage Profile & Context

- **Drive split**: **70% on-road / 30% off-road**, primarily weekends and vacations rather than daily off-roading
- **Vehicle**: Land Rover Discovery 4 (diesel), fitted with **265/65 R18** tires on 18-inch rims
- **Terrain tested**: Italian Alps, sharp rocky terrain in Croatia

---

### ✅ Off-Road Performance

- **Rocky terrain capability**: Handled sharp rocks in Croatia and Alpine trails with no punctures or damage
- **Reinforced sidewalls**: A key safety feature — the sidewalls are over **1 cm thick**, meaning even if a centimeter of rubber is cut away on rocks, the tire remains functional
- **Motorsport pedigree**: The tire has roots in motorsport and is used at the **Baja 1000** rally in California, which the owner discovered after purchasing

---

### 🛣️ On-Road Performance

- **First two years**: Performed well in all conditions including wet roads and snow
- **After two years**: Wet and cold weather grip noticeably degrades — the rubber hardens over time, reducing street traction
- **Winter use**: Owner switches to dedicated winter tires in the cold season, meaning these are not used year-round

---

### ⛽ Fuel Consumption Impact

- **Baseline diesel consumption**: ~**10–10.5 L/100km** in hilly/highway driving
- **Penalty with these tires**: Adds a **minimum of 1.5L, typically ~2L/100km** over standard tires due to the tires' heavy weight and high rolling resistance
- **Key takeaway**: Buyers should budget for meaningfully higher fuel costs — these are not efficient highway tires

---

### 🔧 Tread Life & Durability

- **After 25,000 miles**: Tread depth still measures **7–8 mm**, verified using a 2-euro coin
- **Assessment**: Tread wear is impressively low for the mileage, suggesting strong long-term durability

---

### 💰 Pricing

- **Cost in Europe**: **€250–€300 per tire** in 265/65 R18 size — considered expensive but worth it by the owner
- **Owner verdict**: Would buy again — is already planning to repurchase for the next season

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

BFGoodrich all-terrain tires are a strong choice for Discovery 4 owners who mix serious off-road use with regular road driving. Their standout strengths are **exceptional sidewall protection, impressive tread longevity (7–8mm after 25k miles), and proven rocky terrain capability**. The main trade-offs are a **~2L/100km fuel economy penalty** and **degrading wet-road grip after two years of aging rubber** — factors worth weighing against the premium price.
