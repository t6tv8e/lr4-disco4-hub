## Upgrading a Land Rover Discovery 4 with Auto High Beam Assist Mirror + IID Tool Programming

*A step-by-step guide to installing an aftermarket auto high beam interior mirror on a Land Rover Discovery 4 and activating it via IID Tool coding.*

---

### 🛒 Parts & Cost

- **Aftermarket mirror with auto high beam**: Purchased secondhand for ~**€120** (new price: **€450–€600** depending on source)
- **Replacement cover**: Required if your existing cover lacks the cutout for the camera/sensor housing
- **Bonus feature discovered**: The mirror also includes a **HomeLink system** (3 buttons for garage/gate control) — not needed in this case but included at no extra cost
- **Part numbers** are provided in the video description for easy sourcing

---

### 🔧 Tools Required

- **Small flathead screwdriver** or plastic trim removal tool (to unclip mirror covers)
- **IID Tool** (Diagnostic/coding tool for Land Rover) with smartphone app — essential for activating the feature in the car's software

---

### 🪞 Installation Steps

- **Remove mirror covers**: Use a screwdriver or trim tool to pop the clips and slide covers off both sides
- **Unplug the connector**: Press the small locking pin on the connector and pull it free
- **Remove old mirror**: Rotate it **counterclockwise** to disengage the locking pins from the mount
- **Install new mirror**: Align the pins, push onto mount, and rotate **clockwise** to lock
- **Reconnect plug**: Same connector — plug straight in
- **Refit covers**: Start one side, reposition mirror, then click the other cover into place (fiddly but straightforward)
- **Total install time**: ~**10–15 minutes**

---

### 💻 IID Tool Programming (BCM Coding)

1. Open IID Tool app → **Car Config** → **Confirmed List** → load the list
2. Navigate to **BCM (Body Control Module)**
3. Enable **Auto High Beam 1** → set to **"Fit"**
4. Enable **Auto High Beam 2** → set to **"Enabled"**
5. Click **Upload Changes** → confirm → wait for the process to complete (ignition cycles automatically)
6. After upload: **start engine → turn off → restart** to apply changes
7. Verify in car menu: **Vehicle Setup → High Beam Assist** — the option now appears and is active
8. **Left/right-hand traffic setting** can be adjusted within the same menu

---

### 🚗 Real-World Test Results

- Tested at night driving at ~**30+ km/h**
- High beams **activated automatically** when no oncoming traffic detected
- System **switched off correctly** when other vehicles were present
- Confirmed fully functional after coding

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This upgrade delivers a **genuine OEM-level safety feature** (automatic high beam assist) for as little as **€120 secondhand**, in under **15 minutes of work**. The key enabler is the **IID Tool**, which unlocks factory features already present in the car's BCM but dormant without the correct hardware. For Discovery 4 owners, this represents exceptional value — a feature that costs **€450–€600 new** can be retrofitted cheaply with basic tools and a coding tool most Land Rover enthusiasts already own.
