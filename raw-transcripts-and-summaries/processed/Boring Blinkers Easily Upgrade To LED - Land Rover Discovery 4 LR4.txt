## How to Replace Door Turn Signals with LED Units (No Tools Required)

*TLDR: A quick DIY guide showing how to swap out factory door-mounted turn signals for aftermarket LED units using only your hands.*

---

### 🔧 What You Need

- **Tools required**: None — the entire job is done with your fingers only
- **Replacement lights**: LED turn signals purchased from **Amazon for ~€30**, featuring a dark/blacked-out housing and a sequential LED sweep effect (light travels from back to front)

---

### 🚗 Removal Process

- **Step 1 – Push forward**: Press the existing turn signal forward (toward the front of the car) while it's still in the door
- **Step 2 – Pull out**: Once pushed forward, pull the unit outward to free it from the door
- **Step 3 – Release the clip**: A small retaining clip on the connector must be pressed to disconnect the wiring harness
- **Cable caution**: Keep the wiring cable pulled outside the door so it doesn't fall back into the door panel, making reconnection harder

---

### 💡 Installation Process

- **Orientation matters**: The retaining clip on the new unit must face the **front side** before inserting
- **Plug in first**: Connect the wiring harness before seating the light back into the door
- **Snap back in**: Push the unit back into the door until it seats securely
- **Repeat**: The second side uses the **exact same process**

---

### ✨ Old vs. New Lights

- **Old signal**: Standard single-bulb blinker with a basic on/off flash pattern
- **New LED signal**: Sequential sweep animation gives a modern, premium appearance similar to newer vehicle designs; housing is dark/smoked for a sportier look

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

This is an accessible, **zero-tool upgrade** that takes only minutes per side and noticeably modernizes a vehicle's appearance. At **~€30 for a pair**, the LED door turn signals offer a high visual impact-to-cost ratio for car enthusiasts looking for a simple cosmetic improvement.
