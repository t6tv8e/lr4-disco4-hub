## Installing an MP 4x4 Aluminium Roof Rack on a Land Rover Discovery 5

*A hands-on installation walkthrough of an MP 4x4 aluminium roof rack, including honest pros and cons compared to Front Runner.*

---

### 🔧 Product Background & Purchasing Decision

- **Why MP 4x4 over Front Runner**: The previous Front Runner rack on a black Discovery 4 was aluminium but used **steel screws**, which rusted over time and dripped rust stains onto the car's paintwork — unacceptable at that price point.
- **MP 4x4 advantage**: Their rack uses **stainless steel screws** and is **fully aluminium**, eliminating the rust problem.
- **Two versions available**: A steel version (~€200–300 cheaper) and the aluminium version; the aluminium version was chosen specifically to avoid future corrosion.
- **Price**: Approximately **€1,000** for the aluminium version (discount applied via MP 4x4).
- **Brand loyalty earned**: MP 4x4 previously responded to customer feedback about missing foam and sandpaper on a ladder order — and actually implemented those fixes in the next production run, demonstrating genuine customer responsiveness.

---

### 📦 Unboxing & Build Quality

- **Packaging**: Each aluminium profile is individually separated to prevent scratches — notably high-quality packaging.
- **Finish**: The rack is **powder coated and painted**, adding durability beyond raw aluminium.
- **Hardware included**: Stainless steel screws of various sizes (M6 50mm, M8 25mm, etc.), washers, and self-locking nuts.
- **Instructions**: A numbered parts plan is included; assembly is described as straightforward once you match parts to the diagram.

---

### 🏗️ Installation Process

- **Prep work**: Remove existing roof rail covers (held by **T20 screws** at front/back and **T30 screws** under plastic covers), then clean the roof thoroughly before mounting.
- **Assembly strategy**: Rather than pre-building on the ground, the team assembled **part by part directly on the roof** — starting with side walls, then middle rails, then the front windshield/deflector, and finally the front and rear bars.
- **Key challenge**: The roof is not flat — it tapers in a **V-shape from front to back**, meaning you cannot assume equal left-right measurements at each rail position. Every single rail must be measured individually.
- **Windshield/deflector**: Mounted with **six M6 50mm screws** with washers and self-locking nuts; a rubber seal underneath helps reduce wind noise and protects the roof.
- **Torque/tightening approach**: All screws were left slightly loose during assembly to allow alignment adjustments, then fully tightened at the end.

---

### 📊 Technical Specifications

- **Static load capacity**: **400 kg**
- **Dynamic load capacity (in motion)**: **200 kg**
- **Single crossbar load capacity**: **75 kg** per bar when mounted

---

### ✅ Pros & ❌ Cons

- **Pro**: Fully aluminium construction with stainless steel screws — **no rust risk**.
- **Pro**: Powder coated and painted finish for long-term durability.
- **Pro**: Strong load ratings (400 kg static / 200 kg dynamic).
- **Pro**: Side mounting points allow accessories to be attached to the rack's outer walls.
- **Con (significant)**: The crossbars **have no pre-drilled holes** along their length, unlike Front Runner which includes evenly spaced holes for adjustable accessory mounting. This makes it much harder to attach gear anywhere along a bar without pre-inserting nuts or drilling custom holes.
- **Suggested improvement**: MP 4x4 should add a series of holes along each crossbar — standard practice on competing racks — to allow flexible, tool-free accessory placement.
- **Workaround**: Pre-inserting nuts into the rail channel before closing the assembly, or drilling holes post-install.

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

The MP 4x4 aluminium roof rack is a **well-built, rust-free alternative to Front Runner** at a comparable price point, with superior corrosion resistance and impressive load ratings. However, the **lack of pre-drilled mounting holes along the crossbars** is a meaningful limitation for overlanders who want flexible accessory mounting — a single design change that would make it a near-perfect product. Worth buying if you prioritize build quality and longevity over out-of-the-box accessory compatibility.
