## DIY Dent Removal on a Land Rover Discovery 4 Using a Glue Pull Kit

*A car owner demonstrates how to remove dents at home using a ~€90 Amazon glue pull repair kit, covering tools, technique, and realistic expectations for amateur results.*

---

### 🧰 The Kit & What You Get

- **Cost**: ~**€90** purchased on Amazon — significantly cheaper than a body shop repair
- **Included tools**: Multiple pull tabs (various shapes/sizes), a **glue gun**, a **dent lifter**, a **reverse/pull hammer**, a standard hammer (for pushing metal back in), a **line board with integrated LED light**, and a small bottle (no isopropanol included — must be purchased separately)
- **Glue types**: **Yellow** = general use (wide temperature range); **Clear** = finishing/small dents; **Black** = hot weather use

---

### 🔦 Setup & Preparation

- A **line board** (LED reflector panel) is essential to visually locate and track dents — mount it near the panel to see surface distortions clearly
- **Clean the dent area with isopropanol** before applying glue; skipping this step reduces adhesion and effectiveness
- Choose the **tab shape based on the dent's profile** — smaller tabs work well for tighter or deeper dents

---

### 🔧 Step-by-Step Technique

- **Heat the glue gun**, fill the tab fully, press it firmly over the center of the dent, and wait ~**3 minutes** for the glue to set
- Use the **dent lifter tool** positioned over the tab — pull slowly and steadily; avoid jerking or excessive force
- To remove the tab: use a **plastic pry tool** to create a small gap, spray **isopropanol** underneath, wait ~**30 seconds**, and the tab releases cleanly
- If the metal is pulled **too far out**, use the **hammer gently** to tap it back — but be very careful not to over-strike

---

### ⏱️ Realistic Time & Effort Expectations

- Each dent requires **10–15 repetitions** of the glue-pull cycle — not a quick fix
- The presenter spent roughly **half an hour or more per dent** as a non-professional
- **Sharp edges and thick metal** (e.g., body creases) are the hardest areas — full removal may require interior access with a separate tool
- The process demands **patience**, but the barrier to entry is low for a careful amateur

---

### ✅ Results & Verdict

- After ~**11 pull cycles**, the dent was significantly reduced, though not 100% perfect due to the sharp body edge
- A light **polish of the area** after repairs improves the final appearance
- The presenter judged the result **acceptable for an amateur** and far more affordable than a professional body shop

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

For minor to moderate dents on flat or gently curved panels, a **~€90 glue pull kit** is a genuinely viable DIY alternative to body shop visits, which can cost multiples of that. The key trade-off is **time and patience** — expect repeated pull cycles and imperfect results on sharp body edges. This method is especially worth considering if you have **multiple vehicles with dent damage** (e.g., hail damage), where shop costs would quickly multiply.
