## BMW Transfer Case Oil Change – DIY Guide & Why It Matters

*TLDR: A straightforward walkthrough of changing transfer case (ATC) oil on a BMW, demonstrating why neglecting this service leads to severely degraded fluid.*

---

### 🔧 Job Overview

- **Task**: Drain and refill the transfer case (ATC) oil on a BMW
- **Difficulty**: Easy – estimated **15 to 30 minutes** total
- **Service interval**: Should be replaced every **120,000 km (75,000 miles)**; no service records existed for this vehicle, making the change overdue

---

### 🛒 Parts & Tools Used

- **Oil**: Raven ATC full synthetic oil (purchased from Amazon) – approximately **1.5 liters required**
- **Pump**: A new **1-liter hand pump**, replacing a smaller 200ml pump used previously for differential oil changes – the larger pump makes filling significantly easier
- **Socket**: **8mm hex/Allen key** for drain and fill plugs
- **Copper washers**: Reused after inspection (undamaged); cleaned before reinstalling

---

### 📍 Location & Access

- **Transfer case location**: Driver's side (left side) of the undercarriage
- **No underbody panels need removal** – both plugs are directly accessible
- **Fill plug**: Upper position on the transfer case
- **Drain plug**: Lower position on the transfer case

---

### ⚙️ Torque Specs

- **Fill plug**: **60 Nm**
- **Drain plug**: **22 Nm**

---

### 🔄 Procedure Summary

1. **Remove fill plug first** (critical – ensures you can refill before draining)
2. Remove drain plug and let old oil drain for several minutes
3. Clean copper washers and inspect for damage
4. Reinstall drain plug to **22 Nm**
5. Fill with **1.5 liters** of fresh ATC oil via pump
6. Reinstall fill plug to **60 Nm**
7. Clean any spilled oil with brake disc cleaner

---

### 🔍 Old vs. New Oil Comparison

- **New oil**: Amber/honey-colored, clear, and clean
- **Old oil**: **Visibly black and degraded** – no metal particles on the drain plug, but color alone confirmed severe contamination
- The side-by-side cup comparison made the case for regular fluid changes undeniable

---

### 💡 Bottom Line

Transfer case oil is a commonly overlooked service item, but degraded fluid (as shown here turning jet black) can accelerate wear on the ATC unit – an expensive component to replace. This video proves that even without symptoms, fluid condition can be far worse than expected, making routine changes at the **120,000 km / 75,000 mile** interval essential preventive maintenance.
